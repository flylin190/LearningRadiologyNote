Diastematomyelia

Symptoms:

Diastematomyelia may present with back pain, lower extremity weakness, and bladder or bowel dysfunction. Symptoms can vary depending on the severity of the condition.

Epidemiology:

Diastematomyelia is a rare congenital disorder that is more common in females than males. It is often associated with other spinal cord abnormalities.

Pathology:

Diastematomyelia is characterized by the presence of a longitudinal cleft or clefts in the spinal cord, which can cause tethering of the spinal cord and neurological symptoms.

Treatment:

Treatment for diastematomyelia depends on the severity of symptoms and may include surgery to release tethered spinal cord, relieve pressure on the spinal cord, and stabilize the spine.

Imaging Features:

  • Split spinal cord on MRI
  • Vertebral anomalies may be present

Differential Diagnosis:

  • Spinal cord lipoma
  • Spinal cord syrinx
