Renal Sinus Lipomatosis

Symptoms:

Renal sinus lipomatosis is typically asymptomatic and is often an incidental finding on imaging studies. However, in some cases, it may be associated with flank pain or hematuria.

Epidemiology:

Renal sinus lipomatosis is more common in older individuals and is often associated with obesity and chronic kidney disease.

Pathology:

Renal sinus lipomatosis is characterized by the replacement of renal sinus fat with adipose tissue, leading to enlargement of the renal sinus.

Treatment:

There is no specific treatment for renal sinus lipomatosis. Management is focused on treating any underlying conditions and monitoring for complications.

Imaging Features:

  • Enlargement of the renal sinus on CT or MRI
  • Increased fat density in the renal sinus
  • Compression of the renal pelvis and calyces

Differential Diagnosis:

  • Renal cell carcinoma
  • Renal sinus cyst
