X-linked Adrenoleukodystrophy

Symptoms:

Common symptoms of X-linked adrenoleukodystrophy include progressive neurologic deterioration, adrenal insufficiency, vision and hearing loss, seizures, and difficulty with coordination and gait.

Epidemiology:

X-linked adrenoleukodystrophy is a rare genetic disorder that primarily affects males, with an estimated incidence of 1 in 20,000 to 1 in 50,000 males worldwide.

Pathology:

X-linked adrenoleukodystrophy is caused by mutations in the ABCD1 gene, which leads to the accumulation of very long-chain fatty acids (VLCFAs) in the brain and adrenal glands, resulting in damage to the myelin sheath and adrenal insufficiency.

Treatment:

There is currently no cure for X-linked adrenoleukodystrophy. Treatment focuses on managing symptoms and may include dietary therapy, medication to lower VLCFA levels, and gene therapy in some cases.

Imaging Features:

  • White matter abnormalities on MRI, typically involving the parieto-occipital regions
  • Adrenal atrophy on abdominal imaging

Differential Diagnosis

  • Cerebral X-linked adrenoleukodystrophy-like syndrome
  • Adrenoleukodystrophy with cerebral demyelination
