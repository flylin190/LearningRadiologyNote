Multinodular and Vacuolating Neuronal Tumor

Symptoms:

Multinodular and vacuolating neuronal tumors (MVNTs) can cause a variety of symptoms, including seizures, headaches, and neurological deficits, depending on their location and size.

Epidemiology:

MVNTs are rare, slow-growing tumors that primarily affect young adults and children.

Pathology:

MVNTs are benign tumors composed of vacuolated neuronal cells with a multinodular appearance. They are classified as a subtype of neuronal and mixed neuronal-glial tumors.

Treatment:

The primary treatment for MVNTs is surgical resection, which can be curative if complete removal is achieved. Radiation therapy may be considered for residual or recurrent tumors.

Imaging Features:

  • Well-circumscribed, multinodular lesion
  • Cystic or vacuolated areas
  • Calcifications may be present

Differential Diagnosis

  • Pilocytic astrocytoma
  • Ganglioglioma
  • Dysembryoplastic neuroepithelial tumor
  • Oligodendroglioma
