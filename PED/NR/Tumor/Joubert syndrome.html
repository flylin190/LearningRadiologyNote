Joubert Syndrome

Symptoms:

Joubert syndrome is characterized by a distinctive cerebellar and brainstem malformation that results in neurological features including hypotonia, ataxia, developmental delay, and intellectual disability.

Epidemiology:

Joubert syndrome is a rare genetic disorder that affects approximately 1 in 80,000-100,000 newborns.

Pathology:

Joubert syndrome is caused by mutations in genes that are involved in the development of the cerebellum and brainstem.

Treatment:

There is no specific treatment for Joubert syndrome, but supportive therapies can help manage symptoms and improve quality of life.

Imaging Features:

  • Molar tooth sign on brain MRI, characterized by a deepened interpeduncular fossa, thick and elongated superior cerebellar peduncles, and a deepened fourth ventricle

Differential Diagnosis:

  • Other genetic disorders with cerebellar malformations
  • Cerebellar tumors
  • Cerebellar hypoplasia
