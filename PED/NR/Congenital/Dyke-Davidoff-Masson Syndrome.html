Dyke-Davidoff-Masson Syndrome

Symptoms:

Symptoms of Dyke-Davidoff-Masson syndrome can include seizures, hemiparesis or hemiplegia, facial asymmetry, learning disabilities, and mental retardation.

Epidemiology:

Dyke-Davidoff-Masson syndrome is a rare condition characterized by unilateral cerebral atrophy and skull thickening. It is typically caused by insults to the developing brain during fetal or early childhood stages, such as ischemic or traumatic injuries.

Pathology:

Dyke-Davidoff-Masson syndrome is characterized by unilateral cerebral hemisphere atrophy, with compensatory enlargement of the ipsilateral skull and ventricle. The atrophy results from prenatal or perinatal insults that disrupt normal brain development.

Treatment:

Treatment for Dyke-Davidoff-Masson syndrome is mainly supportive and aimed at managing the associated symptoms and complications, such as seizure control with anticonvulsant medications, physical therapy, and educational interventions for cognitive or learning disabilities.

Imaging Features:

  • Unilateral cerebral hemisphere atrophy
  • Ipsilateral ventricular dilatation
  • Ipsilateral skull thickening and enlargement
  • Potential associated findings (e.g., porencephalic cysts, abnormal gyration)

Differential Diagnosis:

  • Rasmussen's encephalitis
  • Sturge-Weber syndrome
  • Hemimegalencephaly
  • Unilateral ischemic or traumatic brain injury
