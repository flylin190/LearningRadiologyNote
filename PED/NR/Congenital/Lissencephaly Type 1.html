Lissencephaly Type 1

Symptoms:

Lissencephaly type 1, also known as classical lissencephaly or Miller-Dieker syndrome, is characterized by severe neurological impairment, intellectual disability, seizures, and abnormal muscle tone.

Epidemiology:

Lissencephaly type 1 is a rare congenital brain malformation, with an estimated prevalence of 1 in 100,000 births. It can occur in both males and females.

Pathology:

Lissencephaly type 1 is caused by mutations in the LIS1 or DCX genes, which are involved in neuronal migration and brain development. These mutations result in abnormal or absent brain folds (smooth brain), leading to disorganized neuronal layering and malformations.

Treatment:

There is no specific cure for lissencephaly type 1. Treatment is primarily supportive and focuses on managing symptoms such as seizures, muscle tone abnormalities, and developmental delays through medications, physical therapy, and educational interventions.

Imaging Features:

  • Smooth or nearly smooth cerebral surface (agyria or pachygyria)
  • Thickened cortex with abnormal neuronal migration and layering
  • Enlarged ventricles and reduced white matter volume
  • Associated brain malformations may be present

Differential Diagnosis:

  • Other types of lissencephaly (e.g., type 2, subcortical band heterotopia)
  • Polymicrogyria
  • Schizencephaly
  • Severe hypoxic-ischemic injury
