Encephalocele

Symptoms:

Symptoms of encephalocele may include a visible protrusion or swelling on the head, seizures, developmental delays, and neurological deficits depending on the extent and location of the protrusion.

Epidemiology:

Encephaloceles are rare congenital malformations that occur in approximately 1 in 5,000 live births.

Pathology:

Encephalocele is a neural tube defect in which part of the brain and its coverings (meninges) protrude through a defect in the skull.

Treatment:

Treatment typically involves surgical repair to reposition the protruding brain tissue and meninges back into the skull and close the defect. Additional treatments may be required for associated neurological conditions.

Imaging Features:

  • Bony defect in the skull
  • Protrusion of brain tissue and meninges through the defect
  • Associated intracranial abnormalities may be present

Differential Diagnosis

  • Meningocele
  • Cephalocele
  • Dermoid cyst
  • Cystic hygroma
