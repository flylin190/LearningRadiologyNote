Chiari Malformation

Symptoms:

Chiari malformation may be asymptomatic or present with headache, neck pain, dizziness, numbness or tingling in the hands or feet, muscle weakness, and difficulty swallowing.

Epidemiology:

Chiari malformation is a congenital condition that occurs in approximately 1 in 1,000 births.

Pathology:

Chiari malformation is characterized by the downward displacement of the cerebellar tonsils through the foramen magnum, resulting in compression of the brainstem and spinal cord.

Treatment:

Treatment of Chiari malformation depends on the severity of symptoms. Options include observation, pain management, and surgery to decompress the affected area.

Imaging Features:

  • Cerebellar tonsillar herniation below the foramen magnum on MRI
  • Syringomyelia (fluid-filled cavity in the spinal cord) may be present

Differential Diagnosis:

  • Syringobulbia
  • Meningocele
  • Dandy-Walker syndrome
