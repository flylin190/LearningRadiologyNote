Hemimegalencephaly

Symptoms:

Hemimegalencephaly is characterized by unilateral enlargement of one cerebral hemisphere, often associated with intellectual disability, seizures, and contralateral hemiparesis or hemiplegia.

Epidemiology:

Hemimegalencephaly is a rare congenital brain malformation, with an estimated prevalence of 1 in 86,000 to 1 in 200,000 births. It affects both males and females equally.

Pathology:

Hemimegalencephaly is caused by abnormal proliferation and migration of neurons during early brain development, leading to overgrowth and dysplasia of one cerebral hemisphere. The underlying cause is often unknown, but it may be associated with genetic disorders or prenatal insults.

Treatment:

Treatment for hemimegalencephaly is primarily aimed at managing seizures and associated neurological deficits. Surgical resection of the affected hemisphere (hemispherectomy) may be considered in severe cases refractory to medical management.

Imaging Features:

  • Unilateral enlargement of one cerebral hemisphere
  • Thickened cortex and abnormal gyral pattern in the affected hemisphere
  • Ipsilateral ventricular enlargement and midline shift
  • Associated abnormalities (e.g., polymicrogyria, heterotopia) may be present

Differential Diagnosis:

  • Unilateral megalencephaly
  • Congenital brain tumors
  • Sturge-Weber syndrome
  • Rasmussen's encephalitis
