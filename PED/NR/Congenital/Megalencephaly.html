Megalencephaly

Symptoms:

Megalencephaly is characterized by an abnormally large head circumference and an enlarged brain. Symptoms may include developmental delays, intellectual disability, seizures, and neurological deficits, depending on the underlying cause.

Epidemiology:

Megalencephaly is a rare condition, with an estimated prevalence of 1 in 20,000 to 1 in 30,000 births. It can occur as an isolated finding or in association with various genetic conditions or metabolic disorders.

Pathology:

Megalencephaly can be caused by various underlying conditions, including genetic disorders (e.g., PI3K-related overgrowth disorders, mutations in the PTEN gene), metabolic disorders (e.g., glutaric aciduria type 1), or developmental abnormalities affecting brain growth and development.

Treatment:

Treatment for megalencephaly is largely supportive and depends on the underlying cause and associated symptoms. Management may involve anti-seizure medications, physical and occupational therapy, and educational interventions.

Imaging Features:

  • Enlargement of the overall brain size with increased head circumference
  • Abnormal ventricular system and cortical mantle thickness
  • Associated findings may vary depending on the underlying cause

Differential Diagnosis:

  • Hydrocephalus
  • Craniosynostosis
  • Intracranial mass lesions
  • Metabolic disorders with brain involvement
