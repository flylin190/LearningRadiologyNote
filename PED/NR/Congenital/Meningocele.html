Meningocele

Symptoms:

Meningoceles are often asymptomatic, but some may cause discomfort, headaches, or neurological deficits depending on the location and size of the protrusion.

Epidemiology:

Meningoceles are rare congenital malformations that occur in approximately 1 in 5,000 live births.

Pathology:

Meningocele is a neural tube defect in which the meninges (membranes covering the brain and spinal cord) protrude through a defect in the skull or vertebral column, but without the presence of brain tissue.

Treatment:

Treatment typically involves surgical repair to reposition the protruding meninges back into the skull or vertebral column and close the defect.

Imaging Features:

  • Bony defect in the skull or vertebral column
  • Protrusion of meninges through the defect
  • No brain tissue present in the protrusion

Differential Diagnosis

  • Encephalocele
  • Cephalocele
  • Dermoid cyst
  • Cystic hygroma
