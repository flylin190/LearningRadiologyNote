Dandy-Walker Malformation

Symptoms:

Symptoms may include developmental delays, increased head circumference, bulging at the back of the head, abnormal breathing patterns, and seizures.

Epidemiology:

Dandy-Walker malformation is a rare congenital malformation, occurring in approximately 1 in 25,000 to 35,000 live births.

Pathology:

Dandy-Walker malformation is a congenital brain malformation characterized by hypoplasia or aplasia of the cerebellar vermis, cystic dilation of the fourth ventricle, and enlargement of the posterior fossa.

Treatment:

Treatment is primarily supportive, focusing on managing associated complications and symptoms. In some cases, surgical intervention may be required to treat hydrocephalus or relieve pressure on the brain.

Imaging Features:

  • Hypoplasia or aplasia of the cerebellar vermis
  • Cystic dilation of the fourth ventricle
  • Enlarged posterior fossa
  • Hydrocephalus (in some cases)

Differential Diagnosis

  • Arachnoid cyst
  • Posterior fossa tumor
  • Chiari malformation
  • Mega cisterna magna
