Cleidocranial Dysostosis

Symptoms:

Cleidocranial dysostosis is characterized by delayed closure of the fontanelles, hypoplastic or aplastic clavicles, dental abnormalities (such as delayed eruption of permanent teeth and supernumerary teeth), and short stature.

Epidemiology:

Cleidocranial dysostosis is a rare genetic disorder that occurs in approximately 1 in 1,000,000 individuals. It affects males and females equally.

Pathology:

Cleidocranial dysostosis is caused by mutations in the RUNX2 gene, which is involved in the development of bones and teeth.

Treatment:

Treatment for cleidocranial dysostosis depends on the specific symptoms present but may include surgery to correct skeletal abnormalities and dental treatments to address dental abnormalities.

Imaging Features:

  • Delayed closure of fontanelles on skull X-ray
  • Hypoplastic or aplastic clavicles on chest X-ray
  • Supernumerary teeth on dental X-ray

Differential Diagnosis:

  • Cleidocranial dysplasia
  • Cherubism
  • Caffey disease
