Chondroblastoma

Symptoms:

Chondroblastoma typically presents with localized pain and swelling in the affected bone. It commonly affects the long bones of the extremities, such as the femur, tibia, and humerus.

Epidemiology:

Chondroblastoma is a rare benign bone tumor that usually occurs in children and young adults. It accounts for less than 1% of all bone tumors.

Pathology:

Chondroblastoma is characterized by the presence of chondroblasts, which are immature cartilage-forming cells. The exact cause of chondroblastoma is unknown.

Treatment:

Treatment for chondroblastoma usually involves surgery to remove the tumor. In some cases, adjuvant therapy such as cryotherapy or curettage may be used to ensure complete removal of the tumor.

Imaging Features:

  • Well-defined lytic lesion with sclerotic margin on x-ray
  • Endosteal scalloping
  • Occasional calcification within the lesion

Differential Diagnosis:

  • Chondrosarcoma
  • Fibrous dysplasia
  • Unicameral bone cyst
