Osteogenesis Imperfecta

Symptoms:

Osteogenesis imperfecta is characterized by fragile bones that break easily, often with minimal or no apparent trauma. Other symptoms may include short stature, blue sclerae, hearing loss, and dental problems.

Epidemiology:

Osteogenesis imperfecta is a genetic disorder that affects approximately 1 in 10,000 to 20,000 individuals worldwide. It can occur in people of all ethnicities and both sexes.

Pathology:

Osteogenesis imperfecta is caused by mutations in the COL1A1 or COL1A2 genes, which are involved in the production of type I collagen, the main protein in bone.

Treatment:

Treatment for osteogenesis imperfecta focuses on managing symptoms and preventing fractures. This may include physical therapy, bracing, surgery to correct bone deformities, and medications to improve bone density.

Imaging Features:

  • Fragile, thin bones with multiple fractures on x-ray
  • Bowing of long bones
  • Wormian bones (extra bones in the skull)

Differential Diagnosis:

  • Child abuse (non-accidental injury)
  • Other genetic bone disorders, such as osteopetrosis
