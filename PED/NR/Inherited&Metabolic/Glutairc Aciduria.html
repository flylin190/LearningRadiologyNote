Glutaric Aciduria

Symptoms:

Glutaric aciduria is a metabolic disorder that can cause neurological symptoms such as macrocephaly, hypotonia, and developmental delay. It can also lead to acute encephalopathy triggered by a febrile illness or other stressors.

Epidemiology:

Glutaric aciduria is a rare autosomal recessive disorder that usually presents in infancy or early childhood.

Pathology:

Glutaric aciduria is caused by a deficiency of the enzyme glutaryl-CoA dehydrogenase, leading to the accumulation of glutaric acid and other metabolites in the body.

Treatment:

Treatment for glutaric aciduria involves a low-lysine diet and carnitine supplementation to help remove excess glutaric acid.

Imaging Features:

  • Characteristic changes in the basal ganglia, including widening of the Sylvian fissures and frontotemporal atrophy
  • Subdural hemorrhage or effusion
  • Enlarged subarachnoid spaces

Differential Diagnosis:

  • Infantile bilateral striatal necrosis
  • Methylmalonic acidemia
  • Propionic acidemia
