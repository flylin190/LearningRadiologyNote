Kernicterus (Chronic bilirubin encephalophy)

Symptoms:

Kernicterus can cause symptoms such as lethargy, poor feeding, jaundice, and in severe cases, seizures and developmental delay.

Epidemiology:

Kernicterus is a rare condition that occurs when high levels of bilirubin in the blood cross the blood-brain barrier and cause brain damage.

Pathology:

High levels of unconjugated bilirubin can lead to bilirubin deposition in the basal ganglia, brainstem, and cerebellum, resulting in neuronal injury and death.

Treatment:

Treatment for kernicterus focuses on reducing bilirubin levels in the blood through phototherapy, exchange transfusion, and supportive care.

Imaging Features:

  • MRI may show bilirubin deposition in the basal ganglia and brainstem

Differential Diagnosis

  • Hypoxic-ischemic encephalopathy
  • Other metabolic encephalopathies
