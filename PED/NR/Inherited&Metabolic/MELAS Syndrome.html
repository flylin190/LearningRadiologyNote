MELAS Syndrome

Symptoms:

MELAS syndrome is characterized by a combination of stroke-like episodes, seizures, and muscle weakness. Other symptoms may include headaches, vomiting, and vision and hearing loss.

Epidemiology:

MELAS syndrome is a rare condition that typically begins in childhood or early adulthood. It is caused by mutations in mitochondrial DNA.

Pathology:

MELAS syndrome is caused by mutations in mitochondrial DNA, which leads to impaired energy production in cells, particularly in the brain, muscles, and nerves.

Treatment:

Treatment for MELAS syndrome is mainly supportive and aims to manage symptoms. This may include medications to control seizures and prevent stroke-like episodes, as well as physical therapy to improve muscle function.

Imaging Features:

  • Stroke-like lesions in the brain on imaging
  • Diffusion-weighted imaging may show restricted diffusion in affected areas
  • Cerebral atrophy in advanced cases

Differential Diagnosis:

  • Other mitochondrial disorders
  • Stroke due to other causes
  • Metabolic disorders
