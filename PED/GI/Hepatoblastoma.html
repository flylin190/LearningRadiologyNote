Hepatoblastoma

Symptoms:

Common symptoms of hepatoblastoma include an abdominal mass or swelling, abdominal pain, loss of appetite, weight loss, jaundice, and vomiting.

Epidemiology:

Hepatoblastoma is a rare liver cancer that primarily affects children, typically younger than 3 years of age. It accounts for approximately 1% of all pediatric cancers.

Pathology:

Hepatoblastoma is a malignant embryonal tumor that arises from the immature liver precursor cells, known as hepatoblasts. It can be classified into different histological subtypes, including epithelial, mixed epithelial and mesenchymal, and undifferentiated subtypes.

Treatment:

Treatment for hepatoblastoma typically involves a combination of chemotherapy, surgical resection (if possible), and, in some cases, liver transplantation. The specific treatment plan depends on the stage of the tumor, its size, and the patient's overall health.

Imaging Features:

  • Well-defined solid mass or masses within the liver
  • Heterogeneous enhancement on contrast-enhanced imaging
  • Calcifications within the tumor may be present
  • Vascular invasion or metastases may be seen in advanced stages

Differential Diagnosis:

  • Hepatocellular carcinoma
  • Hepatic adenoma
  • Metastatic lesions
  • Benign liver tumors (e.g., hemangioma, focal nodular hyperplasia)
