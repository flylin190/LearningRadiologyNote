Choledochal Cyst

Symptoms:

Choledochal cysts may present with abdominal pain, jaundice, and fever. In some cases, they may be asymptomatic and discovered incidentally on imaging.

Epidemiology:

Choledochal cysts are more common in females and are often diagnosed in infancy or early childhood.

Pathology:

Choledochal cysts are congenital bile duct abnormalities that result in cystic dilatation of the biliary tree. The exact cause is unknown, but genetic and environmental factors may play a role.

Treatment:

The main treatment for choledochal cysts is surgical removal of the cyst and reconstruction of the biliary tract.

Imaging Features:

  • Cystic dilatation of the common bile duct on ultrasound or CT scan
  • Communication between the cyst and the bile duct on MR cholangiopancreatography (MRCP)

Differential Diagnosis

  • Biliary atresia
  • Choledocholithiasis
  • Caroli disease
