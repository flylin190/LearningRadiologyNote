Gaucher Disease

Symptoms:

Common symptoms of Gaucher disease include abdominal pain, fatigue, easy bruising, and bone pain. In severe cases, it can lead to neurological complications, such as seizures and cognitive impairment.

Epidemiology:

Gaucher disease is a rare inherited disorder, with an estimated prevalence of 1 in 50,000 to 100,000 individuals worldwide. It is more common in certain ethnic groups, such as Ashkenazi Jews.

Pathology:

Gaucher disease is caused by a deficiency of the enzyme glucocerebrosidase, which leads to the accumulation of a fatty substance called glucocerebroside in various organs, particularly the spleen, liver, and bone marrow.

Treatment:

Treatment for Gaucher disease involves enzyme replacement therapy (ERT) or substrate reduction therapy (SRT). Supportive care, such as blood transfusions and pain management, may also be necessary.

Imaging Features:

  • Hepatosplenomegaly
  • Bone marrow involvement (Erlenmeyer flask deformity)
  • Lytic bone lesions

Differential Diagnosis:

  • Other lysosomal storage disorders
  • Hematological malignancies
  • Chronic liver disease
