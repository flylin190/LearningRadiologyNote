Meckel's Diverticulum

Symptoms:

Meckel's diverticulum may be asymptomatic, but it can cause symptoms such as abdominal pain, bloating, rectal bleeding, and intestinal obstruction.

Epidemiology:

Meckel's diverticulum is a congenital anomaly that occurs in about 2% of the population. It is more common in males than females.

Pathology:

Meckel's diverticulum is a remnant of the omphalomesenteric duct, which normally disappears during embryonic development. It is located on the antimesenteric border of the ileum, usually within 2 feet of the ileocecal valve.

Treatment:

Treatment for symptomatic Meckel's diverticulum usually involves surgical removal (diverticulectomy).

Imaging Features:

  • Meckel's diverticulum may be visualized on imaging studies such as CT scan or ultrasound.

Differential Diagnosis:

  • Appendicitis
  • Small bowel obstruction
  • Crohn's disease
