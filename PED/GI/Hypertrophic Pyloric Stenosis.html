Hypertrophic Pyloric Stenosis

Symptoms:

Common symptoms include projectile vomiting, constant hunger, weight loss, and visible gastric peristaltic waves in the abdomen.

Epidemiology:

Hypertrophic pyloric stenosis is a relatively common condition, affecting about 3 in 1,000 live births. It is more common in males and firstborn infants.

Pathology:

Hypertrophic pyloric stenosis is characterized by hypertrophy and thickening of the pyloric muscle, leading to gastric outlet obstruction.

Treatment:

The definitive treatment is surgical correction through a pyloromyotomy, which involves incising the hypertrophied pyloric muscle to relieve the obstruction.

Imaging Features:

  • Thickened pyloric muscle ("pyloric olive")
  • Elongated and distended stomach
  • Minimal or no gas in the small bowel

Differential Diagnosis

  • Gastroesophageal reflux disease (GERD)
  • Intestinal malrotation
  • Duodenal atresia
  • Annular pancreas
