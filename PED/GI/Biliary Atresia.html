Biliary Atresia

Symptoms:

Biliary atresia typically presents in infants with jaundice that persists beyond the first two weeks of life, dark urine, pale stools, and hepatomegaly.

Epidemiology:

Biliary atresia is a rare disease that affects infants, with the majority of cases occurring in the first few months of life. The exact cause is unknown, but it is thought to involve a combination of genetic and environmental factors.

Pathology:

Biliary atresia is characterized by the progressive destruction or absence of the bile ducts inside and outside the liver, leading to bile flow obstruction and subsequent liver damage.

Treatment:

The main treatment for biliary atresia is surgery to restore bile flow. The Kasai procedure (hepatoportoenterostomy) is performed to create a new pathway for bile drainage.

Imaging Features:

  • Absence or discontinuity of the extrahepatic bile ducts on ultrasound
  • Non-visualization of the gallbladder on nuclear imaging

Differential Diagnosis

  • Choledochal cysts
  • Alagille syndrome
  • Neonatal hepatitis
