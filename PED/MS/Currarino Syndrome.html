Currarino Syndrome

Symptoms:

Currarino syndrome is characterized by a triad of sacral anomalies, anorectal malformations, and presacral mass. Patients may present with chronic constipation, urinary retention, or neurological symptoms due to the presacral mass.

Epidemiology:

Currarino syndrome is a rare congenital disorder that typically presents in childhood. It is more common in females than males.

Treatment:

The treatment of Currarino syndrome often involves a multidisciplinary approach, including surgery to correct anorectal malformations and remove the presacral mass. The specific treatment plan depends on the individual's symptoms and the severity of the condition.

Imaging Features:

  • Sacral anomalies, such as sacral agenesis or dysplasia
  • Anorectal malformations, such as imperforate anus or anteriorly placed anus
  • Presacral mass, such as anterior meningocele or teratoma

Differential Diagnosis

  • Hirschsprung disease
  • Congenital sacrococcygeal teratoma
  • Sacral agenesis
