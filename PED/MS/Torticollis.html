Torticollis

Symptoms:

The main symptom of torticollis is an abnormal head position, with the head tilted toward one shoulder and the chin rotated toward the opposite shoulder.

Epidemiology:

Torticollis can occur at any age, but it is most commonly seen in infants (congenital muscular torticollis) or adults (acquired torticollis).

Pathology:

Torticollis can be caused by various factors, including congenital abnormalities, muscular disorders, trauma, or central nervous system disorders. In congenital muscular torticollis, the sternocleidomastoid muscle is shortened or fibrotic.

Treatment:

Treatment for torticollis depends on the underlying cause and severity. It may involve physical therapy, stretching exercises, botulinum toxin injections, or surgical release of the affected muscle(s).

Imaging Features:

  • Thickening or shortening of the sternocleidomastoid muscle (in congenital muscular torticollis)
  • Displacement of midline structures (e.g., trachea, esophagus)
  • Vertebral or soft tissue abnormalities (in acquired torticollis)

Differential Diagnosis:

  • Congenital muscular torticollis
  • Acquired torticollis (e.g., trauma, inflammation, neoplasm)
  • Cervical dystonia
  • Klippel-Feil syndrome
