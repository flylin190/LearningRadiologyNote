Ewing Sarcoma

Symptoms:

Ewing sarcoma often presents with pain, swelling, or a lump in the affected area. Other symptoms may include fever, fatigue, and weight loss.

Epidemiology:

Ewing sarcoma is a rare type of cancer that primarily affects children and young adults. It most commonly occurs in the bones, but can also affect soft tissues.

Pathology:

Ewing sarcoma is characterized by the presence of small round blue cells. It is a type of primitive neuroectodermal tumor (PNET) and is associated with chromosomal translocations involving the EWSR1 gene.

Treatment:

Treatment for Ewing sarcoma typically involves a combination of surgery, chemotherapy, and radiation therapy. Targeted therapies and immunotherapy may also be used in some cases.

Imaging Features:

  • Aggressive lytic bone lesion on X-ray
  • Soft tissue mass with aggressive features on MRI

Differential Diagnosis:

  • Osteosarcoma
  • Chondrosarcoma
  • Lymphoma
