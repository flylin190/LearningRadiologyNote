Salter-Harris Fracture

Symptoms:

The symptoms of a Salter-Harris fracture vary depending on the severity and location of the fracture. Common symptoms include pain, swelling, bruising, and difficulty using the affected limb.

Epidemiology:

Salter-Harris fractures are most common in children and adolescents, especially those involved in sports or activities that put stress on the bones.

Pathology:

A Salter-Harris fracture is a type of fracture that involves the growth plate (physis) of a bone. It is classified into five types (I-V) based on the extent of the fracture through the growth plate and the surrounding bone.

Treatment:

The treatment for a Salter-Harris fracture depends on the type and severity of the fracture but may include immobilization with a cast or splint, closed reduction (manipulation of the fracture without surgery), or open reduction (surgery to realign the fracture).

Imaging Features:

  • Fracture line extending through the growth plate on X-ray
  • Varies depending on the Salter-Harris type

Differential Diagnosis:

  • Other types of bone fractures (e.g., transverse, oblique, comminuted)
  • Growth plate injuries without fracture
  • Soft tissue injuries (e.g., sprains, strains)
