Kohler Disease

Symptoms:

Kohler disease symptoms include gait abnormalities, tarsal pain, and foot swelling.

Epidemiology:

Kohler disease typically affects children, especially boys. It usually only affects one foot.

Pathology:

Kohler disease is avascular necrosis of the navicular bone, possibly due to insufficient blood supply.

Treatment:

Kohler disease usually does not require treatment as it resolves on its own as the child grows. In some cases, supportive shoe inserts or shoes may be needed.

Imaging Features:

  • X-ray may show abnormal shape and density of the navicular bone

Differential Diagnosis

  • Avascular necrosis of the navicular bone
  • Navicular fracture
  • Navicular osteomyelitis
