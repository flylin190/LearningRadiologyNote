Croup

Symptoms:

Common symptoms of croup include a barking cough, hoarseness, stridor (high-pitched breathing sound), and respiratory distress.

Epidemiology:

Croup is a common respiratory illness that primarily affects children between 6 months and 3 years of age. It is more prevalent during the fall and winter months.

Pathology:

Croup is caused by viral infections, most commonly parainfluenza virus, that lead to inflammation and narrowing of the upper airways, particularly the subglottic region.

Treatment:

Mild cases of croup can be managed with hydration, humidified air, and over-the-counter medications for symptom relief. Severe cases may require corticosteroids, nebulized epinephrine, or hospitalization for airway management.

Imaging Features:

  • Subglottic narrowing (steeple sign) on lateral neck radiographs
  • Overinflation of the lungs

Differential Diagnosis:

  • Epiglottitis
  • Foreign body aspiration
  • Bacterial tracheitis
  • Retropharyngeal abscess
