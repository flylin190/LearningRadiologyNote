Congenital Pulmonary Airway Malformation (CPAM)

Symptoms:

CPAM may be asymptomatic or present with respiratory distress, recurrent respiratory infections, or a palpable mass in the chest.

Epidemiology:

CPAM is a rare congenital lung abnormality that occurs in approximately 1 in 10,000 to 1 in 35,000 live births.

Pathology:

CPAM is characterized by the presence of cystic lesions in the lungs that result from abnormal branching of the airways during fetal development.

Treatment:

The treatment of CPAM depends on the size and location of the lesion. Options include observation, surgery, or, in some cases, lung transplantation.

Imaging Features:

  • Cystic lesions in the lungs on chest X-ray or CT scan

Differential Diagnosis:

  • Pulmonary sequestration
  • Bronchogenic cyst
  • Congenital lobar emphysema
