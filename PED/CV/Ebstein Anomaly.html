Ebstein Anomaly

Symptoms:

Ebstein anomaly may be asymptomatic or present with cyanosis (bluish discoloration of the skin), shortness of breath, fatigue, and heart palpitations.

Epidemiology:

Ebstein anomaly is a rare congenital heart defect that occurs in approximately 1 in 20,000 live births.

Pathology:

Ebstein anomaly is characterized by the downward displacement of the tricuspid valve into the right ventricle, leading to atrialization of a portion of the right ventricle and enlargement of the right atrium.

Treatment:

Treatment of Ebstein anomaly depends on the severity of symptoms and the degree of tricuspid regurgitation. Options include medication, surgery to repair or replace the tricuspid valve, and in severe cases, heart transplantation.

Imaging Features:

  • Enlarged right atrium and right ventricle on chest X-ray or echocardiogram
  • Downward displacement of the tricuspid valve on echocardiogram

Differential Diagnosis:

  • Pulmonary atresia
  • Tricuspid atresia
  • Tetralogy of Fallot
