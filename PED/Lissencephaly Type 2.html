Lissencephaly Type 2

Symptoms:

Lissencephaly type 2, also known as cobblestone lissencephaly or Walker-Warburg syndrome, is characterized by severe neurological impairment, intellectual disability, seizures, and congenital muscular dystrophy.

Epidemiology:

Lissencephaly type 2 is an extremely rare congenital brain malformation, with an estimated prevalence of less than 1 in 100,000 births. It affects both males and females.

Pathology:

Lissencephaly type 2 is caused by mutations in various genes involved in the formation and function of the basement membrane surrounding the brain and muscles. These mutations lead to abnormal neuronal migration, brain malformations, and congenital muscular dystrophy.

Treatment:

There is no specific cure for lissencephaly type 2. Treatment is supportive and focuses on managing symptoms such as seizures, respiratory issues, feeding difficulties, and developmental delays through medications, physical therapy, and specialized care.

Imaging Features:

  • Smooth or nearly smooth cerebral surface (agyria or pachygyria)
  • Cobblestone-like cortical malformation with dysplastic and disorganized neurons
  • Cerebellar and brainstem abnormalities
  • Associated malformations (e.g., hydrocephalus, encephalocele, eye abnormalities)

Differential Diagnosis:

  • Other types of lissencephaly (e.g., type 1, subcortical band heterotopia)
  • Congenital muscular dystrophies without brain malformations
  • Other congenital brain malformations
