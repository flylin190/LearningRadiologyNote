Epiglottitis

Symptoms:

The main symptoms of epiglottitis include severe sore throat, drooling, difficulty swallowing, and respiratory distress due to airway obstruction.

Epidemiology:

Epiglottitis is a medical emergency that can occur at any age, but it is more common in children before the widespread use of the Haemophilus influenzae type B (Hib) vaccine.

Pathology:

Epiglottitis is an acute inflammation of the epiglottis, the cartilage structure that covers the entrance to the larynx. It is typically caused by bacterial infections, such as Haemophilus influenzae or Streptococcus pneumoniae.

Treatment:

Treatment for epiglottitis involves immediate airway management, which may require intubation or tracheostomy, and administration of intravenous antibiotics and corticosteroids.

Imaging Features:

  • Swollen and edematous epiglottis (the "thumb sign")
  • Potential airway narrowing or obstruction
  • Thickening of the aryepiglottic folds

Differential Diagnosis:

  • Acute epiglottitis
  • Peritonsillar abscess
  • Retropharyngeal abscess
  • Laryngeal tumor
