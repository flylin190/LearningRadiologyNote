Fibroadenoma

Symptoms:

Fibroadenomas are usually painless, smooth, rubbery, or firm lumps in the breast that are easily movable. They may change in size with hormonal changes, such as during menstruation.

Epidemiology:

Fibroadenomas are common in women under the age of 30, but they can occur at any age. They are more common in women who are premenopausal or taking hormone therapy.

Pathology:

Fibroadenomas are benign breast tumors composed of glandular and stromal tissue. They are typically well-defined and encapsulated.

Treatment:

Most fibroadenomas do not require treatment unless they are causing symptoms or are of concern. In such cases, they may be surgically removed.

Imaging Features:

  • Well-defined, round or oval mass with smooth margins on ultrasound
  • May show posterior acoustic enhancement
  • Typically no associated calcifications

Differential Diagnosis:

  • Benign breast cyst
  • Phyllodes tumor
  • Complex fibroadenoma
  • Breast cancer
