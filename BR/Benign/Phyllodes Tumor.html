Phyllodes Tumor

Symptoms:

Phyllodes tumors of the breast may present as a painless breast lump or mass. Rarely, they may cause breast pain, nipple discharge, or skin changes.

Epidemiology:

Phyllodes tumors are rare, accounting for less than 1% of all breast tumors. They typically occur in women between the ages of 30 and 70, but can occur at any age.

Pathology:

Phyllodes tumors are characterized by a leaf-like growth pattern on histology. They are classified as benign, borderline, or malignant based on their histological features.

Treatment:

Treatment for phyllodes tumors usually involves surgical removal of the tumor. In some cases, additional treatment such as radiation therapy or chemotherapy may be recommended, especially for malignant tumors.

Imaging Features:

  • Well-defined mass with smooth margins on mammography
  • Heterogeneous hypoechoic mass on ultrasound
  • Enhancing mass with internal septations on MRI

Differential Diagnosis:

  • Fibroadenoma
  • Phyllodes tumor mimicking fibroadenoma
  • Breast carcinoma
