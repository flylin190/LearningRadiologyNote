Galactocele

Symptoms:

Galactoceles are typically painless and may present as a smooth, firm, or cystic mass in the breast. They are most commonly found in women who are breastfeeding.

Epidemiology:

Galactoceles are more common in women who are breastfeeding or have recently stopped breastfeeding. They are caused by the accumulation of milk in the breast ducts.

Pathology:

Galactoceles are cystic lesions filled with milk. They are typically surrounded by a fibrous capsule and are not associated with an increased risk of breast cancer.

Treatment:

Most galactoceles do not require treatment unless they are causing symptoms. In such cases, the cyst may be drained using a fine needle aspiration.

Imaging Features:

  • Well-defined, round or oval mass with smooth margins on ultrasound
  • May contain milk with variable echogenicity
  • Typically no associated calcifications

Differential Diagnosis:

  • Simple breast cyst
  • Fibroadenoma
  • Other benign breast lesions
