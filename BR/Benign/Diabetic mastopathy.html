Diabetic Mastopathy

Symptoms:

Diabetic mastopathy typically presents as a painless, firm breast mass or lump. It is usually unilateral and may be associated with skin thickening or retraction.

Epidemiology:

Diabetic mastopathy is a relatively uncommon condition, occurring in approximately 0.6-3% of women with diabetes. It is more common in premenopausal women with long-standing, poorly controlled diabetes.

Pathology:

Diabetic mastopathy is a fibrous breast disease characterized by excessive deposition of extracellular matrix proteins, such as collagen and glycoproteins, in the breast tissue.

Treatment:

Treatment typically involves close monitoring and follow-up, as diabetic mastopathy is benign and does not require surgical intervention unless it causes significant discomfort or concern.

Imaging Features:

  • Well-defined, irregular or spiculated mass on mammogram or ultrasound
  • Increased density or architectural distortion
  • May mimic breast cancer on imaging

Differential Diagnosis:

  • Breast cancer
  • Fibroadenoma
  • Radial scar
