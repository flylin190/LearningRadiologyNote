Benign Breast Cyst

Symptoms:

Benign breast cysts may present as a smooth, firm, or rubbery lump in the breast that is movable and may change in size with the menstrual cycle. They are usually painless but may cause discomfort.

Epidemiology:

Benign breast cysts are common in women, especially those between the ages of 30 and 50. They may be more common in women who are premenopausal or taking hormone therapy.

Pathology:

Benign breast cysts are fluid-filled sacs that form in the breast tissue. They are typically surrounded by a thin, fibrous capsule and are not associated with an increased risk of breast cancer.

Treatment:

Most benign breast cysts do not require treatment unless they are causing symptoms. In such cases, the cyst may be drained using a fine needle aspiration.

Imaging Features:

  • Well-defined, round or oval mass with smooth margins on ultrasound
  • May contain anechoic fluid with posterior acoustic enhancement
  • Typically no associated calcifications

Differential Diagnosis:

  • Fibroadenoma
  • Complex cyst
  • Phyllodes tumor
  • Other benign breast lesions
