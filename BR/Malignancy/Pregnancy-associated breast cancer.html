Pregnancy-associated Breast Cancer

Symptoms:

Pregnancy-associated breast cancer may present with a lump or mass in the breast, changes in the size, shape, or appearance of the breast, nipple discharge, or skin changes on the breast.

Epidemiology:

Pregnancy-associated breast cancer is rare, accounting for less than 5% of all breast cancer cases. It can occur during pregnancy or up to one year postpartum.

Pathology:

The pathology of pregnancy-associated breast cancer is similar to other types of breast cancer, with the presence of abnormal cells that form a tumor in the breast tissue.

Treatment:

Treatment for pregnancy-associated breast cancer depends on the stage of the cancer but may include surgery, chemotherapy, radiation therapy, hormone therapy, or a combination of these treatments.

Imaging Features:

  • Mass or asymmetry on mammography
  • Increased vascularity on Doppler ultrasound
  • Enhancing mass on MRI

Differential Diagnosis:

  • Fibroadenoma
  • Galactocele
  • Benign breast cyst
