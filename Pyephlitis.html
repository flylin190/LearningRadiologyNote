Pylephlelitis

Symptoms:

Pylephlelitis may present with fever, abdominal pain, jaundice, and septicemia. It is often associated with infections of the portal vein system.

Epidemiology:

Pylephlelitis is a rare condition that most commonly occurs as a complication of intra-abdominal infections, such as appendicitis or diverticulitis.

Pathology:

Pylephlelitis is characterized by inflammation of the portal vein and its branches, usually secondary to an intra-abdominal infection.

Treatment:

Treatment for pylephlelitis includes antibiotics to treat the underlying infection, as well as supportive care to manage symptoms.

Imaging Features:

  • Portal vein thrombosis on CT scan
  • Enlarged liver on imaging

Differential Diagnosis:

  • Portal vein thrombosis
  • Acute cholecystitis
