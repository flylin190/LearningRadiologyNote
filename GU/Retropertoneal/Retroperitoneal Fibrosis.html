Retroperitoneal Fibrosis

Symptoms:

Retroperitoneal fibrosis can cause nonspecific symptoms such as abdominal or flank pain, weight loss, and fatigue. In severe cases, it can lead to obstruction of the ureters, causing renal failure.

Epidemiology:

Retroperitoneal fibrosis is a rare disease that most commonly affects men aged 40-60 years.

Pathology:

Retroperitoneal fibrosis is characterized by the development of fibrous tissue in the retroperitoneal space, which can lead to compression and obstruction of the ureters.

Treatment:

Treatment for retroperitoneal fibrosis involves corticosteroids to reduce inflammation and surgery or stenting to relieve ureteral obstruction.

Imaging Features:

  • Soft tissue mass surrounding the abdominal aorta and iliac arteries on CT scan
  • Ureteral obstruction or displacement
  • Hydronephrosis

Differential Diagnosis:

  • Primary retroperitoneal neoplasms
  • Retroperitoneal sarcomas
  • Retroperitoneal lymphadenopathy
