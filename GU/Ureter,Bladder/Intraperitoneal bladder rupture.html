Intraperitoneal Bladder Rupture

Symptoms:

Symptoms of intraperitoneal bladder rupture include severe abdominal pain, distension, tenderness, and difficulty urinating.

Epidemiology:

Intraperitoneal bladder rupture is most commonly caused by blunt abdominal trauma and is more common in males than females.

Pathology:

Intraperitoneal bladder rupture is characterized by a tear in the bladder wall, leading to leakage of urine into the peritoneal cavity.

Treatment:

Treatment for intraperitoneal bladder rupture involves surgical repair of the bladder wall.

Imaging Features:

  • Free intraperitoneal fluid on CT scan
  • Bladder wall discontinuity on CT cystogram

Differential Diagnosis:

  • Bladder injury
  • Perforated viscus
  • Ruptured ovarian cyst
  • Appendicitis with perforation
