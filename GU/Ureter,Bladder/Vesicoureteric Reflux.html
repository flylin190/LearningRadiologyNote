Vesicoureteric Reflux

Symptoms:

Vesicoureteric reflux (VUR) is a condition where urine flows backward from the bladder into the ureters or kidneys. It is often asymptomatic but can lead to urinary tract infections, kidney damage, and other complications.

Epidemiology:

VUR is relatively common in children, especially infants and young children. It is more common in girls than boys.

Pathology:

VUR is typically caused by a defect in the valve-like mechanism where the ureters enter the bladder, allowing urine to flow backward. It can be primary (congenital) or secondary (acquired).

Treatment:

Treatment for VUR depends on the severity and symptoms. It may include medications to prevent urinary tract infections, surgery to correct the defect, or watchful waiting in mild cases.

Imaging Features:

  • Dilated ureters and/or renal pelvis on ultrasound
  • Reflux of contrast into the ureters or kidneys on voiding cystourethrogram (VCUG)

Differential Diagnosis

  • Ureteropelvic junction obstruction
  • Bladder outlet obstruction
  • Prune belly syndrome
