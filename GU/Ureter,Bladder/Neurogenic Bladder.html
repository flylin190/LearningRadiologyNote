Neurogenic Bladder

Symptoms:

Neurogenic bladder is characterized by dysfunction of the urinary bladder due to a neurologic condition, such as spinal cord injury, multiple sclerosis, or stroke. Symptoms may include urinary incontinence, urinary retention, and frequent urinary tract infections.

Epidemiology:

Neurogenic bladder can occur at any age but is more common in older adults and individuals with neurologic conditions.

Pathology:

Neurogenic bladder is caused by damage to the nerves that control bladder function, leading to abnormal bladder contractions or inability to empty the bladder completely.

Treatment:

Treatment for neurogenic bladder depends on the underlying cause and may include medications, catheterization, and surgery to repair nerve damage or improve bladder function.

Imaging Features:

  • Bladder wall thickening on ultrasound or CT scan
  • Post-void residual urine volume on ultrasound

Differential Diagnosis:

  • Detrusor overactivity
  • Bladder outlet obstruction
  • Urinary tract infection
