Ureterocele

Symptoms:

Ureterocele may present with urinary tract infections, hematuria, and urinary retention.

Epidemiology:

Ureterocele is more common in females and may be associated with other urinary tract anomalies.

Pathology:

Ureterocele is a congenital abnormality characterized by the protrusion of the distal ureter into the bladder, forming a cystic dilation.

Treatment:

Treatment for ureterocele depends on the size and symptoms, but may include surgery to remove the ureterocele or to reconstruct the ureter.

Imaging Features:

  • Cystic lesion in the bladder on ultrasound or CT scan
  • Delayed emptying of the ureterocele on voiding cystourethrogram

Differential Diagnosis:

  • Bladder diverticulum
  • Bladder tumor
  • Bladder calculi
  • Posterior urethral valves
