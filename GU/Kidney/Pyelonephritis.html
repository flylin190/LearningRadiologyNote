Pyelonephritis

Symptoms:

Common symptoms of pyelonephritis include fever, chills, back pain, abdominal pain, frequent urination, and painful urination.

Epidemiology:

Pyelonephritis is more common in women than men, and is often caused by bacterial infection, such as Escherichia coli (E. coli).

Pathology:

Pyelonephritis is an infection of the kidneys, usually caused by bacteria entering the kidneys from the urinary tract.

Treatment:

Treatment for pyelonephritis typically involves antibiotics to clear the infection, as well as pain medication and fluids to prevent dehydration.

Imaging Features:

  • Enlarged kidneys with areas of decreased enhancement on CT scan
  • Presence of gas in the kidney on imaging (emphysematous pyelonephritis)

Differential Diagnosis:

  • Renal abscess
  • Renal cell carcinoma
  • Renal tuberculosis
