Medullary Sponge Kidney

Symptoms:

Medullary sponge kidney is a congenital disorder characterized by the formation of cysts in the inner medullary collecting ducts of the kidney. It is usually asymptomatic, but in some cases, it can cause recurrent kidney stones and urinary tract infections.

Epidemiology:

Medullary sponge kidney is a rare condition that is present from birth. It can affect people of all ages, but it is most commonly diagnosed in adults.

Pathology:

The exact cause of medullary sponge kidney is unknown, but it is believed to be related to abnormalities in the development of the kidney tubules.

Treatment:

Treatment for medullary sponge kidney focuses on managing symptoms, such as kidney stones and urinary tract infections. This may include medications to prevent stone formation and antibiotics to treat infections.

Imaging Features:

  • Multiple small cysts in the medullary region of the kidney on imaging studies
  • Calcifications in the cysts or in the renal papillae

Differential Diagnosis:

  • Polycystic kidney disease
  • Calyceal diverticula
