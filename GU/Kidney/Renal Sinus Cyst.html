Renal Sinus Cyst

Symptoms:

Renal sinus cysts are often asymptomatic and may be discovered incidentally during imaging studies for other reasons.

Epidemiology:

Renal sinus cysts are relatively common and can occur in individuals of all ages. They are more common in older individuals.

Pathology:

Renal sinus cysts are benign fluid-filled sacs located in the renal sinus, which is the central part of the kidney where the renal pelvis, calyces, and blood vessels are located.

Treatment:

Asymptomatic renal sinus cysts usually do not require treatment. However, large or symptomatic cysts may require drainage or surgical removal.

Imaging Features:

  • Well-defined cystic lesion in the renal sinus
  • Smooth, thin wall
  • No internal septations or solid components

Differential Diagnosis

  • Simple renal cyst
  • Hydronephrosis
  • Renal cell carcinoma
