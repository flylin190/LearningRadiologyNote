Posterior Nutcracker Syndrome

Symptoms:

Symptoms of posterior nutcracker syndrome are similar to those of nutcracker syndrome, including pelvic pain, hematuria, and proteinuria.

Epidemiology:

Posterior nutcracker syndrome is an even rarer variant of nutcracker syndrome, with a prevalence that is not well established.

Pathology:

Posterior nutcracker syndrome is caused by compression of the left renal vein between the aorta and the vertebral body, rather than the superior mesenteric artery.

Treatment:

Treatment options for posterior nutcracker syndrome are similar to those for nutcracker syndrome, including conservative management, endovascular stenting, or open surgical procedures.

Imaging Features:

  • Compression of the left renal vein between the aorta and vertebral body
  • Dilated and tortuous left renal vein with collateral vessels
  • Gonadal vein varices

Differential Diagnosis:

  • Nutcracker syndrome
  • Renal vein thrombosis
  • Pelvic congestion syndrome
