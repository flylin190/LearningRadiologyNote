Renal Infarction

Symptoms:

Common symptoms of renal infarction include sudden onset of severe flank pain, fever, and blood in the urine.

Epidemiology:

Renal infarction is rare and usually occurs in individuals with underlying conditions that predispose them to blood clot formation, such as atrial fibrillation or a history of blood clots.

Pathology:

Renal infarction occurs when blood flow to a part of the kidney is blocked, leading to tissue damage and infarction.

Treatment:

Treatment for renal infarction may include anticoagulant therapy to prevent further clot formation, pain medication, and management of underlying conditions.

Imaging Features:

  • Non-enhancing wedge-shaped areas of infarction on CT scan
  • Loss of corticomedullary differentiation on imaging

Differential Diagnosis:

  • Renal cell carcinoma
  • Renal abscess
  • Renal artery thrombosis
