Renal Cell Carcinoma

Symptoms:

Renal cell carcinoma may present with symptoms such as hematuria, flank pain, a palpable mass in the abdomen, and weight loss.

Epidemiology:

Renal cell carcinoma is more common in adults, with the highest incidence in individuals aged 50-70 years. It is more common in men than women.

Pathology:

Renal cell carcinoma arises from the renal tubular epithelium and can invade the renal vein and vena cava.

Treatment:

Treatment for renal cell carcinoma may include surgery, radiation therapy, chemotherapy, immunotherapy, and targeted therapy.

Imaging Features:

  • Solid renal mass on imaging studies
  • Enhancing mass with central necrosis
  • Renal vein or vena cava invasion

Differential Diagnosis

  • Renal papillary necrosis
  • Renal oncocytoma
  • Angiomyolipoma
