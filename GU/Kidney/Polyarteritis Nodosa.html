Polyarteritis Nodosa

Symptoms:

Symptoms of polyarteritis nodosa may include fever, weight loss, muscle pain, abdominal pain, hypertension, and various organ-specific manifestations depending on the affected blood vessels.

Epidemiology:

Polyarteritis nodosa is a rare systemic vasculitis, with an estimated annual incidence of 1-2 cases per million population.

Pathology:

Polyarteritis nodosa is an autoimmune disorder characterized by necrotizing inflammation of medium-sized arteries, leading to the development of microaneurysms and occlusions.

Treatment:

Treatment for polyarteritis nodosa typically involves immunosuppressive medications, such as corticosteroids and cyclophosphamide, to control the inflammatory process.

Imaging Features:

  • Microaneurysms or occlusions of medium-sized arteries
  • Infarcts or hemorrhage in affected organs
  • Renal involvement (e.g., renovascular hypertension, renal infarcts)
