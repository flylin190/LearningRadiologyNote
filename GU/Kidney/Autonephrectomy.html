Autonephrectomy

Symptoms:

Autonephrectomy is a rare condition where a kidney undergoes self-destruction and is eventually replaced by fibrous tissue. This process is usually asymptomatic, but in some cases, it can cause flank pain or hematuria.

Epidemiology:

Autonephrectomy is extremely rare and can occur at any age, but it is most commonly seen in older adults.

Pathology:

The exact cause of autonephrectomy is unknown, but it is believed to be associated with chronic inflammation or obstruction of the kidney.

Treatment:

There is no specific treatment for autonephrectomy, as it is a rare and usually asymptomatic condition. In cases where symptoms occur, supportive care may be provided.

Imaging Features:

  • Shrunken kidney on imaging studies
  • Replacement of renal tissue with fibrous tissue

Differential Diagnosis:

  • Chronic pyelonephritis
  • Renal infarction
