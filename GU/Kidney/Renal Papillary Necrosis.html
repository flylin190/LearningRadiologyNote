Renal Papillary Necrosis

Symptoms:

Renal papillary necrosis may present with symptoms such as flank pain, hematuria (blood in urine), and urinary tract infections (UTIs).

Epidemiology:

Renal papillary necrosis is more common in individuals with conditions such as diabetes, sickle cell disease, urinary tract obstruction, and long-term use of analgesics.

Pathology:

Renal papillary necrosis is characterized by the necrosis of the renal papillae, which are the tips of the renal pyramids in the kidneys.

Treatment:

Treatment for renal papillary necrosis focuses on managing the underlying conditions and may include pain management and treatment of infections.

Imaging Features:

  • Renal papillary sloughing on imaging studies
  • Irregular papillary outlines
  • Renal papillary calcifications

Differential Diagnosis

  • Renal cell carcinoma
  • Pyelonephritis
  • Renal infarction
