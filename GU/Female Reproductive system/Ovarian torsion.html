Ovarian Torsion

Symptoms:

Common symptoms of ovarian torsion include sudden, severe lower abdominal pain, nausea, vomiting, and fever.

Epidemiology:

Ovarian torsion can occur at any age but is most common in women of reproductive age.

Pathology:

Ovarian torsion occurs when the ovary twists on its vascular pedicle, leading to compromised blood flow to the ovary.

Treatment:

Treatment usually involves surgery to untwist the ovary and restore blood flow. In some cases, the ovary may need to be removed.

Imaging Features:

  • Ovarian enlargement with whirlpool sign on ultrasound

Differential Diagnosis

  • Ovarian cyst
  • Torsed ovarian cyst or pedunculated fibroid
  • Appendicitis
