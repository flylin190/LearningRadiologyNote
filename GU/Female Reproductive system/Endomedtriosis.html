Endometriosis

Symptoms:

Common symptoms of endometriosis include pelvic pain, painful menstrual periods (dysmenorrhea), painful intercourse (dyspareunia), and infertility.

Epidemiology:

Endometriosis is a common condition, affecting up to 10-15% of women of reproductive age. It is most commonly diagnosed in women between the ages of 25 and 35.

Pathology:

Endometriosis is a condition in which endometrial tissue (the tissue that normally lines the inside of the uterus) grows outside the uterus, typically on the ovaries, fallopian tubes, and other pelvic structures.

Treatment:

Treatment options for endometriosis include pain management with medications (such as NSAIDs or hormonal therapy), surgery to remove endometrial lesions, or a combination of both approaches. In severe cases, hysterectomy (removal of the uterus) may be considered.

Imaging Features:

  • Ovarian endometriomas (chocolate cysts)
  • Adhesions or implants in the pelvis
  • Obliteration of the cul-de-sac (pouch of Douglas)
  • Possible involvement of the bladder, bowel, or other structures

Differential Diagnosis:

  • Pelvic inflammatory disease (PID)
  • Ovarian cysts or masses
  • Adenomyosis
  • Pelvic adhesions from other causes
