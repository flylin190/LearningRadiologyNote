Herlyn-Werner-Wunderlich Syndrome

Symptoms:

Symptoms of Herlyn-Werner-Wunderlich syndrome include pelvic pain, dysmenorrhea, and sometimes, dyspareunia or infertility. Some patients may also have recurrent urinary tract infections.

Epidemiology:

Herlyn-Werner-Wunderlich syndrome is a rare congenital anomaly, with an estimated prevalence of 1 in 200,000 women. It is caused by embryonic developmental abnormalities of the Müllerian ducts.

Pathology:

Herlyn-Werner-Wunderlich syndrome is characterized by the triad of uterine didelphys (double uterus), obstructed hemivaginal septum, and ipsilateral renal agenesis.

Treatment:

Treatment typically involves surgical resection of the obstructed vaginal septum and excision of the obstructed uterine horn or cavity. Depending on the severity, a hysterectomy may be required in some cases.

Imaging Features:

  • Uterine didelphys (double uterus) on ultrasound or MRI
  • Obstructed hemivaginal septum with associated hematocolpos or hematometra
  • Ipsilateral renal agenesis

Differential Diagnosis:

  • Other Müllerian duct anomalies (e.g., uterine septum, bicornuate uterus)
  • Pelvic masses or cysts
  • Endometriosis
