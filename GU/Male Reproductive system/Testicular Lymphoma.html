Testicular Lymphoma

Symptoms:

Symptoms of testicular lymphoma include painless testicular swelling, heaviness or discomfort in the scrotum, and, in some cases, back pain or abdominal discomfort.

Epidemiology:

Testicular lymphoma is a rare form of extranodal lymphoma, accounting for 1-2% of all non-Hodgkin lymphomas and 1-7% of testicular malignancies.

Pathology:

Testicular lymphoma is typically a diffuse large B-cell lymphoma, arising from the testicular parenchyma or the spermatic cord. It can be primary (confined to the testis) or secondary (spread from elsewhere).

Treatment:

Treatment typically involves orchiectomy (surgical removal of the affected testis) followed by chemotherapy, with or without radiation therapy, depending on the stage and risk factors.

Imaging Features:

  • Testicular enlargement and heterogeneous appearance on ultrasound
  • Diffuse or focal areas of decreased echogenicity
  • Irregular margins and invasion of surrounding structures

Differential Diagnosis:

  • Testicular germ cell tumors
  • Testicular torsion
  • Testicular infarction
