Testicular Microlithiasis

Symptoms:

Testicular Microlithiasis is usually asymptomatic and is often an incidental finding on testicular ultrasound.

Epidemiology:

Testicular Microlithiasis is most commonly seen in adult men, particularly those with certain risk factors such as a history of cryptorchidism, testicular trauma, or infertility.

Pathology:

Testicular Microlithiasis is characterized by the presence of multiple tiny calcifications within the seminiferous tubules of the testicles. The exact cause is unknown.

Treatment:

There is no specific treatment for Testicular Microlithiasis. Regular follow-up with a urologist may be recommended to monitor for any changes or complications.

Imaging Features:

  • Multiple, small, hyperechoic foci within the testicular parenchyma on ultrasound

Differential Diagnosis

  • Testicular tumor
  • Calcification from previous trauma or infection
