Yolk Sac Tumor (AFP)

Symptoms:

Yolk sac tumor may present with abdominal pain, bloating, a palpable mass in the abdomen, weight loss, and symptoms related to metastasis, such as respiratory symptoms if the tumor has spread to the lungs.

Epidemiology:

Yolk sac tumor is a type of germ cell tumor that most commonly occurs in children and young adults. It is the most common testicular tumor in infants and young boys.

Pathology:

The pathology of yolk sac tumor involves the presence of yolk sac-like structures, which are characteristic on histological examination. Elevated levels of alpha-fetoprotein (AFP) are also typical.

Treatment:

Treatment for yolk sac tumor usually involves surgery to remove the tumor, followed by chemotherapy. In some cases, radiation therapy may also be used.

Imaging Features:

  • Large, heterogeneous mass on ultrasound
  • Enhancing mass with areas of necrosis on CT or MRI
  • Elevated levels of AFP in blood tests

Differential Diagnosis:

  • Teratoma
  • Embryonal carcinoma
  • Endodermal sinus tumor
