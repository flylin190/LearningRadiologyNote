Adrenal Myelolipoma

Symptoms:

Adrenal myelolipomas are usually asymptomatic and are often discovered incidentally on imaging studies. However, in some cases, they may cause abdominal or flank pain.

Epidemiology:

Adrenal myelolipomas are rare benign tumors that occur most commonly in adults between the ages of 40 and 60 years old.

Pathology:

Adrenal myelolipomas are composed of mature adipose tissue and hematopoietic elements, such as myeloid cells, erythrocytes, and megakaryocytes.

Treatment:

Most adrenal myelolipomas do not require treatment unless they are causing symptoms or are large in size. Surgical removal may be considered in these cases.

Imaging Features:

  • Well-circumscribed mass with fat density on CT or MRI
  • Presence of hematopoietic elements, which may appear hyperdense on imaging

Differential Diagnosis:

  • Adrenal adenoma
  • Adrenal carcinoma
