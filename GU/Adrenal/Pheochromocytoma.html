Pheochromocytoma

Symptoms:

Pheochromocytoma may present with paroxysmal hypertension, headaches, palpitations, sweating, and tremors.

Epidemiology:

Pheochromocytoma is a rare tumor that can occur at any age, but most commonly diagnosed in adults aged 30-50 years.

Pathology:

Pheochromocytoma arises from chromaffin cells of the adrenal medulla or sympathetic ganglia.

Treatment:

Treatment involves surgical resection of the tumor. Preoperative management with alpha-adrenergic blockers followed by beta-blockers may be necessary to control hypertension.

Imaging Features:

  • Enhancing adrenal mass on CT or MRI
  • May show areas of hemorrhage or necrosis

Differential Diagnosis

  • Adrenal adenoma
  • Metastasis to the adrenal gland
  • Adrenal cortical carcinoma
