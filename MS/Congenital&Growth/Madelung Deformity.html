Medelung Deformity

Symptoms:

Medelung deformity is characterized by bilateral wrist deformity, usually with volar subluxation of the distal ulna, leading to a prominent ulnar head and shortened ulna. Patients may also experience wrist pain and limited range of motion.

Epidemiology:

Medelung deformity is a rare condition that typically presents in adolescence and is more common in females.

Pathology:

The exact cause of Medelung deformity is unknown, but it is thought to be related to abnormal growth of the ulnar physis during development.

Treatment:

Treatment for Medelung deformity may include conservative measures such as splinting and physical therapy. In severe cases, surgery may be required to correct the deformity.

Imaging Features:

  • Shortened and tilted distal ulna on x-ray
  • Prominent ulnar head

Differential Diagnosis:

  • Madelung disease
  • Radial head dislocation
  • Chronic wrist sprain
