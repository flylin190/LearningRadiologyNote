Bipartite Patella

Symptoms:

Bipartite patella typically presents with anterior knee pain, especially with activities that require bending of the knee. Swelling and tenderness over the bipartite fragment may also be present.

Epidemiology:

Bipartite patella is a relatively rare condition that is more common in males and often affects individuals between the ages of 10 and 20 years.

Pathology:

Bipartite patella is a congenital condition characterized by the failure of the patella to fuse during childhood, resulting in a bipartite (two-part) patella.

Treatment:

Treatment for bipartite patella is usually conservative and may include rest, ice, anti-inflammatory medications, and physical therapy. In some cases, surgery may be necessary to remove the bipartite fragment.

Imaging Features:

  • Radiographs typically show a well-defined, corticated fragment of bone adjacent to the superolateral aspect of the patella
  • CT or MRI may be used to further evaluate the fragment and assess for any associated pathology
