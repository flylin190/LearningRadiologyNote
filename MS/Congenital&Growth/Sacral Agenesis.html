Sacral Agenesis

Symptoms:

Sacral agenesis may present with a variety of symptoms depending on the severity of the condition. Common symptoms include lower back pain, leg weakness, sensory deficits, and bowel and bladder dysfunction.

Epidemiology:

Sacral agenesis is a rare congenital condition that affects the development of the sacrum, the triangular bone at the base of the spine. It is more common in females than males.

Pathology:

Sacral agenesis results from abnormal development of the caudal portion of the embryo during early pregnancy. It can occur sporadically or be associated with genetic syndromes such as VACTERL association.

Treatment:

Treatment for sacral agenesis focuses on managing symptoms and improving quality of life. This may include physical therapy, assistive devices, and surgical interventions to address specific complications.

Imaging Features:

  • Absence or underdevelopment of the sacrum on radiographs
  • Malformations of adjacent vertebrae
  • Associated anomalies such as spinal dysraphism or lower limb abnormalities

Differential Diagnosis:

  • Caudal regression syndrome
  • Sacral dysgenesis
  • Spinal cord tethering
