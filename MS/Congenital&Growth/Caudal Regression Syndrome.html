Caudal Regression Syndrome

Symptoms:

Symptoms of caudal regression syndrome can vary depending on the severity, but may include abnormalities of the lower spine, lower limbs, and urogenital system. Neurological deficits, incontinence, and impaired mobility are common.

Epidemiology:

Caudal regression syndrome is a rare congenital anomaly, with an estimated incidence of 1 in 60,000 live births. It is more common in infants of diabetic mothers.

Pathology:

Caudal regression syndrome is a developmental disorder characterized by abnormal formation or regression of the caudal (lower) end of the embryo, resulting in varying degrees of malformations of the lower spine, lower limbs, and urogenital system.

Treatment:

Treatment for caudal regression syndrome is supportive and depends on the specific malformations present. It may involve surgical interventions, such as reconstruction of the urogenital system, spinal stabilization, or amputation of the lower limbs in severe cases.

Imaging Features:

  • Partial or complete absence of the lower spine (sacrum and coccyx)
  • Malformations or absence of the lower limbs
  • Abnormalities of the urogenital system (e.g., renal agenesis, imperforate anus)

Differential Diagnosis:

  • Spina bifida
  • Sacral agenesis
  • Other congenital spinal and limb malformations
