PCL Tear

Symptoms:

Common symptoms of a PCL tear include pain, swelling, and instability in the knee. Patients may also experience difficulty walking or bearing weight on the affected knee.

Epidemiology:

PCL tears are less common than ACL tears and are often seen in sports-related injuries or trauma to the knee.

Pathology:

A PCL tear is a sprain or tear of the posterior cruciate ligament, which is located in the back of the knee. It can occur due to a direct blow to the front of the knee or from hyperextension of the knee.

Treatment:

Treatment for a PCL tear may include rest, physical therapy, and in some cases, surgery to reconstruct the torn ligament.

Imaging Features:

  • Posterior sag sign on X-ray
  • Increased signal intensity in the PCL on MRI

Differential Diagnosis:

  • ACL tear
  • Meniscus tear
  • Collateral ligament sprain
