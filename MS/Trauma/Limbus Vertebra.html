Limbus Vertebra

Symptoms:

Limbus vertebra is typically an incidental finding and asymptomatic. However, in some cases, it may be associated with back pain or radiculopathy (nerve root compression) if the abnormal ossification impinges on nearby structures.

Epidemiology:

Limbus vertebra is a relatively uncommon congenital anomaly, with a reported prevalence of around 3-15% in the general population. It is more commonly seen in the lower lumbar and upper sacral regions.

Pathology:

Limbus vertebra is characterized by the presence of an osseous or cartilaginous ridge or rim arising from the anterior/superior margins of a vertebral body.

Treatment:

Most cases of limbus vertebra are asymptomatic and do not require treatment. If symptoms are present, conservative management with physical therapy, activity modification, and pain medication may be recommended. Surgical intervention is rarely necessary.

Imaging Features:

  • Bony or cartilaginous ridge or rim arising from the vertebral body margins on X-ray, CT, or MRI
  • May be associated with spinal canal or foraminal narrowing in some cases

Differential Diagnosis:

  • Osteophytes (bone spurs)
  • Facet joint hypertrophy
  • Bony or cartilaginous tumors
