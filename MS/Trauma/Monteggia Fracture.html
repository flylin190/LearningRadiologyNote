Monteggia Fracture

Symptoms:

Monteggia fracture is a fracture of the proximal ulna with dislocation of the proximal radius. Symptoms include pain, swelling, and deformity of the forearm.

Epidemiology:

Monteggia fracture is more common in children and young adults and is often caused by a direct blow to the forearm.

Pathology:

The fracture occurs at the proximal end of the ulna, often with dislocation of the radial head.

Treatment:

Treatment of Monteggia fracture involves surgical fixation of the ulna and reduction of the radial head. Immobilization with a cast may also be necessary.

Imaging Features:

  • Fracture of the proximal ulna
  • Dislocation of the proximal radius
