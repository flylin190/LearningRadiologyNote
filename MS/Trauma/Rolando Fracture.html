Rolando Fracture

Symptoms:

Patients with a Rolando fracture typically experience pain, swelling, and deformity in the affected hand or wrist. Decreased range of motion and instability may also be present.

Epidemiology:

Rolando fractures are relatively uncommon and account for approximately 2-4% of all hand fractures. They are more common in young, active individuals due to high-energy trauma.

Pathology:

A Rolando fracture is a comminuted intra-articular fracture involving the base of the first metacarpal bone and extending into the carpometacarpal joint of the thumb.

Treatment:

Treatment for Rolando fractures typically involves closed reduction and immobilization with a thumb spica cast or surgical fixation, depending on the fracture pattern and degree of displacement.

Imaging Features:

  • Fracture line extending through the base of the first metacarpal and into the carpometacarpal joint
  • Potential comminution or displacement of fracture fragments
  • Joint space widening or subluxation of the carpometacarpal joint

Differential Diagnosis:

  • Bennett's fracture
  • Boxer's fracture
  • Carpometacarpal joint dislocation
