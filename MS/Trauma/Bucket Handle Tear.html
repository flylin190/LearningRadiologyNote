Bucket Handle Tear

Symptoms:

Symptoms of a bucket handle tear include sudden pain, swelling, and locking or catching of the knee joint during movement.

Epidemiology:

Bucket handle tears are a type of meniscal tear that can occur in people of any age, but are more common in athletes and individuals who participate in activities that involve twisting or pivoting movements of the knee.

Pathology:

A bucket handle tear is a longitudinal tear in the meniscus that extends through the full thickness of the cartilage, resulting in a displaced fragment that can flip or displace into the joint space, causing locking or catching.

Treatment:

Treatment options for bucket handle tears include immobilization, physical therapy, and, in many cases, arthroscopic surgery to repair or partially remove the torn meniscus.

Imaging Features:

  • Displaced meniscal fragment or "bucket handle" on MRI or arthroscopy
  • Associated joint effusion or bone marrow edema on MRI

Differential Diagnosis:

  • Other types of meniscal tears (e.g., radial, horizontal)
  • Loose bodies in the joint
  • Ligament or cartilage injuries
