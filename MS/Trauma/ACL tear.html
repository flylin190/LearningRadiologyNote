ACL Tear

Symptoms:

Common symptoms of an ACL tear include a popping sound or sensation at the time of injury, severe pain, swelling, instability of the knee, and difficulty walking.

Epidemiology:

ACL tears are most commonly seen in athletes who participate in sports that involve sudden stops and changes in direction, such as basketball, soccer, and football.

Pathology:

An ACL tear is a sprain or tear of the anterior cruciate ligament, which is one of the major ligaments in the knee. It can occur due to sudden twisting or hyperextension of the knee.

Treatment:

Treatment for an ACL tear may include rest, physical therapy, and surgery to reconstruct the torn ligament.

Imaging Features:

  • Increased signal intensity in the ACL on MRI

Differential Diagnosis:

  • Meniscus tear
  • Collateral ligament sprain
  • Patellar dislocation
