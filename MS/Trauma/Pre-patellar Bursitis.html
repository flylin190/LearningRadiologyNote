Pre-patellar Bursitis

Symptoms:

Pre-patellar bursitis typically presents with swelling, pain, and tenderness over the kneecap. The area may also be warm to the touch.

Epidemiology:

Pre-patellar bursitis can occur in anyone, but it is more common in individuals who kneel for extended periods, such as carpet layers, plumbers, and gardeners.

Pathology:

Pre-patellar bursitis is caused by irritation or inflammation of the pre-patellar bursa, a small fluid-filled sac located in front of the kneecap.

Treatment:

Treatment for pre-patellar bursitis includes rest, ice, compression, and elevation (RICE therapy), as well as anti-inflammatory medications. In severe cases, aspiration of the bursa or corticosteroid injections may be necessary.

Imaging Features:

  • Soft tissue swelling around the knee on ultrasound or MRI

Differential Diagnosis:

  • Patellar tendonitis
  • Septic arthritis
