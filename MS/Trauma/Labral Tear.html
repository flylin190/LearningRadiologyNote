Labral Tear

Symptoms:

Common symptoms of a labral tear in the shoulder or hip include pain, especially with certain movements or activities, and a catching or locking sensation in the joint.

Epidemiology:

Labral tears can occur in people of all ages, but are more common in athletes and individuals who perform repetitive overhead activities.

Pathology:

A labral tear is a tear in the ring of cartilage (labrum) that surrounds the socket of the shoulder or hip joint.

Treatment:

Treatment for a labral tear may include rest, physical therapy, anti-inflammatory medications, and in some cases, surgery to repair the torn labrum.

Imaging Features:

  • Labral detachment or abnormality on MRI
  • Fluid signal within the labrum on MRI

Differential Diagnosis:

  • Shoulder impingement syndrome
  • Rotator cuff tear
  • Shoulder instability
