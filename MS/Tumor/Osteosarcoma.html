Osteosarcoma

Symptoms:

Common symptoms of osteosarcoma include bone pain, swelling, and, in some cases, a noticeable mass.

Epidemiology:

Osteosarcoma is most common in children and young adults, with a slight male predominance.

Pathology:

Osteosarcoma is a malignant bone tumor characterized by the production of osteoid tissue by tumor cells.

Treatment:

Treatment typically involves a combination of surgery, chemotherapy, and sometimes radiation therapy.

Imaging Features:

  • Aggressive bone destruction with periosteal reaction on X-ray
  • Osteoid matrix formation on histology

Differential Diagnosis

  • Osteomyelitis
  • Ewing sarcoma
  • Chondrosarcoma
