Cortical Desmoid

Symptoms:

Cortical desmoids are usually asymptomatic and are often discovered incidentally on imaging studies. Rarely, they may present with pain or swelling in the affected bone.

Epidemiology:

Cortical desmoids are rare benign lesions that can occur at any age, with a slight male predominance.

Pathology:

Cortical desmoids are fibrous cortical defects that are thought to result from a reactive process rather than a true neoplasm.

Treatment:

Most cortical desmoids do not require treatment and are managed conservatively with observation. Surgical excision may be considered in cases of symptomatic lesions or those at risk for fracture.

Imaging Features:

  • Well-circumscribed radiolucent lesion with sclerotic margins on X-ray
  • Typically located in the cortex of long bones
  • No periosteal reaction or soft tissue mass

Differential Diagnosis

  • Fibrous dysplasia
  • Simple bone cyst
  • Nonossifying fibroma
