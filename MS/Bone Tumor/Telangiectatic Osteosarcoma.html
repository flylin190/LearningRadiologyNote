Telangiectatic Osteosarcoma

Symptoms:

Symptoms of telangiectatic osteosarcoma may include bone pain, swelling, and in some cases, pathological fractures.

Epidemiology:

Telangiectatic osteosarcoma is a rare subtype of osteosarcoma, accounting for approximately 3-5% of all osteosarcoma cases. It typically affects older adults, with a peak incidence in the second and third decades of life.

Pathology:

Telangiectatic osteosarcoma is characterized by a highly vascular tumor with large, dilated blood-filled spaces and areas of necrosis. It is an aggressive form of osteosarcoma and has a higher risk of metastasis compared to other subtypes.

Treatment:

Treatment typically involves a combination of chemotherapy and surgical resection, followed by additional chemotherapy. In some cases, radiation therapy may also be used as part of the treatment regimen.

Imaging Features:

  • Lytic, destructive bone lesion with a "moth-eaten" appearance on X-ray
  • Heterogeneous signal intensity with fluid-fluid levels on MRI
  • Marked enhancement and surrounding edema on contrast-enhanced MRI or CT

Differential Diagnosis:

  • Other subtypes of osteosarcoma (e.g., conventional, chondroblastic)
  • Aneurysmal bone cyst
  • Bone metastases
