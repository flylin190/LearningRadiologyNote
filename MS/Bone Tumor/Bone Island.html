Bone Island

Symptoms:

Bone islands are typically asymptomatic and are incidental findings on imaging studies.

Epidemiology:

Bone islands are relatively common and can be found in up to 25% of the population. They are more prevalent in older individuals and have a slight predilection for women.

Pathology:

Bone islands are benign, localized areas of compact bone within the cancellous bone. They are thought to represent areas of non-neoplastic and dysplastic bone formation.

Treatment:

Bone islands are benign and usually do not require any treatment. They are typically left alone unless they are causing symptoms or confusion with other pathologies.

Imaging Features:

  • Well-circumscribed, round or oval, sclerotic lesion
  • Typically located in the pelvis, proximal femur, or other long bones
  • May show a central radiolucent area or concentric rings

Differential Diagnosis

  • Osteoid osteoma
  • Osteoblastic metastasis
  • Fibrous dysplasia
  • Osteosarcoma
