Chondromyxoid Fibroma

Symptoms:

Chondromyxoid fibroma often presents as a slow-growing, painless swelling or mass in the affected bone. It may cause pain if it grows large enough to compress surrounding structures.

Epidemiology:

Chondromyxoid fibroma is a rare benign bone tumor that usually affects young adults. It most commonly occurs in the long bones of the lower extremities.

Pathology:

Chondromyxoid fibroma is characterized by the presence of chondroid and myxoid tissue within the tumor. It is considered a benign tumor but can be locally aggressive.

Treatment:

The main treatment for chondromyxoid fibroma is surgical removal of the tumor. In some cases, additional treatments such as radiation therapy or bisphosphonates may be considered.

Imaging Features:

  • Well-defined lytic lesion with sclerotic margins on X-ray
  • Mixed lytic and sclerotic appearance on CT scan
  • Low to intermediate signal intensity on T1-weighted MRI
  • High signal intensity on T2-weighted MRI

Differential Diagnosis:

  • Chondrosarcoma
  • Fibrous dysplasia
  • Enchondroma
