Simple Bone Cyst

Symptoms:

Simple bone cysts are often asymptomatic and may be discovered incidentally on imaging studies. If symptomatic, they may cause pain or pathological fractures.

Epidemiology:

Simple bone cysts are most commonly seen in children and adolescents, with a slight male predominance.

Pathology:

Simple bone cysts are benign bone lesions that are filled with fluid. The exact cause is unknown, but they may be related to abnormal development of the bone.

Treatment:

Treatment for simple bone cysts may include observation, aspiration (removal of fluid from the cyst), injection of steroid or bone marrow, or surgical procedures such as curettage and bone grafting.

Imaging Features:

  • Well-defined, lucent (dark) lesion on X-ray
  • No periosteal reaction
  • Cystic lesion with fluid level on MRI

Differential Diagnosis:

  • Aneurysmal bone cyst
  • Unicameral bone cyst
  • Fibrous dysplasia
