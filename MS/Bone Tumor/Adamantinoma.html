Adamantinoma

Symptoms:

Adamantinoma commonly presents with pain and swelling in the affected bone, most commonly in the tibia. Some patients may also experience a palpable mass and pathological fractures.

Epidemiology:

Adamantinoma is a rare primary bone tumor that usually occurs in young adults, with a slight female predominance.

Pathology:

Adamantinoma is characterized by epithelial-like cells in a fibrous stroma. It is typically low-grade and locally aggressive.

Treatment:

Treatment for adamantinoma usually involves surgery to remove the tumor, followed by radiation therapy in some cases. Chemotherapy may be used in cases of metastatic disease.

Imaging Features:

  • Well-defined osteolytic lesion with cortical thickening on X-ray
  • Soft tissue extension and periosteal reaction
  • Low signal intensity on T1-weighted MRI

Differential Diagnosis

  • Osteofibrous dysplasia
  • Ewing sarcoma
  • Chondromyxoid fibroma
