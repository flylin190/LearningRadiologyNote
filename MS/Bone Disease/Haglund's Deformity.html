Haglund's Deformity

Symptoms:

Haglund's deformity typically presents with a painful bump on the back of the heel, swelling, and redness in the affected area. It may also cause heel pain when walking or running.

Epidemiology:

Haglund's deformity is more common in women and often occurs in people who wear shoes with rigid backs that irritate the heel.

Pathology:

Haglund's deformity is characterized by a bony enlargement on the back of the heel bone, often associated with Achilles tendonitis.

Treatment:

Treatment for Haglund's deformity includes wearing shoes with soft backs, using orthotic devices, physical therapy, and in severe cases, surgery to remove the bony prominence.

Imaging Features:

  • Heel spurs or bony enlargement on X-ray

Differential Diagnosis:

  • Achilles tendonitis
  • Calcaneal stress fracture
  • Sever's disease (in adolescents)
