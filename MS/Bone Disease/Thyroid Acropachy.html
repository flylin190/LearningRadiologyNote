Thyroid Aropachy

Symptoms:

Thyroid aropachy is a condition that affects the thyroid gland, causing symptoms such as bulging eyes (exophthalmos), eye irritation, and double vision. It is often associated with Graves' disease.

Epidemiology:

Thyroid aropachy is more common in women than men, and usually occurs in people with Graves' disease, an autoimmune disorder that causes overactivity of the thyroid gland.

Pathology:

The exact cause of thyroid aropachy is not fully understood, but it is thought to involve an autoimmune response that targets the tissues around the eyes and the thyroid gland.

Treatment:

Treatment for thyroid aropachy focuses on managing symptoms and may include medications to reduce inflammation and swelling, as well as surgery in severe cases to correct eye bulging.

Imaging Features:

  • Enlargement of the extraocular muscles on MRI or CT scan
  • Thyroid gland enlargement on ultrasound

Differential Diagnosis:

  • Graves' ophthalmopathy
  • Orbital cellulitis
  • Thyroid cancer metastasis
