Septic Arthritis

Symptoms:

Common symptoms of septic arthritis include pain, swelling, warmth, and redness in the affected joint, as well as fever and chills.

Epidemiology:

Septic arthritis can occur at any age, but it is more common in older adults and people with certain medical conditions, such as diabetes or a weakened immune system.

Pathology:

Septic arthritis is usually caused by bacteria, such as Staphylococcus aureus, that enter the joint through the bloodstream or from an infection in the surrounding tissue.

Treatment:

Treatment for septic arthritis usually involves antibiotics to kill the bacteria, as well as drainage of the infected joint fluid.

Imaging Features:

  • Joint effusion on X-ray
  • Joint space narrowing on X-ray
  • Increased uptake on bone scan

Differential Diagnosis:

  • Gout
  • Rheumatoid arthritis
  • Reactive arthritis
