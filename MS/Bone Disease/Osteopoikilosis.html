Osteopoikilosis

Symptoms:

Osteopoikilosis is typically asymptomatic and is often discovered incidentally on radiographic studies performed for other reasons.

Epidemiology:

Osteopoikilosis is a rare, benign skeletal dysplasia that can occur sporadically or as an inherited autosomal dominant condition. It is present from birth and affects both males and females equally.

Pathology:

Osteopoikilosis is characterized by the presence of multiple, randomly distributed, compact bone lesions or islands throughout the skeleton. These lesions are thought to result from a defect in endochondral bone formation during embryonic development.

Treatment:

Osteopoikilosis is a benign condition that does not require specific treatment. Management involves reassurance and education about the benign nature of the condition.

Imaging Features:

  • Multiple, round or oval, dense bone lesions scattered throughout the skeleton
  • Lesions are typically small (1-10 mm) and have a uniform appearance
  • Lesions are most commonly found in the epiphyses and metaphyses of long bones, but can also be present in the axial skeleton
  • No associated soft tissue or bony reaction

Differential Diagnosis:

  • Bone islands (enostoses)
  • Osteoblastic metastases
  • Mastocytosis with bone involvement
  • Osteosarcoma (in cases with atypical or aggressive appearance)
