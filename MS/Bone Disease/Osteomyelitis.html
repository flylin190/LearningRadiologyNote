Osteomyelitis

Symptoms:

Common symptoms of osteomyelitis include pain, swelling, warmth, and redness over the affected bone, as well as fever and chills.

Epidemiology:

Osteomyelitis can occur at any age, but it is more common in children and older adults. It can affect any bone in the body.

Pathology:

Osteomyelitis is usually caused by bacteria, such as Staphylococcus aureus, that enter the body through an injury or surgery and spread to the bone.

Treatment:

Treatment for osteomyelitis usually involves antibiotics to kill the bacteria, as well as surgery to remove any dead or infected tissue.

Imaging Features:

  • Bone destruction on X-ray
  • Soft tissue swelling on X-ray
  • Bone abscess formation on MRI

Differential Diagnosis:

  • Bone tumors
  • Septic arthritis
  • Cellulitis
