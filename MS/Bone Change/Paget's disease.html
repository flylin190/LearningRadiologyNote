Paget's Disease

Symptoms:

Most people with Paget's disease of bone have no symptoms. However, some may experience bone pain, joint pain, and deformities.

Epidemiology:

Paget's disease of bone is more common in older adults, and its prevalence increases with age. It is more common in men than women.

Pathology:

Paget's disease is characterized by excessive bone remodeling, leading to enlarged and weakened bones.

Treatment:

Treatment for Paget's disease may include medications to reduce bone turnover, such as bisphosphonates, and in some cases, surgery to correct deformities or repair fractures.

Imaging Features:

  • Characteristic "picture frame" appearance on X-ray
  • Increased uptake on bone scan

Differential Diagnosis:

  • Metastatic bone disease
  • Osteoporosis
  • Primary bone tumors
