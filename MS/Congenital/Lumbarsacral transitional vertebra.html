Lumbosacral Transitional Vertebra

Symptoms:

Lumbosacral transitional vertebra may be asymptomatic in many cases. However, it can contribute to lower back pain, sciatica, and spinal instability in some individuals.

Epidemiology:

Lumbosacral transitional vertebra is a common anatomical variant that occurs in approximately 10% of the population. It is more common in males and may be bilateral or unilateral.

Pathology:

Lumbosacral transitional vertebra is characterized by the presence of an additional lumbar vertebra or sacralized L5 vertebra. This can lead to variations in the number and shape of lumbar and sacral segments.

Treatment:

Treatment for lumbosacral transitional vertebra is usually conservative and focused on managing symptoms. This may include physical therapy, pain management, and lifestyle modifications.

Imaging Features:

  • Abnormal morphology of the lumbosacral junction on radiographs
  • Presence of an additional lumbar vertebra or sacralized L5 vertebra
  • Altered biomechanics of the lumbar spine

Differential Diagnosis:

  • Lumbarization of S1 vertebra
  • Sacralization of L5 vertebra
  • Lumbar disc herniation
  • Spondylolisthesis
