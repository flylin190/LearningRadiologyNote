Heterotopic Ossification

Symptoms:

Heterotopic ossification may not cause any symptoms, or it may cause pain, swelling, and limited range of motion in the affected joint.

Epidemiology:

Heterotopic ossification is a relatively rare condition that most commonly occurs after major trauma or surgery, particularly in the hip and elbow joints.

Pathology:

Heterotopic ossification is the abnormal formation of bone in soft tissues outside the skeleton. It is thought to result from the transformation of mesenchymal stem cells into osteoblasts, which then form bone.

Treatment:

Treatment for heterotopic ossification may include physical therapy, nonsteroidal anti-inflammatory drugs (NSAIDs), or in severe cases, surgery to remove the excess bone.

Imaging Features:

  • Calcification or ossification in soft tissues on X-ray
  • Increased uptake on bone scan
  • Loss of joint space on X-ray

Differential Diagnosis

  • Myositis ossificans
  • Osteosarcoma
  • Metastatic bone disease
