Fibrous Dysplasia

Symptoms:

Fibrous dysplasia may be asymptomatic or present with pain, swelling, or deformity of the affected bone. It can also lead to fractures or other complications.

Epidemiology:

Fibrous dysplasia can occur at any age but is most commonly diagnosed in children and young adults. It can affect any bone in the body.

Pathology:

Fibrous dysplasia is characterized by the replacement of normal bone with fibrous tissue and immature woven bone. It is caused by a genetic mutation in the GNAS gene.

Treatment:

The treatment for fibrous dysplasia depends on the symptoms and extent of the disease. It may include monitoring, pain management, and surgical intervention in some cases.

Imaging Features:

  • Ground-glass appearance on X-ray
  • Homogeneous and expansile lesion on CT scan
  • Hypointense on T1-weighted MRI
  • Variable signal intensity on T2-weighted MRI

Differential Diagnosis:

  • Non-ossifying fibroma
  • Simple bone cyst
  • Low-grade osteosarcoma
