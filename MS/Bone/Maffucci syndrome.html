Maffucci Syndrome

Symptoms:

Maffucci syndrome is characterized by multiple enchondromas and hemangiomas. Patients may present with pain, swelling, and deformity of the affected bones.

Epidemiology:

Maffucci syndrome is a rare disorder that typically presents in childhood. The exact cause is unknown, but it is thought to be a sporadic genetic mutation.

Pathology:

Maffucci syndrome is characterized by the presence of multiple enchondromas, which are benign cartilaginous tumors, and hemangiomas, which are benign vascular tumors.

Treatment:

There is no specific treatment for Maffucci syndrome. Management is focused on addressing symptoms and complications, such as pain and fractures, associated with the tumors.

Imaging Features:

  • Multiple enchondromas and hemangiomas on x-ray
  • Enchondromas typically have a stippled or ring-and-arc calcification pattern
  • Hemangiomas may appear as areas of increased vascularity

Differential Diagnosis:

  • Ollier disease (multiple enchondromatosis)
  • Hereditary multiple exostoses
  • Metastatic disease
