Ollier Disease

Symptoms:

Ollier disease is characterized by multiple enchondromas, which are benign cartilaginous tumors, in the bones. It can lead to bone deformities, limb length discrepancies, and fractures.

Epidemiology:

Ollier disease is a rare condition that usually presents in childhood. The exact cause is unknown, but it is not inherited in a simple Mendelian manner.

Pathology:

Enchondromas in Ollier disease are typically located in the metaphysis and diaphysis of long bones and are composed of hyaline cartilage.

Treatment:

Treatment for Ollier disease is typically focused on managing symptoms and complications, such as fractures. Surgery may be required to correct bone deformities.

Imaging Features:

  • Multiple well-defined lucent lesions with thin sclerotic rims on plain radiographs
  • Enchondromas typically involve the metaphysis and diaphysis of long bones
  • May lead to bowing of affected bones

Differential Diagnosis:

  • Maffucci syndrome
  • Metastatic disease
  • Paget disease
