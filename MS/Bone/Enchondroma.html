Enchondroma

Symptoms:

Enchondromas are usually asymptomatic and are often discovered incidentally on imaging studies performed for other reasons. However, they can cause pain, swelling, or fractures if they occur in weight-bearing bones or undergo malignant transformation.

Epidemiology:

Enchondromas are the most common primary bone tumor of the hand, typically affecting individuals between the ages of 10 and 40 years.

Pathology:

Enchondromas are benign cartilaginous tumors that arise from the medullary cavity of the bone. They are composed of hyaline cartilage and can undergo malignant transformation to chondrosarcoma in rare cases.

Treatment:

Asymptomatic enchondromas usually do not require treatment. However, symptomatic or aggressive lesions may require surgical resection.

Imaging Features:

  • Well-defined lytic lesion with stippled or ring-and-arc calcifications on x-ray
  • Homogeneous low signal intensity on T1-weighted MRI
  • Enhancement may be seen with contrast administration

Differential Diagnosis:

  • Chondrosarcoma
  • Fibrous dysplasia
  • Osteochondroma
