Macrodystrophia Lipomatosa

Symptoms:

Macrodystrophia lipomatosa is a rare congenital disorder characterized by overgrowth of the bone and surrounding soft tissues, typically affecting the fingers and toes.

Epidemiology:

Macrodystrophia lipomatosa is a rare condition, with less than 100 cases reported in the literature. It is usually present at birth or becomes apparent during childhood.

Pathology:

The exact cause of macrodystrophia lipomatosa is unknown, but it is thought to result from a disturbance in the growth of the nerves that supply the affected area, leading to overgrowth of the bone and soft tissues.

Treatment:

Treatment for macrodystrophia lipomatosa depends on the severity of the condition and the extent of the overgrowth. It may include surgery to remove excess tissue and improve function and appearance.

Imaging Features:

  • Soft tissue and bone overgrowth on X-ray
  • Presence of fatty tissue within the overgrown area

Differential Diagnosis:

  • Neurofibromatosis
  • Proteus syndrome
  • Mesenchymal hamartoma
