Rheumatoid Arthritis

Symptoms:

Rheumatoid arthritis is characterized by joint pain, swelling, and stiffness, especially in the morning or after periods of inactivity. It can also cause fatigue, fever, and weight loss.

Epidemiology:

Rheumatoid arthritis affects about 1% of the population worldwide, with women being more commonly affected than men. It usually begins between the ages of 30 and 60.

Pathology:

Rheumatoid arthritis is an autoimmune disease that causes chronic inflammation of the joints, leading to damage and deformity. The exact cause is unknown, but it is believed to involve a combination of genetic and environmental factors.

Treatment:

Treatment for rheumatoid arthritis aims to reduce inflammation, relieve symptoms, and prevent joint damage. This may include medications such as nonsteroidal anti-inflammatory drugs (NSAIDs), corticosteroids, disease-modifying antirheumatic drugs (DMARDs), and biologic agents.

Imaging Features:

  • Joint erosions and narrowing on X-ray
  • Synovitis and joint effusion on ultrasound
  • Bone marrow edema on MRI

Differential Diagnosis

  • Osteoarthritis
  • Psoriatic arthritis
  • Lupus arthritis
