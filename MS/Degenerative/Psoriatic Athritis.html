Psoriatic Arthritis

Symptoms:

Psoriatic arthritis typically presents with joint pain, stiffness, and swelling, especially in the fingers and toes. It may also cause skin changes, such as red, scaly patches.

Epidemiology:

Psoriatic arthritis affects people with psoriasis, a skin condition characterized by red, scaly patches. It usually develops between the ages of 30 and 50, but can occur at any age.

Pathology:

Psoriatic arthritis is an autoimmune disease that causes inflammation in the joints and skin. The exact cause is unknown, but genetics and environmental factors may play a role.

Treatment:

Treatment for psoriatic arthritis aims to control symptoms and prevent joint damage. This may include nonsteroidal anti-inflammatory drugs (NSAIDs), disease-modifying antirheumatic drugs (DMARDs), biologic therapies, and physical therapy.

Imaging Features:

  • Joint erosions and bone proliferation on X-ray
  • Sausage-like swelling of fingers or toes (dactylitis)

Differential Diagnosis:

  • Rheumatoid arthritis
  • Reactive arthritis
  • Ankylosing spondylitis
  • Gout
