Scheuermann Disease

Symptoms:

Scheuermann disease typically presents in adolescents with back pain, which may be aggravated by physical activity and is often associated with stiffness and deformity of the spine.

Epidemiology:

Scheuermann disease is a common cause of kyphosis in adolescents, with a prevalence of around 0.4% to 10% in the general population.

Pathology:

Scheuermann disease is characterized by wedging of vertebral bodies, especially in the thoracic spine, resulting in kyphosis.

Treatment:

Treatment for Scheuermann disease may include physical therapy, bracing, and in severe cases, surgery to correct the spinal deformity.

Imaging Features:

  • Anterior wedging of vertebral bodies on lateral radiographs
  • Irregularities of the vertebral endplates
  • Increased thoracic kyphosis on lateral radiographs

Differential Diagnosis:

  • Postural kyphosis
  • Ankylosing spondylitis
  • Spinal tumor
