Charcot Joint

Symptoms:

Charcot joint, also known as neuropathic arthropathy, is characterized by progressive destruction of a joint, leading to deformity, instability, and loss of function.

Epidemiology:

Charcot joint most commonly affects the feet and ankles, particularly in people with diabetes and peripheral neuropathy.

Pathology:

The exact cause of Charcot joint is not fully understood, but it is believed to involve repetitive trauma to a neuropathic joint, leading to bone and joint destruction.

Treatment:

Treatment for Charcot joint aims to reduce inflammation, stabilize the joint, and prevent further damage. This may include immobilization, orthotic devices, and surgical intervention in severe cases.

Imaging Features:

  • Joint destruction and fragmentation on X-ray
  • Soft tissue swelling and joint effusion on MRI

Differential Diagnosis:

  • Septic arthritis
  • Osteomyelitis
  • Rheumatoid arthritis
