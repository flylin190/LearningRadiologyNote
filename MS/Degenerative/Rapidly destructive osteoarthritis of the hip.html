Rapidly Destructive Osteoarthritis of Hip

Symptoms:

Symptoms of rapidly destructive osteoarthritis of the hip include sudden onset of severe hip pain, limited range of motion, and rapid progression of disability within a few months.

Epidemiology:

Rapidly destructive osteoarthritis of the hip is a rare condition, typically affecting elderly patients, particularly those with underlying conditions like rheumatoid arthritis, diabetes, or osteoporosis.

Pathology:

The exact pathology is unknown, but it is believed to involve a rapid and severe inflammatory process that leads to rapid cartilage and bone destruction in the hip joint.

Treatment:

Treatment options include pain management with medications, physical therapy, and ultimately, total hip replacement surgery in advanced cases.

Imaging Features:

  • Rapid joint space narrowing and bone destruction on X-ray
  • Bone marrow edema and subchondral cystic changes on MRI
  • Rapid progression of changes over a few months

Differential Diagnosis:

  • Septic arthritis
  • Rapidly progressive osteoarthritis
  • Transient osteoporosis of the hip
