Codfish Vertebra

Symptoms:

Codfish vertebrae typically do not cause symptoms on their own. They are often an incidental finding on imaging studies.

Epidemiology:

Codfish vertebrae can occur in individuals of any age and are more commonly seen in certain conditions such as Paget's disease of the bone or osteoporosis.

Pathology:

Codfish vertebrae are characterized by multiple compression fractures of the vertebral bodies, giving them a characteristic appearance resembling the bones of a codfish.

Treatment:

Treatment for codfish vertebrae depends on the underlying cause. In cases where they are due to osteoporosis, treatment may include calcium and vitamin D supplementation, bisphosphonates, and lifestyle modifications.

Imaging Features:

  • Multiple vertebral compression fractures
  • Vertebral bodies with a concave anterior surface, giving a fish-like appearance

Differential Diagnosis:

  • Vertebral compression fractures due to osteoporosis
  • Vertebral hemangioma
  • Osteomalacia
  • Renal osteodystrophy
  • Anemic disease
  • Homocystiuria
