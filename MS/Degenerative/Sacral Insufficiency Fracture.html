Sacral Insufficiency Fracture

Symptoms:

Sacral insufficiency fractures can cause lower back pain that worsens with standing or walking. Pain may also radiate to the buttocks or thighs.

Epidemiology:

Sacral insufficiency fractures are more common in elderly individuals and those with conditions that weaken the bones, such as osteoporosis or long-term corticosteroid use.

Pathology:

Sacral insufficiency fractures occur due to repetitive stress on weakened sacral bones, leading to a fracture along the sacrum.

Treatment:

Treatment for sacral insufficiency fractures may include pain management, rest, physical therapy, and in some cases, surgical intervention.

Imaging Features:

  • Linear or curvilinear fracture line through the sacrum
  • Bone marrow edema on MRI

Differential Diagnosis:

  • Metastatic disease to the sacrum
  • Sacroiliitis
  • Other causes of lower back pain
