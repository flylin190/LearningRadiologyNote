Müller-Weiss Syndrome

Symptoms:

Müller-Weiss syndrome is characterized by chronic foot pain, particularly in the midfoot area. Other symptoms may include swelling, stiffness, and difficulty walking.

Epidemiology:

Müller-Weiss syndrome is a rare condition that typically affects middle-aged women. The exact cause is unknown, but it is thought to be related to abnormal foot anatomy and biomechanics.

Pathology:

Müller-Weiss syndrome is believed to result from a collapse of the navicular bone in the midfoot, leading to progressive degeneration of the surrounding joints and tissues.

Treatment:

Treatment for Müller-Weiss syndrome aims to relieve pain and improve foot function. This may include orthotic devices, physical therapy, pain medications, and in severe cases, surgery to correct the foot deformity.

Imaging Features:

  • X-ray may show collapse of the navicular bone and degenerative changes in the midfoot joints

Differential Diagnosis

  • Plantar fasciitis
  • Tarsal tunnel syndrome
  • Arthritis of the midfoot joints
