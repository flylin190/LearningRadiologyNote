Glomangioma(Glomus Tumor)

Symptoms:

Glomangioma is typically asymptomatic but may present with pain or tenderness if it compresses surrounding structures.

Epidemiology:

Glomangioma is a rare tumor that can occur at any age. It is more common in women than men.

Pathology:

Glomangioma is a benign tumor derived from glomus bodies, which are specialized arteriovenous shunts involved in thermoregulation.

Treatment:

Treatment for glomangioma usually involves surgical excision of the tumor.

Imaging Features:

  • Well-defined soft tissue mass on ultrasound or MRI
  • Enhancement on contrast-enhanced MRI
  • May be hypervascular on angiography

Differential Diagnosis

  • Other soft tissue tumors
  • Vascular malformations
  • Neurogenic tumors
