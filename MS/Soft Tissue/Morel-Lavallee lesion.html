Morel-Lavallée Lesion

Symptoms:

Morel-Lavallée lesions typically present as a fluctuant, tender, soft tissue mass. They are commonly found over the greater trochanter, thigh, or knee, and may be associated with overlying skin changes such as ecchymosis or erythema.

Epidemiology:

Morel-Lavallée lesions are often the result of trauma, such as a blunt impact or shearing force, and are more common in active individuals, such as athletes.

Pathology:

A Morel-Lavallée lesion is a closed degloving soft tissue injury that results from shearing forces that disrupt the blood supply between the subcutaneous tissue and the underlying fascia.

Treatment:

Treatment for Morel-Lavallée lesions may include drainage of the fluid collection, compression therapy, and in some cases, surgical intervention to remove the lesion.

Imaging Features:

  • Fluid collection between subcutaneous tissue and fascia on ultrasound or MRI
  • Hyperintense signal on T1-weighted MRI

Differential Diagnosis:

  • Hematoma
  • Seroma
  • Soft tissue abscess
