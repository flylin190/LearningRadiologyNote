Lipoma Arborescens

Symptoms:

Lipoma arborescens can cause joint pain, swelling, and restricted range of motion, typically affecting the knee or other large joints.

Epidemiology:

Lipoma arborescens is a rare condition, with an estimated prevalence of around 1 in 100,000 individuals.

Pathology:

Lipoma arborescens is characterized by the proliferation of synovial villi, which become infiltrated with mature adipocytes, leading to a frond-like appearance.

Treatment:

Treatment for lipoma arborescens may involve anti-inflammatory medications, synovial fluid aspiration, or surgical debridement or synovectomy in severe cases.

Imaging Features:

  • Frond-like or villous synovial proliferation within the joint
  • Joint effusion and synovial thickening
  • Signal characteristics similar to fat on MRI

Differential Diagnosis

  • Pigmented villonodular synovitis
  • Synovial chondromatosis
  • Rheumatoid arthritis
  • Synovial sarcoma
