Basal Cell Nevus Syndrome

Symptoms:

Basal Cell Nevus Syndrome, also known as Gorlin Syndrome, is characterized by multiple basal cell carcinomas of the skin, jaw cysts, and skeletal abnormalities.

Epidemiology:

Basal Cell Nevus Syndrome is rare, with an estimated prevalence of 1 in 50,000 individuals.

Pathology:

The syndrome is caused by mutations in the PTCH1 gene, which is involved in the Hedgehog signaling pathway.

Treatment:

Treatment may involve surgical removal of basal cell carcinomas, dental management of jaw cysts, and monitoring for skeletal abnormalities.

Imaging Features:

  • Multiple basal cell carcinomas on skin examination
  • Jaw cysts on dental imaging
  • Skeletal abnormalities on X-ray

Differential Diagnosis:

  • Other syndromes with multiple basal cell carcinomas
  • Other causes of jaw cysts and skeletal abnormalities
