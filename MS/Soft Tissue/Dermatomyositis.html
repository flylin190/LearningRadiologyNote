Dermatomyositis

Symptoms:

Dermatomyositis presents with muscle weakness, skin rash (Gottron's papules, heliotrope rash), joint pain, and fatigue.

Epidemiology:

Dermatomyositis can affect individuals of all ages, but it is most common in children and adults in their 40s to 60s.

Pathology:

Dermatomyositis is an autoimmune disease characterized by inflammation of the muscles and skin. It is thought to involve both genetic and environmental factors.

Treatment:

Treatment usually involves medications to suppress the immune system, such as corticosteroids, immunosuppressants, and biologic agents.

Imaging Features:

  • Muscle edema and inflammation on MRI
  • dystrophic calcification in muscles and soft tissues (calcinosis universalis)

Differential Diagnosis:

  • Polymyositis
  • Lupus
  • Sarcoidosis
