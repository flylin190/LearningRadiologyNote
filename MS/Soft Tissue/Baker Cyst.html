Baker Cyst

Symptoms:

A Baker cyst may be asymptomatic or cause symptoms such as swelling and stiffness behind the knee, pain, and discomfort when bending the knee.

Epidemiology:

Baker cysts are relatively common, occurring in about 5% of the general population. They are more common in individuals with underlying conditions like osteoarthritis, rheumatoid arthritis, or knee injuries.

Pathology:

A Baker cyst is a fluid-filled swelling that forms behind the knee joint, due to protrusion or herniation of the synovial membrane through the posterior capsule of the knee.

Treatment:

Treatment options for Baker cysts include conservative management with physical therapy, anti-inflammatory medications, and aspiration or injection of the cyst with steroids. In some cases, surgical removal of the cyst may be necessary.

Imaging Features:

  • Fluid-filled, well-defined mass in the popliteal fossa (behind the knee) on ultrasound or MRI
  • May communicate with the knee joint on MRI
  • Associated joint effusion or synovitis may be present

Differential Diagnosis:

  • Popliteal artery aneurysm
  • Popliteal cyst (non-communicating with joint)
  • Soft tissue mass or tumor
