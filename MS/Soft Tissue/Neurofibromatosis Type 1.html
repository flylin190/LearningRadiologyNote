Neurofibromatosis Type 1

Symptoms:

Neurofibromatosis type 1 (NF1) is characterized by multiple neurofibromas, cafe-au-lait spots, Lisch nodules, and skeletal abnormalities.

Epidemiology:

NF1 is a common autosomal dominant disorder affecting approximately 1 in 3000 individuals worldwide.

Pathology:

NF1 is caused by mutations in the NF1 gene, which encodes neurofibromin, a protein involved in the regulation of cell growth and division.

Treatment:

Treatment for NF1 focuses on managing symptoms and complications. Surgery may be needed to remove neurofibromas that are causing pain or disfigurement.

Imaging Features:

  • Multiple neurofibromas on MRI
  • Cafe-au-lait spots on skin examination

Differential Diagnosis:

  • Schwannomatosis
  • Multiple endocrine neoplasia type 2B (MEN2B)
  • Von Recklinghausen disease of the bone
