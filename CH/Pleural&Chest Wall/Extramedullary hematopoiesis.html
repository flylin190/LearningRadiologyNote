Extramedullary Hematopoiesis

Symptoms:

Extramedullary hematopoiesis is often asymptomatic, but it can cause symptoms depending on the location and size of the hematopoietic masses, such as abdominal pain, back pain, or neurological deficits.

Epidemiology:

Extramedullary hematopoiesis is most commonly seen in conditions that cause chronic anemia or other hematological disorders, such as thalassemia, sickle cell disease, and myelofibrosis.

Pathology:

Extramedullary hematopoiesis is the production of blood cells outside the bone marrow, typically in response to chronic anemia or other hematological disorders.

Treatment:

Treatment is typically aimed at managing the underlying condition causing the extramedullary hematopoiesis. In some cases, surgical intervention may be required to remove large or symptomatic hematopoietic masses.

Imaging Features:

  • Soft tissue masses with characteristic "salt and pepper" appearance on CT or MRI
  • Potential involvement of various locations, such as the liver, spleen, lymph nodes, or paraspinal regions

Differential Diagnosis

  • Metastatic disease
  • Lymphoma
  • Abscess
  • Hematoma
