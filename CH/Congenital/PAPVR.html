Partial Anomaly of Pulmonary Venous Return (PAPVR)

Symptoms:

PAPVR may be asymptomatic or present with symptoms such as shortness of breath, fatigue, and respiratory infections.

Epidemiology:

PAPVR is a rare congenital heart defect that occurs when one or more pulmonary veins drain into the right atrium or its tributaries, instead of the left atrium.

Pathology:

The abnormal drainage of pulmonary veins results in a left-to-right shunt, which can lead to increased blood volume in the right atrium and right ventricle.

Treatment:

Treatment depends on the severity of the condition and may include medications to manage symptoms, surgery to repair the defect, or catheter-based interventions.

Imaging Features:

  • Chest X-ray may show enlarged right heart border and increased pulmonary vascular markings
  • Echocardiography can visualize the anomalous connection of pulmonary veins

Differential Diagnosis

  • Other congenital heart defects with right-to-left shunting
  • Pulmonary arterial hypertension
