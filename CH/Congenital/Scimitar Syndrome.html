Scimitar Syndrome

Symptoms:

Symptoms of scimitar syndrome may include shortness of breath, exercise intolerance, recurrent respiratory infections, and cardiac complications such as heart failure or pulmonary hypertension.

Epidemiology:

Scimitar syndrome is a rare congenital anomaly, affecting approximately 1 in 100,000 to 300,000 live births.

Pathology:

Scimitar syndrome is characterized by an anomalous pulmonary venous drainage of the right lung into the inferior vena cava, along with other associated anomalies, such as hypoplasia of the right lung and dextroposition of the heart.

Treatment:

Treatment for scimitar syndrome depends on the severity of symptoms and may involve surgical repair of the anomalous venous drainage, management of associated complications, or, in severe cases, lung transplantation.

Imaging Features:

  • Anomalous pulmonary venous drainage into the inferior vena cava
  • Hypoplasia of the right lung
  • Dextroposition of the heart

Differential Diagnosis

  • Pulmonary sequestration
  • Partial anomalous pulmonary venous return
  • Hypogenetic lung syndrome
  • Pulmonary hypoplasia
