Pulmonary Hamartoma

Symptoms:

Most pulmonary hamartomas are asymptomatic and are often discovered incidentally on imaging studies. However, larger lesions may cause symptoms such as cough, chest pain, or hemoptysis.

Epidemiology:

Pulmonary hamartomas are relatively common benign lung tumors, accounting for approximately 7-14% of all solitary pulmonary nodules.

Pathology:

A pulmonary hamartoma is a benign tumor-like malformation composed of disorganized respiratory epithelium, cartilage, and connective tissue elements.

Treatment:

Treatment for pulmonary hamartomas typically involves surgical resection, particularly for larger or symptomatic lesions, to establish a definitive diagnosis and exclude malignancy.

Imaging Features:

  • Well-circumscribed, smooth or lobulated lung mass
  • Popcorn calcification or fat densities within the lesion
  • May show air bronchograms or obtuse angles with the pleura

Differential Diagnosis

  • Lung cancer (e.g., adenocarcinoma, squamous cell carcinoma)
  • Pulmonary metastasis
  • Pulmonary chondroid lesions
  • Pulmonary sclerosing hemangioma
