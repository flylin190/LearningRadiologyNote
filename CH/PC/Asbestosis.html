Asbestosis

Symptoms:

Asbestosis symptoms include shortness of breath, a persistent cough, chest pain, and clubbing of the fingers or toes.

Epidemiology:

Asbestosis is caused by inhaling asbestos fibers and is most commonly seen in individuals with a history of occupational asbestos exposure.

Pathology:

Asbestos fibers cause inflammation and scarring in the lungs, leading to the symptoms of asbestosis.

Treatment:

There is no cure for asbestosis, but treatment focuses on relieving symptoms and preventing complications. This may include oxygen therapy, pulmonary rehabilitation, and in severe cases, lung transplantation.

Imaging Features:

  • Reticular or reticulonodular opacities on chest X-ray or CT scan
  • Pleural plaques

Differential Diagnosis

  • Idiopathic pulmonary fibrosis
  • Chronic hypersensitivity pneumonitis
  • Diffuse malignant mesothelioma
