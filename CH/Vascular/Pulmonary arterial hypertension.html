Pulmonary Hypertension

Symptoms:

Common symptoms of pulmonary hypertension include shortness of breath, fatigue, chest pain, dizziness, and syncope.

Epidemiology:

Pulmonary hypertension is a rare condition, with an estimated prevalence of 15-50 cases per million adults. It can occur at any age but is more common in individuals between 20 and 60 years old.

Pathology:

Pulmonary hypertension is characterized by elevated blood pressure in the pulmonary arteries, which can lead to right ventricular strain and ultimately right heart failure. It can be idiopathic or associated with underlying conditions such as lung diseases, heart diseases, or connective tissue disorders.

Treatment:

Treatment options for pulmonary hypertension include oxygen therapy, medications (e.g., vasodilators, endothelin receptor antagonists, phosphodiesterase inhibitors), and in severe cases, lung transplantation.

Imaging Features:

  • Enlarged pulmonary arteries
  • Right ventricular hypertrophy and dilation
  • Potential mosaic attenuation pattern on CT

Differential Diagnosis:

  • Chronic obstructive pulmonary disease (COPD)
  • Pulmonary fibrosis
  • Congestive heart failure
