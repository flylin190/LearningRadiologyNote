Pulmonary Arteriovenous Malformation (AVM)

Symptoms:

Pulmonary AVM may present with symptoms such as dyspnea (shortness of breath), cyanosis (bluish discoloration of the skin), clubbing of fingers, and migraine headaches (due to paradoxical emboli).

Epidemiology:

Pulmonary AVMs are rare and can occur sporadically or as part of hereditary hemorrhagic telangiectasia (HHT), also known as Osler-Weber-Rendu syndrome.

Pathology:

Pulmonary AVM is an abnormal connection between pulmonary arteries and veins, bypassing the capillary bed, which can lead to right-to-left shunting of blood.

Treatment:

Treatment for pulmonary AVM may include embolization of the abnormal vessels to prevent shunting and reduce the risk of complications such as stroke or brain abscess.

Imaging Features:

  • Contrast-enhanced CT or MRI can show the abnormal connection between pulmonary arteries and veins
  • Pulmonary angiography is the gold standard for diagnosis

Differential Diagnosis:

  • Aorto-pulmonary fistula
  • Pulmonary embolism
  • Pulmonary vasculitis
