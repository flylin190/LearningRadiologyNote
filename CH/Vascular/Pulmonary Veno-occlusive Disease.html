Pulmonary Veno-occlusive Disease

Symptoms:

Symptoms of pulmonary veno-occlusive disease include shortness of breath, fatigue, cough, and eventually, right-sided heart failure.

Epidemiology:

Pulmonary veno-occlusive disease is a rare condition, with an estimated incidence of less than 1 per million population. It can occur at any age but is more common in children and young adults.

Pathology:

Pulmonary veno-occlusive disease is characterized by progressive obstruction and remodeling of the small pulmonary veins, leading to increased pulmonary vascular resistance and eventual right-sided heart failure.

Treatment:

Treatment options for pulmonary veno-occlusive disease are limited. Supportive measures, such as oxygen therapy and diuretics, may provide symptomatic relief. Lung transplantation is the only potentially curative option for eligible patients.

Imaging Features:

  • Ground-glass opacities and septal lines on CT scan
  • Enlarged pulmonary arteries and right ventricle on CT or MRI
  • Pleural and pericardial effusions in advanced cases

Differential Diagnosis:

  • Pulmonary arterial hypertension
  • Interstitial lung disease
  • Pulmonary edema
