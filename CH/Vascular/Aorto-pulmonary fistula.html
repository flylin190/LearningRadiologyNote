Aorto-pulmonary Fistula

Symptoms:

Aorto-pulmonary fistula may present with symptoms such as dyspnea (shortness of breath), palpitations, chest pain, and hemoptysis (coughing up blood).

Epidemiology:

Aorto-pulmonary fistula is a rare condition that can occur as a complication of thoracic aortic aneurysm repair, aortic dissection, or trauma.

Pathology:

Aorto-pulmonary fistula is an abnormal connection between the aorta and the pulmonary artery, leading to abnormal blood flow between these two vessels.

Treatment:

Treatment for aorto-pulmonary fistula may include surgical repair to close the abnormal connection and restore normal blood flow.

Imaging Features:

  • Contrast-enhanced CT or MRI can show the abnormal connection between the aorta and pulmonary artery
  • Pulmonary artery angiography can also be used for diagnosis

Differential Diagnosis:

  • Pulmonary arteriovenous malformation (AVM)
  • Pulmonary embolism
  • Aortic dissection
