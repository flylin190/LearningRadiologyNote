Pulmonary Embolism

Symptoms:

Common symptoms of pulmonary embolism include shortness of breath, chest pain, cough, and hemoptysis. In severe cases, it can lead to shock and cardiac arrest.

Epidemiology:

Pulmonary embolism is a relatively common condition, with an estimated incidence of 1-2 cases per 1,000 individuals per year. Risk factors include immobilization, surgery, cancer, obesity, and certain inherited conditions.

Pathology:

Pulmonary embolism occurs when a blood clot (thrombus) travels from another part of the body, typically the deep veins of the legs or pelvis, and lodges in the pulmonary arterial system, obstructing blood flow to the lungs.

Treatment:

Treatment for pulmonary embolism typically involves anticoagulation therapy to prevent further clot formation and promote clot resolution. In severe cases, thrombolytic therapy or surgical embolectomy may be necessary.

Imaging Features:

  • Filling defect in the pulmonary arteries
  • Wedge-shaped peripheral opacity (infarct)
  • Potential right heart strain or dilation

Differential Diagnosis:

  • Pneumonia
  • Lung cancer
  • Pulmonary hypertension
