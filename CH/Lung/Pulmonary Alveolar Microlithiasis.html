Pulmonary Alveolar Microlithiasis

Symptoms:

Pulmonary Alveolar Microlithiasis is usually asymptomatic in the early stages. Symptoms, when present, may include shortness of breath, cough, chest pain, and fatigue.

Epidemiology:

Pulmonary Alveolar Microlithiasis is a rare lung disease that typically affects young adults. It is more common in certain populations, such as those of Turkish, Italian, or Japanese descent.

Pathology:

Pulmonary Alveolar Microlithiasis is characterized by the formation and accumulation of microliths (small stones) in the alveoli (air sacs) of the lungs. The exact cause is unknown, but it is thought to be a genetic disorder.

Treatment:

There is no specific treatment for Pulmonary Alveolar Microlithiasis. Treatment focuses on managing symptoms and providing supportive care.

Imaging Features:

  • Diffuse, sandstorm-like micronodular opacities on chest X-ray
  • Calcified micronodules throughout both lungs on CT scan

Differential Diagnosis

  • Pulmonary tuberculosis
  • Pulmonary alveolar proteinosis
  • Pulmonary fibrosis
