Pulmonary Sarcoidosis

Symptoms:

Pulmonary sarcoidosis is a disease that causes inflammation in the lungs. Symptoms may include cough, shortness of breath, chest pain, and fatigue.

Epidemiology:

Sarcoidosis can affect people of all ages and races, but it is most common in adults between 20 and 40 years old. The cause is unknown.

Pathology:

Sarcoidosis is characterized by the formation of granulomas, which are small clumps of cells that form in the affected organs, including the lungs.

Treatment:

Treatment may include corticosteroids to reduce inflammation, immunosuppressive drugs, and medications to relieve symptoms.

Imaging Features:

  • Bilateral hilar lymphadenopathy on chest X-ray
  • Ground-glass opacities or nodules on chest CT scan
