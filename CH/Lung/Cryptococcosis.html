Pulmonary Cryptococcosis

Symptoms:

Symptoms of pulmonary cryptococcosis may include cough, fever, chest pain, and shortness of breath, although some cases can be asymptomatic.

Epidemiology:

Pulmonary cryptococcosis is caused by the fungus Cryptococcus neoformans and primarily affects immunocompromised individuals, such as those with HIV/AIDS or receiving immunosuppressive therapy.

Pathology:

Pulmonary cryptococcosis occurs when the cryptococcal fungus is inhaled and causes an infection in the lungs, which can disseminate to other organs, particularly the central nervous system.

Treatment:

Treatment for pulmonary cryptococcosis typically involves antifungal medications, such as amphotericin B and fluconazole, depending on the severity and extent of the infection.

Imaging Features:

  • Nodules or masses, often with cavitation
  • Consolidation or ground-glass opacities
  • Pleural effusion or thickening

Differential Diagnosis

  • Tuberculosis
  • Lung cancer
  • Other fungal infections (e.g., histoplasmosis)
  • Bacterial pneumonia
