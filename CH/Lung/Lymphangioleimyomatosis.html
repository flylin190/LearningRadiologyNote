Lymphangioleimyomatosis

Symptoms:

Lymphangioleimyomatosis (LAM) commonly presents with dyspnea, cough, and chest pain. It can also lead to pneumothorax and chylous effusions.

Epidemiology:

LAM primarily affects women of childbearing age. It is often associated with tuberous sclerosis complex (TSC), but sporadic cases also occur.

Pathology:

LAM is characterized by the proliferation of abnormal smooth muscle-like cells (LAM cells) in the lungs, lymphatics, and kidneys. These cells form cystic lesions and cause tissue destruction.

Treatment:

Treatment for LAM focuses on symptom management and may include bronchodilators, oxygen therapy, and, in some cases, lung transplantation.

Imaging Features:

  • Multiple thin-walled cysts in the lungs on CT scan
  • Chylous effusions may be seen on imaging
  • Pneumothorax may be present

Differential Diagnosis:

  • Lymphocytic interstitial pneumonia (LIP)
  • Lymphoma involving the lungs
  • Lymphatic spread of malignancy
