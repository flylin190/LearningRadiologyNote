Granulomatosis with Polyangiitis

Symptoms:

Symptoms of granulomatosis with polyangiitis may include upper and lower respiratory tract involvement (e.g., sinusitis, pulmonary nodules), renal involvement (e.g., glomerulonephritis), and systemic manifestations such as fever, fatigue, and arthralgia.

Epidemiology:

Granulomatosis with polyangiitis is a rare systemic vasculitis, with an estimated annual incidence of 3-14 cases per million population.

Pathology:

Granulomatosis with polyangiitis is an autoimmune disorder characterized by necrotizing granulomatous inflammation and vasculitis, primarily affecting small and medium-sized vessels.

Treatment:

Treatment for granulomatosis with polyangiitis typically involves immunosuppressive medications, such as corticosteroids and cyclophosphamide, to control the inflammatory process and prevent organ damage.

Imaging Features:

  • Pulmonary nodules or cavities
  • Sinus and nasal involvement
  • Renal infarcts or masses

Differential Diagnosis

  • Microscopic polyangiitis
  • Eosinophilic granulomatosis with polyangiitis (Churg-Strauss)
  • Tuberculosis
  • Lung cancer
