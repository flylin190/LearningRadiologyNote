Pulmonary Aspergillosis

Symptoms:

Symptoms of pulmonary aspergillosis may include cough, fever, hemoptysis, and shortness of breath, depending on the form and severity of the infection.

Epidemiology:

Pulmonary aspergillosis is caused by fungi of the Aspergillus species and primarily affects individuals with underlying lung diseases or immunocompromised states.

Pathology:

Aspergillus spores can cause various forms of pulmonary disease, including allergic bronchopulmonary aspergillosis, aspergilloma, invasive aspergillosis, and chronic pulmonary aspergillosis.

Treatment:

Treatment for pulmonary aspergillosis depends on the form and severity, but may involve antifungal medications, surgery, or supportive care.

Imaging Features:

  • Nodules, cavities, or consolidations
  • Aspergilloma (fungal ball) within a pre-existing cavity
  • Pleural effusion or thickening

Differential Diagnosis

  • Tuberculosis
  • Lung cancer
  • Other fungal infections (e.g., coccidioidomycosis)
  • Bacterial pneumonia
