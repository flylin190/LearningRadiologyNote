Re-expansion Pulmonary Edema

Symptoms:

Symptoms of re-expansion pulmonary edema include shortness of breath, cough, chest pain, and hypoxemia (low blood oxygen levels).

Epidemiology:

Re-expansion pulmonary edema is a rare complication that can occur after rapid re-expansion of a collapsed lung, typically after thoracentesis (removal of pleural fluid) or chest tube placement.

Pathology:

Re-expansion pulmonary edema occurs due to a sudden increase in negative intrathoracic pressure after lung re-expansion, leading to increased blood flow to the re-expanded lung and leakage of fluid into the alveolar spaces.

Treatment:

Treatment involves supplemental oxygen, positive pressure ventilation, and diuretics to manage the pulmonary edema. In severe cases, intubation and mechanical ventilation may be required.

Imaging Features:

  • Diffuse or unilateral pulmonary opacities on chest X-ray or CT scan
  • Pleural effusion may be present

Differential Diagnosis:

  • Pneumonia
  • Congestive heart failure
  • Acute respiratory distress syndrome (ARDS)
