Lung atelectasis

Symptoms:

Lung atelectasis can be asymptomatic or present with symptoms such as shortness of breath, chest pain, and cough.

Epidemiology:

Lung atelectasis can occur at any age and is more common in individuals who are immobile or have underlying lung conditions.

Pathology:

Lung atelectasis is the collapse of lung tissue, leading to reduced gas exchange and lung function.

Treatment:

Treatment for lung atelectasis depends on the underlying cause and may include chest physiotherapy, bronchodilators, and in severe cases, surgery.

Imaging Features:

  • Chest X-ray may show lung collapse with mediastinal shift towards the affected side
  • CT scan can provide detailed imaging of the collapsed lung segments

Differential Diagnosis

  • Pneumonia
  • Pleural effusion
  • Pulmonary embolism
  • Lung cancer
