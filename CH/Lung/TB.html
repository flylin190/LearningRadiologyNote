Pulmonary Tuberculosis

Symptoms:

Pulmonary tuberculosis (TB) is a bacterial infection caused by Mycobacterium tuberculosis. It primarily affects the lungs but can also affect other parts of the body. Symptoms include coughing up blood, chest pain, fatigue, weight loss, and night sweats.

Epidemiology:

TB is a global health problem, with the highest burden in developing countries. It is spread through the air when people with active TB cough or sneeze.

Pathology:

M. tuberculosis is an acid-fast bacillus that primarily infects the lungs. It can cause caseating granulomas and cavities in the lungs.

Treatment:

Standard treatment for TB involves a combination of antibiotics taken for 6 to 9 months. Treatment is essential to prevent the spread of TB and the development of drug-resistant strains.

Imaging Features:

  • Upper lobe cavitary lesions on chest X-ray
  • Tree-in-bud appearance on chest CT

Differential Diagnosis:

  • Fungal infections (e.g., histoplasmosis, coccidioidomycosis)
  • Non-tuberculous mycobacterial infections
  • Lung cancer
