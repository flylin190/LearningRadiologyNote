Pneumocytis Infection

Symptoms:

Pneumocystis infection can cause a variety of respiratory symptoms, including cough, fever, and shortness of breath. In severe cases, it can lead to respiratory failure.

Epidemiology:

Pneumocystis infection is caused by the fungus Pneumocystis jirovecii. It primarily affects individuals with weakened immune systems, such as those with HIV/AIDS, cancer, or taking immunosuppressive medications.

Pathology:

Pneumocystis jirovecii infects the lungs and causes inflammation and damage to the lung tissue, leading to the symptoms of pneumonia.

Treatment:

Treatment for Pneumocystis infection involves the use of antibiotics, such as trimethoprim-sulfamethoxazole (TMP-SMX). In severe cases, corticosteroids may also be used to reduce inflammation in the lungs.

Imaging Features:

  • Ground-glass opacities on chest X-ray or CT scan
  • Bilateral perihilar infiltrates
  • Occasional cystic changes in advanced disease

Differential Diagnosis

  • Bacterial pneumonia
  • Other fungal pneumonias
  • Non-infectious causes of lung infiltrates
