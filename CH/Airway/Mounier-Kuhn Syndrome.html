Mounier-Kuhn Syndrome

Symptoms:

Mounier-Kuhn syndrome, also known as tracheobronchomegaly, is a rare condition characterized by abnormal dilation of the trachea and main bronchi. It can lead to chronic respiratory symptoms such as cough, sputum production, and recurrent respiratory infections.

Epidemiology:

Mounier-Kuhn syndrome is a rare disease that typically presents in adulthood. It is more common in men than women.

Pathology:

The exact cause of Mounier-Kuhn syndrome is unknown. It is thought to be related to a weakness in the tracheal and bronchial walls, leading to their dilation.

Treatment:

Treatment for Mounier-Kuhn syndrome aims to manage symptoms and prevent complications. This may include bronchodilators, mucolytic agents, antibiotics for infections, and in severe cases, surgery to repair or support the weakened airways.

Imaging Features:

  • Dilated trachea and main bronchi on CT scan
  • Thin and atrophic tracheal and bronchial walls on CT scan

Differential Diagnosis:

  • Tracheobronchomalacia
  • Bronchiectasis
  • Chronic obstructive pulmonary disease (COPD)
