Carotid Body Paraganglioma

Symptoms:

Carotid body paraganglioma may present as a painless neck mass, cranial nerve palsies, and symptoms related to mass effect on adjacent structures.

Epidemiology:

Carotid body paraganglioma is a rare tumor that typically occurs in adults aged 40-60 years.

Pathology:

Carotid body paraganglioma arises from the carotid body, which is a chemoreceptor located at the bifurcation of the common carotid artery.

Treatment:

Treatment involves surgical resection of the tumor. Preoperative embolization may be performed to reduce vascularity and intraoperative bleeding.

Imaging Features:

  • Enhancing mass at the carotid bifurcation on CT or MRI
  • May show splaying of the internal and external carotid arteries

Differential Diagnosis

  • Carotid artery aneurysm
  • Carotid body tumor
  • Metastatic lymphadenopathy
