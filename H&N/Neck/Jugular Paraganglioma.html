Jugular Paraganglioma

Symptoms:

Jugular paraganglioma may present with a painless neck mass, hearing loss, tinnitus, and cranial nerve deficits.

Epidemiology:

Jugular paraganglioma is a rare tumor that typically occurs in adults aged 40-60 years.

Pathology:

Jugular paraganglioma arises from the paraganglionic tissue of the jugular bulb or along the glossopharyngeal nerve.

Treatment:

Treatment involves surgical resection of the tumor. Radiation therapy may be used in cases where complete resection is not possible.

Imaging Features:

  • Enhancing mass at the jugular foramen on CT or MRI
  • Typically hypervascular

Differential Diagnosis

  • Carotid body tumor
  • Metastatic lymphadenopathy
  • Branchial cleft cyst
