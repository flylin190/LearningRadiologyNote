Dentigerous Cyst

Symptoms:

Dentigerous cysts are often asymptomatic and are usually discovered during routine dental exams. Symptoms may include swelling, pain, or infection if the cyst becomes large or infected.

Epidemiology:

Dentigerous cysts are most commonly seen in young adults and are associated with impacted, unerupted teeth, most commonly the mandibular third molars.

Pathology:

Dentigerous cysts develop from the accumulation of fluid between the crown of an unerupted tooth and the surrounding dental follicle.

Treatment:

Treatment for dentigerous cysts typically involves surgical removal of the cyst and any associated impacted teeth.

Imaging Features:

  • Well-defined radiolucent lesion around the crown of an unerupted tooth on dental radiographs
  • May cause displacement of adjacent teeth

Differential Diagnosis:

  • Odontogenic keratocyst
  • Ameloblastoma
  • Calcifying odontogenic cyst
