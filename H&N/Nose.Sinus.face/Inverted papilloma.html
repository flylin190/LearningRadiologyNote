Inverted Papilloma

Symptoms:

Inverted papilloma often presents with unilateral nasal obstruction, epistaxis, and sinusitis symptoms. It may also be associated with anosmia and facial pain or pressure.

Epidemiology:

Inverted papilloma is a rare benign tumor that most commonly affects adults in their 5th to 7th decades of life. It is more common in males than females.

Pathology:

Inverted papilloma is characterized by its endophytic growth pattern, with epithelial invaginations into the underlying stroma. It is often associated with human papillomavirus (HPV) infection.

Treatment:

The main treatment for inverted papilloma is surgical resection. Endoscopic sinus surgery is often used to remove the tumor and affected mucosa. Recurrence rates are low, but regular follow-up is recommended.

Imaging Features:

  • Enhancing soft tissue mass in the sinonasal cavity on CT or MRI
  • Bone erosion or remodeling of the sinonasal structures
  • May be associated with air-fluid levels or sinus opacification

Differential Diagnosis:

  • Antrochoanal polyp
  • Benign sinonasal tumors (e.g., nasal polyps)
  • Malignant sinonasal tumors (e.g., squamous cell carcinoma)
