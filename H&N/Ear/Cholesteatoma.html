Cholesteatoma

Symptoms:

Cholesteatoma may cause hearing loss, ear pain, ear discharge, dizziness, and recurrent ear infections.

Epidemiology:

Cholesteatoma is more common in adults than in children and is typically associated with a history of repeated middle ear infections.

Pathology: (=Epidermoid Cyst)

Cholesteatoma is a benign growth of keratinizing squamous epithelium in the middle ear. It can erode surrounding bone and lead to complications such as mastoiditis and facial nerve paralysis.

Treatment:

Treatment usually involves surgery to remove the cholesteatoma and repair any damage to the ear structures.

Imaging Features:

  • Soft tissue mass in the middle ear on CT scan
  • Erosion of ossicles and surrounding bone

Differential Diagnosis:

  • Middle ear tumors
  • Chronic otitis media
