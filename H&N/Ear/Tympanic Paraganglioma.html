Tympanic Paragangliomas

Symptoms:

Tympanic paragangliomas are rare tumors that arise from the glomus tympanicum, a collection of cells near the middle ear. Symptoms may include hearing loss, tinnitus, and ear fullness.

Epidemiology:

Tympanic paragangliomas are rare and most commonly affect adults between 40 and 70 years old. The exact cause is unknown.

Pathology:

Tympanic paragangliomas arise from the paraganglionic tissue near the middle ear and are typically benign.

Treatment:

Treatment may include surgery to remove the tumor, radiation therapy, or embolization to shrink the tumor and reduce symptoms.

Imaging Features:

  • Soft tissue mass in the middle ear on CT or MRI
  • Bone erosion or remodeling of the ossicles on imaging

Differential Diagnosis:

  • Glomus jugulare tumors
  • Cholesteatoma
  • Ossicular chain pathology
