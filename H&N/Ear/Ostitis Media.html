Otitis Media

Symptoms:

Otitis media is an infection or inflammation of the middle ear. Symptoms may include ear pain, fever, irritability, and hearing loss.

Epidemiology:

Otitis media is common, especially in children. It can occur after a cold or upper respiratory infection.

Pathology:

Otitis media is often caused by bacteria or viruses that enter the middle ear through the eustachian tube.

Treatment:

Treatment may include antibiotics for bacterial infections, pain relievers, and eardrops. In some cases, a procedure called tympanostomy tube placement may be necessary.

Imaging Features:

  • Fluid in the middle ear on otoscopy or tympanometry

Differential Diagnosis:

  • Mastoiditis
  • Cholesteatoma
  • Otitis externa
