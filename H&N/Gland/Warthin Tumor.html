Warthin Tumor

Symptoms:

Warthin tumor is usually asymptomatic and is often discovered incidentally on imaging studies or physical examination. Rarely, it may cause pain or tenderness in the affected area.

Epidemiology:

Warthin tumor is more common in older adults, particularly in men. It is strongly associated with cigarette smoking.

Pathology:

Warthin tumor is a benign salivary gland tumor that typically occurs in the parotid gland. It is characterized by cystic spaces lined by epithelial and lymphoid cells.

Treatment:

Warthin tumor is usually treated with surgical removal. In most cases, the tumor is benign and does not recur after removal.

Imaging Features:

  • Well-circumscribed, cystic lesion on imaging
  • Enhancement of the cystic component with contrast
  • Typically located in the parotid gland

Differential Diagnosis:

  • Other salivary gland tumors
  • Lymphadenopathy
