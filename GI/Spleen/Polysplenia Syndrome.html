Polysplenia Syndrome

Symptoms:

Patients with polysplenia syndrome may have varying symptoms depending on the associated congenital abnormalities. Common symptoms include heart murmurs, cyanosis, respiratory distress, and gastrointestinal issues.

Epidemiology:

Polysplenia syndrome is a rare congenital disorder, occurring in approximately 1 in 250,000 live births.

Pathology:

Polysplenia syndrome is characterized by the presence of multiple spleniculi (small accessory spleens) instead of a single spleen. It is associated with various congenital malformations, including cardiac, gastrointestinal, and respiratory abnormalities.

Treatment:

Treatment for polysplenia syndrome depends on the specific congenital abnormalities present. It often involves surgical correction of cardiac or gastrointestinal malformations, as well as supportive care for respiratory issues.

Imaging Features:

  • Multiple spleniculi visible on imaging studies
  • Associated cardiac, gastrointestinal, and respiratory malformations

Differential Diagnosis:

  • Asplenia syndrome
  • Isolated congenital cardiovascular or gastrointestinal malformations
