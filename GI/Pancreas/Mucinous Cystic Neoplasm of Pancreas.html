Mucinous Cystic Neoplasm of Pancreas

Symptoms:

Mucinous cystic neoplasms of the pancreas are often asymptomatic, but larger lesions may cause abdominal pain, nausea, vomiting, or weight loss.

Epidemiology:

Mucinous cystic neoplasms account for around 2-5% of all pancreatic cystic lesions and predominantly affect middle-aged to elderly women.

Pathology:

Mucinous cystic neoplasms are mucin-producing cystic neoplasms with a potential for malignant transformation. They are characterized by the presence of ovarian-type stroma and can progress from mucinous cystadenoma to mucinous cystadenocarcinoma.

Treatment:

Treatment for mucinous cystic neoplasms typically involves surgical resection, as these lesions have a significant malignant potential.

Imaging Features:

  • Unilocular or multilocular cystic lesion
  • Possible presence of internal septations or mural nodules
  • Typically located in the body or tail of the pancreas

Differential Diagnosis:

  • Serous cystic neoplasm
  • Pseudocyst
  • Intraductal papillary mucinous neoplasm (IPMN)
