Solid Pseudopapillary Tumor of Pancreas

Symptoms:

Solid pseudopapillary tumors of the pancreas can be asymptomatic or cause abdominal pain, weight loss, and other non-specific symptoms.

Epidemiology:

Solid pseudopapillary tumors are rare, accounting for approximately 1-2% of all pancreatic tumors. They predominantly affect young women in their 20s and 30s.

Pathology:

Solid pseudopapillary tumors are low-grade malignant neoplasms with uncertain histogenesis. They are characterized by a solid, cystic, and pseudopapillary pattern, with a distinctive fibrovascular core and degenerated hemorrhagic areas.

Treatment:

The primary treatment for solid pseudopapillary tumors is surgical resection, which can be curative in most cases. Adjuvant therapy is generally not required due to the low malignant potential of these tumors.

Imaging Features:

  • Well-circumscribed, encapsulated mass
  • Solid and cystic components
  • Heterogeneous enhancement
  • Peripheral calcifications

Differential Diagnosis:

  • Pancreatic neuroendocrine tumors
  • Mucinous cystic neoplasms
  • Pseudocysts
