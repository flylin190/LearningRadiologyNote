Annular Pancreas

Symptoms:

Many patients with annular pancreas are asymptomatic. However, some may experience abdominal pain, nausea, vomiting, and weight loss due to duodenal obstruction or pancreatitis.

Epidemiology:

Annular pancreas is a rare congenital anomaly, occurring in approximately 1 in 20,000 to 1 in 40,000 live births.

Pathology:

Annular pancreas is a congenital anomaly in which a ring of pancreatic tissue surrounds the second portion of the duodenum, potentially causing duodenal obstruction or pancreatitis.

Treatment:

Treatment for annular pancreas depends on the severity of symptoms. In asymptomatic cases, no treatment may be required. In symptomatic cases, surgical bypass or resection of the obstructing pancreatic tissue may be necessary.

Imaging Features:

  • Pancreatic tissue encircling the second portion of the duodenum
  • Duodenal obstruction or dilated duodenal loop
  • Potential pancreatitis or pancreatic duct dilation

Differential Diagnosis:

  • Duodenal stenosis or atresia
  • Periampullary tumor
  • Duodenal duplication cyst
