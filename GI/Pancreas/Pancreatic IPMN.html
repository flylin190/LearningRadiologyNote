Pancreatic Intraductal Papillary Mucinous Neoplasm (IPMN)

Symptoms:

Pancreatic IPMN is often asymptomatic but can cause abdominal pain, jaundice, weight loss, and pancreatitis in some cases.

Epidemiology:

IPMN is more common in older individuals and is often discovered incidentally on imaging studies.

Pathology:

IPMN is characterized by the growth of mucin-producing cells in the pancreatic ducts, which can lead to the formation of cystic lesions.

Treatment:

Treatment depends on the size and location of the IPMN. Options may include surveillance, surgery to remove the affected part of the pancreas, or endoscopic procedures to drain the cysts.

Imaging Features:

  • Cystic lesions in the pancreas on CT or MRI
  • Dilation of the pancreatic duct
  • Presence of intraductal nodules or papillary projections

Differential Diagnosis:

  • Pancreatic pseudocyst
  • Pancreatic ductal adenocarcinoma
  • Serous cystadenoma
