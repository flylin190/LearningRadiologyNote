Serous Cystic Neoplasm of Pancreas

Symptoms:

Most serous cystic neoplasms are asymptomatic and are often discovered incidentally on imaging studies. Larger lesions may cause abdominal pain or discomfort.

Epidemiology:

Serous cystic neoplasms account for approximately 16-32% of all pancreatic cystic lesions and are more common in elderly women.

Pathology:

Serous cystic neoplasms are benign cystic lesions composed of glycogen-rich cuboidal epithelial cells that produce a watery, non-mucinous fluid. They are typically encapsulated and do not have malignant potential.

Treatment:

Most serous cystic neoplasms do not require treatment, as they are benign lesions. However, surgical resection may be considered for larger or symptomatic lesions.

Imaging Features:

  • Multilocular cystic lesion with a honeycomb or microcystic appearance
  • Well-defined, lobulated margins
  • Calcifications may be present within the cyst walls

Differential Diagnosis:

  • Mucinous cystic neoplasm
  • Intraductal papillary mucinous neoplasm (IPMN)
  • Pseudocyst
