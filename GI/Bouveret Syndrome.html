Bouveret Syndrome

Symptoms:

Bouveret syndrome is characterized by symptoms of gastric outlet obstruction, including nausea, vomiting, and abdominal pain. It can also lead to dehydration and electrolyte imbalances.

Epidemiology:

Bouveret syndrome is a rare complication of gallstone disease, typically occurring in older adults with a history of gallstones.

Pathology:

Bouveret syndrome occurs when a large gallstone erodes through the wall of the gallbladder and into the duodenum or stomach, causing obstruction.

Treatment:

Treatment for Bouveret syndrome may involve endoscopic removal of the gallstone or surgery to remove the gallbladder and repair the fistula between the gallbladder and the gastrointestinal tract.

Imaging Features:

  • Rigler's triad on abdominal X-ray (pneumobilia, ectopic gallstone, bowel obstruction)
  • Cholecystoduodenal fistula on imaging studies
  • Distended stomach with air-fluid levels

Differential Diagnosis

  • Gallstone ileus
  • Gastric outlet obstruction from other causes
  • Acute pancreatitis
