Gastric Volvulus

Symptoms:

Common symptoms of gastric volvulus include severe abdominal pain, nausea, vomiting, and difficulty swallowing.

Epidemiology:

Gastric volvulus is a rare condition that can occur at any age but is most common in older adults. It is more common in women than men.

Pathology:

Gastric volvulus occurs when the stomach twists on its own axis, leading to obstruction of the stomach and potentially cutting off its blood supply.

Treatment:

Treatment for gastric volvulus usually involves surgery to untwist the stomach and prevent it from twisting again.

Imaging Features:

  • Upside-down stomach sign on X-ray or CT scan
  • Distended stomach with air-fluid levels

Differential Diagnosis:

  • Acute gastric dilatation
  • Gastric outlet obstruction
  • Acute pancreatitis
