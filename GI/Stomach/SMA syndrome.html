SMA Syndrome

Symptoms:

Superior mesenteric artery (SMA) syndrome typically presents with abdominal pain, bloating, nausea, and vomiting. Patients may also experience weight loss due to poor nutritional intake.

Epidemiology:

SMA syndrome is a rare condition that usually affects individuals with significant weight loss or a history of surgery, trauma, or prolonged bed rest.

Pathology:

SMA syndrome is caused by compression of the duodenum between the superior mesenteric artery and the aorta, resulting in partial or complete obstruction of the duodenum.

Treatment:

Treatment for SMA syndrome aims to relieve the obstruction and improve nutritional intake. This may include dietary modifications, weight gain, and in severe cases, surgery to relieve the compression.

Imaging Features:

  • Dilated stomach and proximal duodenum on abdominal X-ray
  • Compression of the duodenum between the SMA and aorta on CT scan

Differential Diagnosis

  • Duodenal obstruction
  • Peptic ulcer disease
  • Acute pancreatitis
