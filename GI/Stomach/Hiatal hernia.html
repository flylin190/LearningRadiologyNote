Hiatal Hernia

Symptoms:

Hiatal hernia often presents with symptoms such as heartburn, chest pain, difficulty swallowing, and regurgitation of stomach acid into the esophagus.

Epidemiology:

Hiatal hernia is a common condition that can occur at any age, but it is more common in older adults. It is more common in women than men.

Pathology:

Hiatal hernia occurs when part of the stomach pushes up into the chest cavity through the diaphragm, which is the muscle that separates the chest cavity from the abdomen.

Treatment:

Treatment for hiatal hernia usually involves lifestyle changes to reduce symptoms, such as avoiding large meals, not lying down after eating, and losing weight if necessary. In severe cases, surgery may be necessary to repair the hernia.

Imaging Features:

  • Hiatal hernia may be visible on X-ray or CT scan
  • Barium swallow may show the herniated stomach

Differential Diagnosis:

  • Gastroesophageal reflux disease (GERD)
  • Peptic ulcer disease
  • Esophageal cancer
