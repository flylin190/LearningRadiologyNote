Rapunzel Syndrome

Symptoms:

Symptoms of Rapunzel syndrome may include abdominal pain, nausea, vomiting, weight loss, and in severe cases, bowel obstruction or perforation.

Epidemiology:

Rapunzel syndrome is a rare condition, primarily seen in individuals with psychiatric disorders or trichotillomania (hair-pulling disorder), and more commonly in young females.

Pathology:

Rapunzel syndrome is characterized by the formation of a trichobezoar (hairball) in the stomach, which may extend into the small intestine, forming a Rapunzel tail.

Treatment:

Treatment typically involves endoscopic or surgical removal of the trichobezoar. In severe cases of bowel obstruction or perforation, surgical intervention may be necessary. Psychiatric evaluation and treatment for underlying conditions, such as trichotillomania, are also important.

Imaging Features:

  • Well-defined intragastric mass with mottled gas pattern on CT (trichobezoar)
  • Extension of the mass into the small bowel (Rapunzel tail)
  • Bowel obstruction or perforation in advanced cases

Differential Diagnosis:

  • Other types of bezoars (phytobezoars, lactobezoars)
  • Gastric or intestinal masses
  • Bowel obstruction due to other causes
