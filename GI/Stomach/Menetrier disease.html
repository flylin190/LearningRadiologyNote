Menetrier Disease

Symptoms:

Menetrier disease is characterized by abdominal pain, nausea, vomiting, weight loss, and swelling of the abdomen.

Epidemiology:

Menetrier disease is a rare condition that usually affects adults. The exact cause is unknown.

Pathology:

Menetrier disease is characterized by giant gastric folds in the stomach, which are caused by an overgrowth of the mucous-secreting cells in the stomach lining.

Treatment:

Treatment for Menetrier disease focuses on relieving symptoms and may include medications to reduce stomach acid production and promote healing of the stomach lining.

Imaging Features:

  • Thickened gastric folds on barium swallow or CT scan
  • Enlarged stomach on imaging

Differential Diagnosis:

  • Gastric cancer
  • Gastric lymphoma
  • Gastric adenocarcinoma
