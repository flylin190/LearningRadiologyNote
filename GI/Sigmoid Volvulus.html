Simoid Volvulus

Symptoms:

Simoid volvulus presents with abdominal pain, distention, constipation, and sometimes, nausea and vomiting. In severe cases, it can lead to bowel obstruction and ischemia.

Epidemiology:

It is more common in elderly individuals and those with chronic constipation or neurological disorders affecting bowel motility.

Pathology:

Simoid volvulus occurs when the sigmoid colon twists on its mesentery, leading to obstruction of the bowel lumen and compromising blood flow.

Treatment:

Initial treatment includes endoscopic decompression, followed by elective sigmoid colon resection to prevent recurrence.

Imaging Features:

  • "Coffee bean" sign on abdominal X-ray
  • Whirl sign on CT scan
