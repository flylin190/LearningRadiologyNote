Hemosiderosis

Symptoms:

Common symptoms of hemosiderosis depend on the specific organs affected but may include fatigue, weakness, shortness of breath, and chest pain. In severe cases, it can lead to organ failure.

Epidemiology:

Hemosiderosis can occur at any age but is more common in adults. It is often associated with conditions such as chronic hemolytic anemia, chronic liver disease, and repeated blood transfusions.

Pathology:

Hemosiderosis is characterized by the accumulation of iron in tissues, particularly in the liver, spleen, and bone marrow. This can lead to tissue damage and organ dysfunction.

Treatment:

Treatment for hemosiderosis may include iron chelation therapy to remove excess iron from the body and manage underlying conditions that contribute to iron overload.

Imaging Features:

  • Increased signal intensity on T2-weighted MRI images due to iron deposition
  • Enlargement of affected organs, such as the liver or spleen, on imaging

Differential Diagnosis:

  • Hemochromatosis
  • Iron deficiency anemia
  • Thalassemia
