Hepatic Hydrothorax

Symptoms:

Hepatic hydrothorax presents with shortness of breath, cough, and chest discomfort. It is often associated with liver cirrhosis.

Epidemiology:

Hepatic hydrothorax is most commonly seen in patients with liver cirrhosis, especially those with decompensated cirrhosis.

Pathology:

Hepatic hydrothorax occurs due to the accumulation of fluid in the pleural cavity as a result of increased pressure in the portal vein system.

Treatment:

Treatment for hepatic hydrothorax may include diuretics, therapeutic thoracentesis, and in severe cases, placement of a chest tube to drain the fluid.

Imaging Features:

  • Pleural effusion on chest X-ray or CT scan, typically on the right side

Differential Diagnosis:

  • Other causes of pleural effusion (e.g., heart failure, pneumonia)
  • Pulmonary embolism
  • Pleural malignancy
