Boerhaave Syndrome

Symptoms:

The classic symptom of Boerhaave syndrome is sudden, severe chest pain often followed by vomiting and difficulty swallowing. Other symptoms may include shortness of breath, fever, and chest tenderness.

Epidemiology:

Boerhaave syndrome is rare, accounting for only about 15% of esophageal perforations. It most commonly occurs in middle-aged men, often after episodes of excessive food or alcohol consumption.

Pathology:

Boerhaave syndrome is caused by a sudden increase in intra-esophageal pressure, leading to rupture of the esophageal wall. This most commonly occurs after forceful vomiting or retching.

Treatment:

The main treatment for Boerhaave syndrome is surgical repair of the esophageal perforation. This is typically done through thoracotomy or thoracoscopy.

Imaging Features:

  • CT scan may show extraluminal air or contrast extravasation from the esophagus.
  • Esophagography may demonstrate contrast extravasation into the mediastinum or pleural space.

Differential Diagnosis:

  • Esophageal perforation due to other causes, such as trauma or instrumentation
  • Esophageal rupture secondary to malignancy
  • Spontaneous pneumomediastinum
