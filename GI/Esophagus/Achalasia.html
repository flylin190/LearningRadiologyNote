Achalasia

Symptoms:

Achalasia is a rare disorder that affects the esophagus and can cause difficulty swallowing, chest pain, regurgitation of food, and weight loss.

Epidemiology:

Achalasia can occur at any age, but it is most commonly diagnosed in adults between the ages of 25 and 60.

Pathology:

In achalasia, the muscles of the esophagus and the lower esophageal sphincter (LES) fail to relax properly, leading to difficulty swallowing and the accumulation of food in the esophagus.

Treatment:

Treatment for achalasia may include medications to relax the LES, pneumatic dilation (stretching) of the LES, botulinum toxin injection into the LES, or surgery to cut the muscle fibers of the LES.

Imaging Features:

  • Dilated esophagus on barium swallow study
  • Decreased esophageal peristalsis on esophageal manometry

Differential Diagnosis:

  • Gastroesophageal reflux disease (GERD)
  • Esophageal stricture
  • Esophageal cancer
