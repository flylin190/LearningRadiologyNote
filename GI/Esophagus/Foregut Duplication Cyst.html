Foregut Duplication Cyst

Symptoms:

Foregut duplication cysts are usually asymptomatic but can present with abdominal pain, distention, nausea, vomiting, and gastrointestinal bleeding if they become enlarged or infected.

Epidemiology:

Foregut duplication cysts are rare congenital anomalies that can occur anywhere along the gastrointestinal tract.

Pathology:

Foregut duplication cysts are believed to result from abnormal embryological development, leading to the formation of a cystic structure lined with gastrointestinal epithelium.

Treatment:

Treatment for foregut duplication cysts depends on the size and symptoms. Small, asymptomatic cysts may be monitored, while larger or symptomatic cysts may require surgical removal.

Imaging Features:

  • Fluid-filled cystic lesion on imaging studies, such as ultrasound, CT, or MRI
  • Communication with the gastrointestinal tract may be seen on contrast studies

Differential Diagnosis

  • Pancreatic pseudocyst
  • Gastrointestinal stromal tumor (GIST)
