Esophageal Leiomyoma

Symptoms:

Esophageal leiomyomas may cause dysphagia, odynophagia (painful swallowing), or upper gastrointestinal bleeding. However, some lesions may be asymptomatic and discovered incidentally.

Epidemiology:

Esophageal leiomyomas are relatively rare, accounting for approximately 2-5% of all esophageal neoplasms.

Pathology:

Esophageal leiomyomas are benign smooth muscle tumors arising from the muscularis propria layer of the esophagus. They are typically well-circumscribed and can grow intramurally or extramurally.

Treatment:

Treatment for esophageal leiomyomas typically involves surgical resection or enucleation, particularly for symptomatic or large lesions. Endoscopic resection or surveillance may be considered for small, asymptomatic lesions.

Imaging Features:

  • Well-circumscribed intramural or extramural mass lesion in the esophagus
  • Homogeneous enhancement on contrast-enhanced CT or MRI
  • Potential compression or displacement of the esophageal lumen

Differential Diagnosis:

  • Gastrointestinal stromal tumor (GIST)
  • Esophageal leiomyosarcoma
  • Esophageal duplication cyst
