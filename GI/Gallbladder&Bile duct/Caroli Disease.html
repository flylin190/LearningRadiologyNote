Caroli Disease

Symptoms:

Symptoms of Caroli disease may include abdominal pain, jaundice, cholangitis, and liver dysfunction.

Epidemiology:

Caroli disease is a rare congenital disorder, with an estimated prevalence of 1 in 1,000,000 individuals.

Pathology:

Caroli disease is characterized by non-obstructive, segmental dilation of the intrahepatic bile ducts, leading to the formation of saccular or cystic structures.

Treatment:

Treatment for Caroli disease may include antibiotics for cholangitis, endoscopic or surgical interventions for biliary drainage, and liver transplantation in severe cases.

Imaging Features:

  • Cystic or saccular dilation of intrahepatic bile ducts
  • Calculi or debris within dilated ducts
  • Dilation may be diffuse or localized

Differential Diagnosis

  • Polycystic liver disease
  • Choledochal cysts
  • Recurrent pyogenic cholangitis
  • Biliary hamartoma
