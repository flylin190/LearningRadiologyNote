Sclerosing Cholangitis

Symptoms:

Symptoms of sclerosing cholangitis may include jaundice, pruritus, abdominal pain, and fatigue due to cholestasis and liver dysfunction.

Epidemiology:

Sclerosing cholangitis is a rare condition, with an estimated prevalence of around 6-16 cases per 100,000 individuals.

Pathology:

Sclerosing cholangitis is characterized by chronic inflammation and fibrosis of the bile ducts, leading to narrowing and strictures of the biliary tree.

Treatment:

Treatment options for sclerosing cholangitis include endoscopic or surgical interventions to improve bile drainage, immunosuppressive medications, and liver transplantation in advanced cases.

Imaging Features:

  • Multifocal strictures and dilation of the bile ducts
  • Beaded appearance of the bile ducts
  • Associated biliary stones or cholangiocarcinoma

Differential Diagnosis

  • Primary biliary cholangitis
  • Choledocholithiasis
  • Cholangiocarcinoma
  • AIDS cholangiopathy
