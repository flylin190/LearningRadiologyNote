Mirizzi Syndrome

Symptoms:

Mirizzi syndrome presents with jaundice, right upper quadrant abdominal pain, and fever, which may mimic gallstone disease.

Epidemiology:

It is a rare complication of long-standing gallstone disease and is more common in women and older individuals.

Pathology:

Mirizzi syndrome is caused by the impaction of a gallstone in the cystic duct or neck of the gallbladder, leading to obstruction of the common bile duct.

Treatment:

Treatment involves surgical intervention to remove the gallstone and repair any resulting bile duct injuries.

Imaging Features:

  • Dilated intrahepatic bile ducts on ultrasound or CT
  • Obstruction of the common bile duct
  • Compression or distortion of the common hepatic duct

Differential Diagnosis:

  • Gallstone ileus
  • Gallbladder cancer
  • Choledocholithiasis
