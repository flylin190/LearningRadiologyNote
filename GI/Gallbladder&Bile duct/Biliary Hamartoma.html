Biliary Hamartoma

Symptoms:

Biliary hamartomas are typically asymptomatic and are often incidental findings on imaging studies.

Epidemiology:

Biliary hamartomas are relatively rare, with an estimated prevalence of 0.6-2.8% in the general population.

Pathology:

A biliary hamartoma is a benign, non-neoplastic malformation composed of disorganized bile ducts, fibrous tissue, and sometimes cysts.

Treatment:

Biliary hamartomas are generally benign and do not require treatment unless they become symptomatic or cause complications.

Imaging Features:

  • Well-circumscribed, subcapsular liver lesion
  • May contain cysts or dilated bile ducts
  • No contrast enhancement

Differential Diagnosis

  • Simple hepatic cyst
  • Polycystic liver disease
  • Liver metastases
  • Hepatocellular carcinoma
