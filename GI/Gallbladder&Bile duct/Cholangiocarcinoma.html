Cholangiocarcinoma

Symptoms:

Symptoms of cholangiocarcinoma may include jaundice, abdominal pain, weight loss, fever, and pruritus (itching).

Epidemiology:

Cholangiocarcinoma is a relatively rare cancer, accounting for approximately 3% of all gastrointestinal cancers. It is more common in individuals with underlying conditions such as primary sclerosing cholangitis and liver fluke infections.

Pathology:

Cholangiocarcinoma is a malignant tumor that arises from the bile duct epithelium. It can occur in the intrahepatic (within the liver) or extrahepatic (outside the liver) bile ducts.

Treatment:

Treatment options for cholangiocarcinoma depend on the location and stage of the tumor, but may include surgical resection, liver transplantation, radiation therapy, chemotherapy, or a combination of these modalities.

Imaging Features:

  • Bile duct dilatation and strictures on ultrasound, CT, or MRI
  • Focal mass lesion with irregular margins and enhancement on CT or MRI
  • Intrahepatic biliary ductal dilatation (for intrahepatic cholangiocarcinoma)

Differential Diagnosis:

  • Bile duct stones
  • Benign bile duct strictures
  • Hepatocellular carcinoma
