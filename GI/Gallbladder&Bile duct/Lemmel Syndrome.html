Lemmel Syndrome

Symptoms:

Lemmel syndrome is characterized by obstructive jaundice due to a periampullary duodenal diverticulum causing a mechanical obstruction of the common bile duct.

Epidemiology:

Lemmel syndrome is rare and typically occurs in older adults.

Pathology:

The exact cause of Lemmel syndrome is unknown, but it is thought to be related to the presence of a periampullary diverticulum that causes compression or obstruction of the common bile duct.

Treatment:

Treatment for Lemmel syndrome involves relieving the obstruction of the common bile duct. This may be done with endoscopic retrograde cholangiopancreatography (ERCP) to remove the obstructing material or with surgery to repair the diverticulum.

Imaging Features:

  • Dilated common bile duct on ultrasound or CT scan
  • Periampullary diverticulum on imaging

Differential Diagnosis

  • Choledocholithiasis
  • Cholangiocarcinoma
  • Periampullary tumors
