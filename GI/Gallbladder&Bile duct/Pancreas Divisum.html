Pancreas Divisum

Symptoms:

Pancreas divisum is often asymptomatic, but it can cause recurrent pancreatitis, abdominal pain, and impaired pancreatic function.

Epidemiology:

Pancreas divisum is a relatively common congenital anomaly, occurring in approximately 5-10% of the population.

Pathology:

Pancreas divisum is a congenital anomaly in which the pancreatic duct system fails to fuse properly during embryonic development. As a result, the main pancreatic duct drains only the head, body, and part of the tail of the pancreas, while the accessory duct drains the remaining portion of the tail.

Treatment:

Treatment for pancreas divisum is typically conservative, involving pain management and lifestyle modifications. In severe cases, endoscopic or surgical interventions may be required to improve pancreatic drainage.

Imaging Features:

  • Failure of the ventral and dorsal pancreatic ducts to fuse
  • Dilated accessory pancreatic duct
  • Restricted outflow of the accessory duct into the minor papilla

Differential Diagnosis:

  • Chronic pancreatitis
  • Pancreatic ductal obstruction
  • Pancreatic neoplasms
