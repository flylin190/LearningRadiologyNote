Omental Infarction

Symptoms:

Omental infarction typically presents with acute abdominal pain, which may be localized to the right or left lower quadrant. Other symptoms may include nausea, vomiting, and fever.

Epidemiology:

Omental infarction is a rare condition that is more common in adults, especially those with risk factors such as obesity or a history of abdominal surgery.

Pathology:

Omental infarction is caused by a blockage of the blood supply to the omentum, leading to ischemia and tissue death.

Treatment:

Treatment for omental infarction may include pain management, hydration, and in some cases, surgery to remove the infarcted omentum.

Imaging Features:

  • Localized fat stranding or omental mass on CT scan

Differential Diagnosis

  • Acute appendicitis
  • Diverticulitis
  • Acute cholecystitis
