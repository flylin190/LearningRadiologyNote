Encapsulating Peritoneal Sclerosis

Symptoms:

Symptoms of encapsulating peritoneal sclerosis include abdominal pain, nausea, vomiting, weight loss, and bowel obstruction due to the formation of a fibrous membrane encapsulating the intestines.

Epidemiology:

Encapsulating peritoneal sclerosis is a rare condition, primarily seen in patients undergoing peritoneal dialysis, with an estimated prevalence of around 1-3% in this population.

Pathology:

Encapsulating peritoneal sclerosis is characterized by the development of a thick, fibrous membrane encasing the intestines, leading to intestinal obstruction and loss of peritoneal function.

Treatment:

Treatment options for encapsulating peritoneal sclerosis include medical management with immunosuppressive agents, surgical intervention for bowel obstruction, and potential transition to hemodialysis in severe cases.

Imaging Features:

  • Peritoneal calcifications and thickening
  • Encapsulation of the intestines by a fibrous membrane
  • Dilated bowel loops and signs of obstruction

Differential Diagnosis

  • Peritoneal carcinomatosis
  • Abdominal tuberculosis
  • Peritoneal mesothelioma
  • Sclerosing encapsulating peritonitis
