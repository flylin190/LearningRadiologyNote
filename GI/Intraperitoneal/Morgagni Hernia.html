Morgagni Hernia

Symptoms:

Morgagni hernia may be asymptomatic or present with symptoms such as chest pain, difficulty breathing, and abdominal pain.

Epidemiology:

Morgagni hernia is a rare type of congenital diaphragmatic hernia that typically occurs on the right side of the chest.

Pathology:

Morgagni hernia occurs due to a defect in the diaphragm, allowing abdominal organs to herniate into the chest cavity.

Treatment:

Treatment for Morgagni hernia usually involves surgical repair to close the defect in the diaphragm.

Imaging Features:

  • Abdominal organs herniating into the chest cavity on chest X-ray or CT scan

Differential Diagnosis:

  • Other types of diaphragmatic hernias
  • Pulmonary hypoplasia
  • Pulmonary sequestration
