Pseudomyxoma Peritonei

Symptoms:

Patients with pseudomyxoma peritonei may experience abdominal pain, distension, nausea, vomiting, and weight loss due to the accumulation of mucinous ascites.

Epidemiology:

Pseudomyxoma peritonei is a rare condition, with an estimated incidence of 1-2 cases per million population per year. It typically affects older adults, with a slight female predominance.

Pathology:

Pseudomyxoma peritonei is characterized by the progressive accumulation of mucinous ascites within the peritoneal cavity, often originating from a ruptured mucinous tumor of the appendix or ovary.

Treatment:

Treatment typically involves surgical debulking or cytoreductive surgery to remove as much of the mucinous tumor as possible, followed by hyperthermic intraperitoneal chemotherapy (HIPEC) to target any remaining cancer cells.

Imaging Features:

  • Mucinous ascites with low attenuation on CT
  • Scalloping or displacement of intra-abdominal organs
  • Possible soft tissue implants or calcifications

Differential Diagnosis:

  • Peritoneal carcinomatosis
  • Mucinous cystic neoplasms
  • Peritoneal mesothelioma
