Gauzoma

Symptoms:

Gauzoma is a rare condition characterized by the presence of gauze or surgical sponge remnants within the body after surgery. Symptoms may vary depending on the location of the retained foreign body.

Epidemiology:

Incidence of gauzoma is low due to strict surgical protocols, but it can occur in any surgical procedure involving the use of surgical sponges or gauze.

Pathology:

The retained gauze or sponge can lead to a foreign body reaction, causing inflammation and potentially leading to abscess formation or other complications.

Treatment:

Treatment for gauzoma involves surgical removal of the retained foreign body. Antibiotics may be prescribed if infection is present.

Imaging Features:

  • Presence of radiopaque material on imaging studies (X-ray, CT scan)
  • Soft tissue mass or abscess formation around the retained foreign body

Differential Diagnosis:

  • Other causes of postoperative complications, such as infection or hematoma
