Bochdalek Hernia

Symptoms:

Bochdalek hernia may be asymptomatic or present with symptoms such as respiratory distress, cyanosis, and abdominal distension in neonates.

Epidemiology:

Bochdalek hernia is a congenital diaphragmatic hernia that typically occurs on the left side of the chest.

Pathology:

Bochdalek hernia occurs due to a defect in the posterolateral aspect of the diaphragm, allowing abdominal organs to herniate into the chest cavity.

Treatment:

Treatment for Bochdalek hernia usually involves surgical repair to close the defect in the diaphragm.

Imaging Features:

  • Abdominal organs herniating into the chest cavity on chest X-ray or CT scan

Differential Diagnosis:

  • Other types of diaphragmatic hernias
  • Pulmonary hypoplasia
  • Pulmonary sequestration
