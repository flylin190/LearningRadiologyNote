Spigelian Hernia

Symptoms:

Symptoms of a Spigelian hernia include a palpable mass or bulge in the lower abdomen, pain or discomfort, and in severe cases, bowel obstruction or strangulation.

Epidemiology:

Spigelian hernias are rare, accounting for approximately 0.1-2% of all hernias. They are more common in individuals with conditions that increase intra-abdominal pressure or previous abdominal surgery.

Pathology:

A Spigelian hernia occurs when abdominal contents (typically small bowel or omentum) protrude through a defect in the Spigelian fascia, a layer of abdominal muscle along the semilunar line.

Treatment:

Treatment typically involves surgical repair, either open or laparoscopic, to reduce the hernia contents and reinforce the fascial defect with mesh or sutures.

Imaging Features:

  • Soft tissue mass or bowel loop protruding through the Spigelian fascia on CT or MRI
  • Characteristic location along the semilunar line, lateral to the rectus abdominis muscle
  • Bowel obstruction or strangulation in advanced cases

Differential Diagnosis:

  • Other types of ventral hernias (e.g., incisional, umbilical)
  • Abdominal wall tumors or masses
  • Hematoma or abscess
