Diverticulitis

Symptoms:

Diverticulitis typically presents with abdominal pain (usually in the lower left side), fever, nausea, and changes in bowel habits.

Epidemiology:

Diverticulitis is more common in older adults and is associated with a low-fiber diet and constipation.

Pathology:

Diverticulitis occurs when diverticula (small, bulging pouches) in the digestive tract become inflamed or infected.

Treatment:

Treatment for diverticulitis may include antibiotics, pain relievers, and, in severe cases, surgery.

Imaging Features:

  • CT scan may show thickening of the colonic wall, pericolic fat stranding, and abscess formation.

Differential Diagnosis:

  • Colon cancer
  • Appendicitis
  • Ischemic colitis
