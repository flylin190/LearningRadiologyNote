Gastrointestinal Stromal Tumor (GIST)

Symptoms:

Symptoms of GIST may include abdominal pain, gastrointestinal bleeding, early satiety, and a palpable abdominal mass, depending on the location and size of the tumor.

Epidemiology:

GISTs are relatively rare, with an estimated incidence of 10-20 cases per million population per year.

Pathology:

GISTs are mesenchymal tumors arising from the interstitial cells of Cajal in the gastrointestinal tract wall. They are commonly driven by mutations in the KIT or PDGFRA genes.

Treatment:

Treatment for GIST typically involves surgical resection for localized tumors, and targeted therapy with tyrosine kinase inhibitors (e.g., imatinib) for advanced or metastatic disease.

Imaging Features:

  • Well-circumscribed, enhancing mass arising from the gastrointestinal tract wall
  • May contain necrotic or cystic areas
  • Possible metastases to the liver or peritoneum

Differential Diagnosis

  • Leiomyoma
  • Leiomyosarcoma
  • Gastrointestinal lymphoma
  • Gastrointestinal carcinoma
