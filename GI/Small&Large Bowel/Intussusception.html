Intussusception

Symptoms:

Common symptoms of intussusception include abdominal pain, vomiting, and bloody stools. In infants and young children, this condition may present with colicky abdominal pain, drawing up of the legs, and a palpable mass in the abdomen.

Epidemiology:

Intussusception is most common in children between the ages of 6 months and 2 years. It is more common in boys than girls.

Pathology:

Intussusception occurs when one portion of the intestine invaginates into another portion, causing obstruction and potentially compromising blood flow to the affected segment of the intestine.

Treatment:

Treatment for intussusception may include an air or barium enema to attempt to reduce the intussuscepted segment, or surgery if conservative measures are unsuccessful or if there are signs of bowel necrosis.

Imaging Features:

  • "Target" or "doughnut" sign on ultrasound or CT scan
  • Presence of a soft tissue mass within the bowel lumen
  • Bowel obstruction or ileus on imaging

Differential Diagnosis:

  • Meckel's diverticulum
  • Appendicitis
  • Lymphoma
