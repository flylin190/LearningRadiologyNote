Epiploic Appendagitis

Symptoms:

The main symptom of epiploic appendagitis is localized abdominal pain, often in the lower quadrants, which can mimic other acute abdominal conditions.

Epidemiology:

Epiploic appendagitis is a relatively uncommon condition, but it is more common in adults, particularly those who are overweight or obese.

Pathology:

Epiploic appendagitis is caused by torsion or spontaneous venous thrombosis of an epiploic appendage, which are small peritoneal pouches filled with fat that protrude from the serosal surface of the colon.

Treatment:

Treatment is typically conservative, including pain management with non-steroidal anti-inflammatory drugs (NSAIDs) and rest. Surgical intervention is rarely required.

Imaging Features:

  • Oval or round fat-density lesion adjacent to the colon wall on CT
  • Surrounding inflammatory changes, such as stranding or fluid
  • No evidence of bowel obstruction or free intraperitoneal air

Differential Diagnosis

  • Acute appendicitis
  • Acute diverticulitis
  • Colonic malignancy
  • Omental infarction
