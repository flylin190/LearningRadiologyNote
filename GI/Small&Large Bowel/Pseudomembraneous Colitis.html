Pseudomembraneous Colitis

Symptoms:

The main symptoms of pseudomembraneous colitis include watery diarrhea, abdominal pain, fever, and sometimes bloody stools.

Epidemiology:

Pseudomembraneous colitis is a relatively common condition, particularly among hospitalized patients receiving broad-spectrum antibiotic therapy. It is caused by the bacterium Clostridioides difficile.

Pathology:

Pseudomembraneous colitis is caused by a toxin produced by Clostridioides difficile, which leads to inflammation and the formation of pseudomembranes (yellow-white plaques) in the colon.

Treatment:

Treatment typically involves discontinuing the offending antibiotic and initiating specific antibiotic therapy against Clostridioides difficile, such as metronidazole or vancomycin. In severe cases, surgery may be required.

Imaging Features:

  • Colonic wall thickening
  • Thumbprinting or accordion-like haustral pattern
  • Possible ascites or perforation in severe cases

Differential Diagnosis:

  • Inflammatory bowel disease
  • Ischemic colitis
  • Infectious colitis
