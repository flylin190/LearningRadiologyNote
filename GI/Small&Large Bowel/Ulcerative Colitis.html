Ulcerative Colitis

Symptoms:

Ulcerative colitis presents with bloody diarrhea, abdominal pain, and urgency to defecate. Systemic symptoms may include fatigue, weight loss, and fever.

Epidemiology:

It typically presents in young adults, with a slightly higher incidence in females. The exact cause is unknown, but it is thought to involve an abnormal immune response to gut flora.

Pathology:

Ulcerative colitis is characterized by inflammation and ulcers in the innermost lining of the colon and rectum.

Treatment:

Treatment aims to control inflammation and symptoms. This may include anti-inflammatory medications, immunosuppressants, and in severe cases, surgery to remove the colon.

Imaging Features:

  • Loss of haustral markings on barium enema
  • Continuous colonic involvement, starting from the rectum
  • Friable mucosa and pseudopolyps on colonoscopy

Differential Diagnosis:

  • Crohn's disease
  • Infectious colitis (e.g., Clostridium difficile)
  • Ischemic colitis
