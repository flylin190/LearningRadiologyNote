Stercoral Colitis

Symptoms:

Stercoral colitis may present with abdominal pain, constipation, rectal bleeding, and signs of sepsis.

Epidemiology:

Stercoral colitis is a rare condition that occurs most commonly in elderly, debilitated patients with chronic constipation or fecal impaction.

Pathology:

Stercoral colitis is characterized by inflammation and ulceration of the colonic wall due to pressure necrosis from impacted fecal material.

Treatment:

Treatment for stercoral colitis involves relieving the fecal impaction and managing any complications, such as perforation or peritonitis, with antibiotics and surgery if necessary.

Imaging Features:

  • Segmental colonic wall thickening on CT scan
  • Presence of impacted feces in the colon

Differential Diagnosis:

  • Ischemic colitis
  • Colonic carcinoma
  • Infectious colitis
