Crohn's Disease

Symptoms:

Crohn's disease symptoms can vary but often include abdominal pain, diarrhea, weight loss, fatigue, and blood in the stool.

Epidemiology:

Crohn's disease can affect people of all ages, but it is most commonly diagnosed in young adults. It is more common in people of Ashkenazi Jewish descent.

Pathology:

Crohn's disease is a chronic inflammatory condition that can affect any part of the gastrointestinal tract, but it most commonly affects the end of the small intestine (ileum) and the beginning of the large intestine (colon).

Treatment:

Treatment for Crohn's disease aims to reduce inflammation and relieve symptoms. This may include medication, such as corticosteroids, immunomodulators, and biologic therapies, as well as dietary and lifestyle changes.

Imaging Features:

  • Bowel wall thickening on CT or MRI
  • Skip lesions (areas of inflammation with normal tissue in between) on imaging
  • Fistulas or abscesses may be seen on imaging

Differential Diagnosis:

  • Ulcerative colitis
  • Intestinal tuberculosis
  • Colon cancer
