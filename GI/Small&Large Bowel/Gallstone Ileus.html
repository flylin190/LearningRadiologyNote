Gallstone Ileus

Symptoms:

Gallstone ileus presents with symptoms of bowel obstruction, including abdominal pain, distension, nausea, and vomiting.

Epidemiology:

It is a rare complication of cholelithiasis and is more common in older individuals with multiple comorbidities.

Pathology:

Gallstone ileus occurs when a gallstone passes into the intestinal tract through a cholecystoenteric fistula and causes obstruction of the bowel.

Treatment:

Treatment involves surgical intervention to remove the obstructing gallstone and repair any fistulas or bowel perforations.

Imaging Features:

  • Pneumobilia (air in the biliary tree) on abdominal x-ray
  • Calcified gallstone in the bowel on abdominal x-ray or CT
  • Bowel obstruction on CT

Differential Diagnosis:

  • Small bowel obstruction
  • Colon cancer
  • Adhesive bowel obstruction
