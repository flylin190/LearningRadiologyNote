Patent Ductus Arteriosus

Symptoms:

Symptoms of patent ductus arteriosus (PDA) may include shortness of breath, fatigue, poor feeding in infants, and signs of heart failure in severe cases.

Epidemiology:

PDA is a congenital heart defect that occurs in approximately 1 in 2,000 live births. It is more common in premature infants and females.

Pathology:

PDA is a persistent patency of the ductus arteriosus, a normal fetal blood vessel that connects the pulmonary artery to the aorta, allowing blood to bypass the lungs in utero.

Treatment:

Treatment for PDA may involve medical management with non-steroidal anti-inflammatory drugs (NSAIDs) or prostaglandin inhibitors in premature infants, or surgical or transcatheter closure of the ductus in older children and adults.

Imaging Features:

  • Continuous flow from the aorta to the pulmonary artery on Doppler ultrasound
  • Enlarged main pulmonary artery and left heart chambers
  • Pulmonary overcirculation and increased pulmonary vascular markings

Differential Diagnosis

  • Aortopulmonary window
  • Truncus arteriosus
  • Aortic or pulmonary artery aneurysm
  • Aortic or pulmonary artery dissection
