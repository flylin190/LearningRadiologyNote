Aneurysm of Sinus of Valsalva

Symptoms:

Common symptoms of aneurysm of the sinus of Valsalva include chest pain, shortness of breath, palpitations, and signs of heart failure.

Epidemiology:

Aneurysms of the sinus of Valsalva are rare and are usually congenital in origin.

Pathology:

The aneurysm typically arises from a defect in the aortic wall near the aortic valve, leading to a communication between the aorta and the right heart chambers.

Treatment:

Treatment depends on the size and location of the aneurysm, but may include surgical repair or percutaneous closure.

Imaging Features:

  • Dilated sinus of Valsalva on echocardiography
  • Aneurysmal sac arising from the aortic root on CT or MRI

Differential Diagnosis

  • Aortic root dilation
  • Aortic valve stenosis or regurgitation
