Ventricular Aneurysm

Symptoms:

The symptoms of ventricular aneurysm can vary depending on the size and location of the aneurysm. Common symptoms may include chest pain, shortness of breath, palpitations, fatigue, and dizziness.

Epidemiology:

Ventricular aneurysms can occur at any age but are more common in older adults who have experienced a heart attack. Other risk factors include high blood pressure, smoking, and a family history of heart disease.

Treatment:

The treatment for ventricular aneurysm depends on the size and symptoms. In some cases, medication may be used to manage symptoms and prevent complications. In other cases, surgery may be necessary to repair or remove the aneurysm.

Imaging Features:

  • Thinning and bulging of the ventricular wall on echocardiography
  • Abnormal contour of the ventricle on cardiac MRI
  • Delayed enhancement of the ventricular wall on cardiac MRI

Differential Diagnosis

  • Left ventricular pseudoaneurysm
  • Cardiac tumor
  • Pericardial cyst
