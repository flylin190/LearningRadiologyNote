Fabry Disease (Cardiac Manifestations)

Symptoms:

Cardiac manifestations of Fabry disease can include angina, arrhythmias, heart failure, and valvular dysfunction.

Epidemiology:

Fabry disease is a rare, X-linked inherited disorder that primarily affects males, with an estimated prevalence of 1 in 40,000 to 1 in 117,000 individuals.

Pathology:

Fabry disease is caused by a deficiency or absence of the enzyme alpha-galactosidase A, leading to the accumulation of globotriaosylceramide (Gb3) in various tissues, including the heart.

Treatment:

The primary treatment for Fabry disease is enzyme replacement therapy (ERT) with recombinant alpha-galactosidase A to help reduce the accumulation of Gb3 and alleviate symptoms, including cardiac manifestations.

Imaging Features:

  • Left ventricular hypertrophy
  • Valvular thickening and regurgitation
  • Late gadolinium enhancement
  • Increased T1 and T2 signal(Myocardial fibrosis and scarring)
  • Myocardial strain abnormalities

Differential Diagnosis

  • Hypertrophic cardiomyopathy
  • Amyloidosis
  • Ischemic heart disease
  • Valvular heart disease
