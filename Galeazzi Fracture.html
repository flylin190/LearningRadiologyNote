Galeazzi Fracture

Symptoms:

Galeazzi fracture is a fracture of the radius bone in the forearm, usually accompanied by dislocation of the distal radioulnar joint (DRUJ). Symptoms include pain, swelling, and deformity of the forearm.

Epidemiology:

Galeazzi fracture is more common in younger individuals and often results from a fall onto an outstretched hand.

Pathology:

The fracture occurs at the junction of the middle and distal thirds of the radius bone, often with associated disruption of the triangular fibrocartilage complex (TFCC).

Treatment:

Treatment of Galeazzi fracture involves surgical fixation of the radius and stabilization of the DRUJ. Immobilization with a cast may also be necessary.

Imaging Features:

  • Fracture of the radius bone, often at the junction of the middle and distal thirds
  • Dislocation of the distal radioulnar joint (DRUJ)
