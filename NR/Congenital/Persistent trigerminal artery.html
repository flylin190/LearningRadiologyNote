Persistent Trigeminal Artery

Symptoms:

Most patients with persistent trigeminal artery are asymptomatic. However, in some cases, it may present with symptoms such as headache, dizziness, or facial pain.

Epidemiology:

Persistent trigeminal artery is a rare anatomical variant that is present in less than 0.1% of the population. It is more common in women and is usually discovered incidentally during imaging studies for other conditions.

Treatment:

Asymptomatic cases of persistent trigeminal artery do not require treatment. Symptomatic cases may be managed with medications for symptom relief.

Imaging Features:

  • Identification of the persistent trigeminal artery on angiography
  • Connection between the internal carotid artery and the basilar artery
  • May be associated with aneurysms or other vascular anomalies

Differential Diagnosis

  • Vertebrobasilar dolichoectasia
  • Internal carotid artery aneurysm
  • Carotid-cavernous fistula
