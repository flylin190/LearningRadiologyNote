Epidermoid Cyst

Symptoms:

Epidermoid cysts are usually asymptomatic but can cause symptoms if they grow large enough to compress surrounding structures. Symptoms may include headache, dizziness, nausea, and neurological deficits.

Epidemiology:

Epidermoid cysts are rare and can occur at any age, but are most commonly diagnosed in adults.

Pathology:

Epidermoid cysts are benign tumors that develop from ectodermal tissue and are filled with keratin and lipid-rich debris.

Treatment:

Treatment for epidermoid cysts involves surgical removal to prevent complications such as infection or compression of surrounding structures.

Imaging Features:

  • Well-defined, round or oval lesion on imaging
  • Low signal intensity on T1-weighted MRI
  • High signal intensity on T2-weighted MRI

Differential Diagnosis:

  • Arachnoid cyst
  • Dermoid cyst
  • Colloid cyst
