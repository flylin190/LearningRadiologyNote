Blake's Pouch Cyst

Symptoms:

Blake's pouch cyst is a congenital abnormality of the posterior fossa that can cause hydrocephalus, cranial nerve palsies, or signs of increased intracranial pressure in infants and young children.

Epidemiology:

Blake's pouch cyst is a rare condition that occurs during embryonic development, and its exact prevalence is unknown. It is more common in females than males.

Treatment:

The treatment of Blake's pouch cyst depends on the symptoms and severity of the condition. In some cases, observation may be sufficient, while in others, surgical intervention may be necessary to relieve hydrocephalus or other associated complications.

Imaging Features:

  • Cystic lesion in the posterior fossa, usually located near the cerebellar vermis
  • Compression of the fourth ventricle
  • Associated hydrocephalus
