Neuroglial Cyst

Symptoms:

Neuroglial cysts are usually asymptomatic and are often discovered incidentally on imaging studies. They may rarely cause symptoms if they become large enough to compress surrounding structures.

Epidemiology:

Neuroglial cysts are rare and can occur at any age, but are most commonly found in children and young adults.

Pathology:

Neuroglial cysts are benign cystic lesions lined by glial tissue, typically located in the brain or spinal cord.

Treatment:

Asymptomatic neuroglial cysts may not require treatment. Symptomatic cysts may be surgically removed if they are causing symptoms.

Imaging Features:

  • Well-defined cystic lesion with smooth margins on MRI
  • No enhancement or surrounding edema

Differential Diagnosis:

  • Arachnoid cyst
  • Epidermoid cyst
  • Colloid cyst
