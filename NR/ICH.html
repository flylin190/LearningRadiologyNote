Intracranial Hemorrhage

Symptoms:

Common symptoms of intracranial hemorrhage include severe headache, nausea, vomiting, weakness, numbness, or tingling (especially on one side of the body), difficulty speaking or understanding speech, changes in vision, and loss of coordination.

Epidemiology:

Intracranial hemorrhage can occur at any age, but it is more common in older adults. Certain conditions, such as high blood pressure, can increase the risk of intracranial hemorrhage.

Treatment:

The treatment for intracranial hemorrhage depends on the cause and severity of the bleeding. It may include medications to control blood pressure and prevent further bleeding, surgery to remove the blood clot or repair damaged blood vessels, and supportive care to manage symptoms.

Imaging Features:

  • Hyperdense lesion on CT scan
  • Mass effect with midline shift
  • Presence of intraventricular hemorrhage

Differential Diagnosis

  • Ischemic stroke
  • Subdural hematoma
  • Epidural hematoma
  • Brain tumor
