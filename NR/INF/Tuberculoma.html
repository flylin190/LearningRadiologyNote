Tuberculoma (brain)

Symptoms:

Common symptoms of tuberculoma in the brain include headache, nausea, vomiting, seizures, and focal neurological deficits such as weakness or sensory loss.

Epidemiology:

Tuberculoma is more common in areas where tuberculosis is endemic. It can affect individuals of any age group.

Treatment:

Treatment of tuberculoma involves anti-tuberculosis medication, such as isoniazid, rifampicin, pyrazinamide, and ethambutol. In some cases, surgical removal may be necessary.

Imaging Features:

  • Rim-enhancing lesion on CT or MRI
  • Central low attenuation or signal intensity
  • Perilesional edema

Differential Diagnosis

  • Cerebral abscess
  • Glioma
  • Metastasis
