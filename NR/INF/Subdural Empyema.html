Subdural Empyema

Symptoms:

Common symptoms of subdural empyema include fever, headache, nausea, vomiting, neurological deficits, and signs of increased intracranial pressure.

Epidemiology:

Subdural empyema is a rare condition that can occur at any age, but it is more common in children and young adults.

Pathology:

Subdural empyema is usually caused by a bacterial infection, often as a complication of sinusitis or otitis media.

Treatment:

Treatment for subdural empyema typically involves surgical drainage of the infected fluid and antibiotic therapy to treat the underlying infection.

Imaging Features:

  • Loculated fluid collection with peripheral enhancement on MRI
  • Compression of the underlying brain tissue

Differential Diagnosis:

  • Subdural hematoma
  • Brain abscess
  • Meningitis
