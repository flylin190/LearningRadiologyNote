Neurocysticercosis

Symptoms:

Common symptoms of neurocysticercosis include headache, seizures, cognitive impairment, and focal neurological deficits. The symptoms can vary depending on the location and number of cysts in the brain.

Epidemiology:

Neurocysticercosis is caused by the larvae of the pork tapeworm, Taenia solium. It is more common in areas where pork is consumed and sanitation is poor. Travel to endemic regions is also a risk factor.

Treatment:

The main treatment for neurocysticercosis is medication to kill the parasite and reduce inflammation in the brain. In some cases, surgery may be needed to remove the cysts.

Imaging Features:

  • Ring-enhancing lesions on CT or MRI
  • Calcifications in the brain

Differential Diagnosis

  • Primary brain tumors
  • Tuberculomas
  • Metastatic brain lesions
