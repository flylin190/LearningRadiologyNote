CLIPPERS

Symptoms:

CLIPPERS (Chronic Lymphocytic Inflammation with Pontine Perivascular Enhancement Responsive to Steroids) typically presents with subacute onset of brainstem and/or cerebellar symptoms, including ataxia, diplopia, dysarthria, and cognitive impairment.

Epidemiology:

CLIPPERS is a rare inflammatory disorder that predominantly affects adults, with a slight male predominance. The exact cause is unknown, but it is thought to be an immune-mediated process.

Pathology:

CLIPPERS is characterized by perivascular inflammation in the central nervous system, particularly in the pons and cerebellum. The inflammation is predominantly composed of lymphocytes.

Treatment:

The mainstay of treatment for CLIPPERS is corticosteroids, which can lead to rapid clinical improvement. However, long-term immunosuppressive therapy may be necessary to prevent relapses.

Imaging Features:

  • Characteristic punctate or curvilinear enhancing lesions in the pons and/or cerebellum on MRI
  • Enhancement is typically perivascular and can extend into the surrounding white matter
  • Lesions may show a "salt-and-pepper" appearance on T2-weighted imaging

Differential Diagnosis:

  • Primary CNS lymphoma
  • Multiple sclerosis (MS)
  • Neuromyelitis optica (NMO)
  • Central pontine myelinolysis
