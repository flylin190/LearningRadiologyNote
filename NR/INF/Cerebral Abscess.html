Cerebral Abscess

Symptoms:

Common symptoms of cerebral abscess include headache, fever, nausea, vomiting, neurological deficits (such as weakness, numbness, or seizures), and altered mental status.

Epidemiology:

Cerebral abscess is a rare condition that can affect individuals of any age. It is more common in males and in individuals with conditions that weaken the immune system, such as HIV/AIDS or diabetes.

Pathology:

A cerebral abscess is a localized collection of pus within the brain parenchyma. It is typically caused by an infection that spreads to the brain from another part of the body, such as the lungs or heart.

Treatment:

The main treatment for cerebral abscess is to drain the abscess and administer antibiotics to treat the underlying infection. In some cases, surgery may be required to remove the abscess.

Imaging Features:

  • Ring-enhancing lesion on CT or MRI
  • Perifocal edema
  • Central hypodensity or signal intensity on CT or MRI

Differential Diagnosis

  • Brain tumor
  • Cerebral infarction
  • Primary or metastatic brain cancer
  • Cerebral hemorrhage
