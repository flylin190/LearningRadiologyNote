Coccidioidomycosis

Symptoms:

Common symptoms of coccidioidomycosis include fever, cough, chest pain, muscle aches, and joint pain. In severe cases, the infection can spread to other parts of the body, causing skin lesions, bone infections, or meningitis.

Epidemiology:

Coccidioidomycosis is endemic to certain areas of the southwestern United States, Mexico, and parts of Central and South America. It is more common in adults over the age of 60 and in individuals with weakened immune systems.

Pathology:

Coccidioidomycosis is caused by the fungus Coccidioides immitis or Coccidioides posadasii, which is found in the soil. The infection is typically acquired by inhaling fungal spores.

Treatment:

The treatment for coccidioidomycosis depends on the severity of the infection. Mild cases may resolve on their own, while more severe cases may require antifungal medications.

Imaging Features:

  • Pulmonary nodules or cavities on chest X-ray or CT
  • Diffuse interstitial or reticulonodular opacities
  • Pleural effusions

Differential Diagnosis:

  • Tuberculosis
  • Lung cancer
  • Other fungal infections
