Toxoplasmosis

Symptoms:

Common symptoms of toxoplasmosis include swollen lymph nodes, muscle aches, and flu-like symptoms. In severe cases, it can cause damage to the brain, eyes, or other organs.

Epidemiology:

Toxoplasmosis is caused by the Toxoplasma gondii parasite. It can be contracted by eating undercooked contaminated meat, drinking contaminated water, or through contact with cat feces.

Pathology:

In the brain, toxoplasmosis can cause focal lesions and necrosis.

Treatment:

Toxoplasmosis is usually treated with a combination of antibiotics, such as pyrimethamine and sulfadiazine.

Imaging Features:

  • Ring-enhancing lesions on MRI
  • Lesions are typically located in the basal ganglia or corticomedullary junction

Differential Diagnosis:

  • Primary central nervous system lymphoma (PCNSL)
  • Cerebral metastases
  • Tuberculosis (TB) brain abscess
  • Cryptococcal meningitis
