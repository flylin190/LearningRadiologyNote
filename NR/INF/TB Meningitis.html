TB Meningitis

Symptoms:

Common symptoms of TB meningitis include headache, fever, neck stiffness, altered mental status, and focal neurological deficits.

Epidemiology:

TB meningitis is more common in areas with high rates of tuberculosis infection. It can affect people of all ages, but it is more common in young children and the elderly.

Pathology:

TB meningitis is caused by Mycobacterium tuberculosis, which is spread through the air when an infected person coughs or sneezes. The bacteria can travel to the brain and cause inflammation of the meninges.

Treatment:

The main treatment for TB meningitis is a combination of antibiotics, usually including isoniazid, rifampin, ethambutol, and pyrazinamide. Treatment is typically prolonged, often lasting for several months.

Imaging Features:

  • Basal meningeal enhancement on contrast-enhanced CT or MRI
  • Hydrocephalus
  • Cerebral infarctions

Differential Diagnosis

  • Viral meningitis
  • Pyogenic meningitis
  • Fungal meningitis
