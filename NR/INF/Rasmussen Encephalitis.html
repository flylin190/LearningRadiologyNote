Rasmussen Encephalitis

Symptoms:

Rasmussen encephalitis typically presents with focal seizures that are difficult to control with medication. Other symptoms may include progressive neurological deficits, such as weakness, cognitive decline, and language difficulties.

Epidemiology:

Rasmussen encephalitis is a rare disease that usually affects children and young adults. The exact cause is unknown, but it is thought to be an autoimmune disorder.

Pathology:

Rasmussen encephalitis is characterized by chronic inflammation of one hemisphere of the brain, leading to progressive destruction of brain tissue. The exact cause of this inflammation is not fully understood, but it is believed to involve an autoimmune response.

Treatment:

Treatment for Rasmussen encephalitis aims to control seizures and reduce inflammation in the brain. This may include anti-seizure medications, corticosteroids, immunoglobulins, and in severe cases, surgery to remove the affected part of the brain.

Imaging Features:

  • Progressive atrophy of one hemisphere on MRI
  • Contralateral hemispheric hyperperfusion on perfusion imaging
  • Loss of gray-white matter differentiation in the affected hemisphere

Differential Diagnosis

  • Chronic focal encephalitis
  • Chronic focal epilepsia partialis continua
  • Herpes simplex encephalitis
