Japanese Encephalitis

Symptoms:

Japanese encephalitis can present with fever, headache, neck stiffness, disorientation, coma, seizures, spastic paralysis, and death.

Epidemiology:

Japanese encephalitis is caused by the Japanese encephalitis virus and is transmitted by mosquitoes. It is endemic in many parts of Asia.

Pathology:

The virus primarily affects the central nervous system, leading to inflammation of the brain.

Treatment:

There is no specific treatment for Japanese encephalitis, and management is supportive.

Imaging Features:

  • Thalamic lesions on MRI
  • Brainstem involvement

Differential Diagnosis

  • Other causes of viral encephalitis
