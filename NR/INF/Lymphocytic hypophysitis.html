Lymphocytic Hypophysitis

Symptoms:

Lymphocytic hypophysitis can present with symptoms related to pituitary hormone deficiencies, such as headache, visual disturbances, fatigue, and hormonal imbalances (e.g., menstrual irregularities, infertility, growth retardation).

Epidemiology:

Lymphocytic hypophysitis is a rare condition, predominantly affecting women in the late pregnancy or postpartum period, although it can occur in men and children as well.

Pathology:

Lymphocytic hypophysitis is an autoimmune disorder characterized by lymphocytic infiltration and inflammation of the pituitary gland, leading to various degrees of pituitary dysfunction.

Treatment:

Treatment for lymphocytic hypophysitis typically involves corticosteroid therapy to reduce inflammation and hormone replacement therapy to address any hormone deficiencies.

Imaging Features:

  • Pituitary gland enlargement or mass lesion on MRI
  • Homogeneous or heterogeneous enhancement on contrast-enhanced MRI
  • Potential involvement of the stalk or infundibulum

Differential Diagnosis:

  • Pituitary adenoma
  • Rathke's cleft cyst
  • Pituitary apoplexy
  • Metastasis or other sellar/parasellar masses
