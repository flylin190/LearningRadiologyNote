Primary Angiitis of the Central Nervous System (PACNS)

Symptoms:

Common symptoms of PACNS include headaches, confusion, seizures, and focal neurological deficits.

Epidemiology:

PACNS is a rare disease that can affect individuals of any age, but it is most commonly diagnosed in adults.

Pathology:

PACNS is characterized by inflammation of the blood vessels in the brain and spinal cord, which can lead to damage and narrowing of the vessels.

Treatment:

Treatment for PACNS typically involves high-dose corticosteroids to reduce inflammation, as well as other immunosuppressive medications.

Imaging Features:

  • Irregular narrowing or dilation of cerebral arteries on angiography
  • Lesions may be multifocal and involve both the anterior and posterior circulations

Differential Diagnosis

  • Central nervous system vasculitis secondary to systemic autoimmune diseases (e.g., lupus, rheumatoid arthritis)
  • Central nervous system infections
