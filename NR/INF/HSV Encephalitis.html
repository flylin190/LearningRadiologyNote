HSV Encephalitis

Symptoms:

HSV encephalitis can present with fever, headache, altered mental status, seizures, and focal neurological deficits.

Epidemiology:

HSV encephalitis is caused by the herpes simplex virus and is most commonly seen in individuals with immunocompromised conditions or elderly patients.

Pathology:

The virus enters the CNS through the olfactory nerve or the trigeminal nerve and causes inflammation and necrosis in the brain.

Treatment:

Treatment involves antiviral medications such as acyclovir to reduce viral replication and inflammation in the brain.

Imaging Features:

  • Increased signal intensity in the temporal lobes on MRI
  • Temporal lobe edema

Differential Diagnosis

  • Other causes of encephalitis
