Ventriculitis

Symptoms:

Common symptoms of ventriculitis include fever, headache, nausea, vomiting, neck stiffness, and changes in mental status.

Epidemiology:

Ventriculitis can occur in individuals of all ages, but it is more common in those with certain risk factors such as recent neurosurgery, shunts, or infections elsewhere in the body.

Pathology:

Ventriculitis is often caused by bacterial or fungal infections, which can lead to inflammation of the ventricles in the brain.

Treatment:

The treatment for ventriculitis typically involves antibiotics or antifungal medications to treat the underlying infection, as well as supportive care to manage symptoms.

Imaging Features:

  • Enhancing ventricular wall on contrast-enhanced CT or MRI
  • Hydrocephalus
  • Periventricular hypodensity on CT

Differential Diagnosis:

  • Neoplastic meningitis
  • Post-infectious ventriculomegaly
  • Choroid plexitis
