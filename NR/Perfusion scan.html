CT Perfusion Scan Values

Cerebral Blood Flow (CBF)

CBF represents the amount of blood flow per unit volume of brain tissue per unit time. It is measured in ml/100g/min. Decreased CBF may indicate ischemia or reduced perfusion.

Cerebral Blood Volume (CBV)

CBV represents the total volume of blood in a region of the brain. It is measured in ml/100g. Increased CBV may indicate hyperemia or increased blood volume.

Mean Transit Time (MTT)

MTT represents the average time it takes for blood to pass through a region of the brain. It is measured in seconds (s). Prolonged MTT may indicate delayed perfusion.

Time to Peak (TTP)

TTP represents the time it takes for the contrast agent to reach its maximum concentration in a region of the brain. It is measured in seconds (s). Prolonged TTP may indicate delayed perfusion.

Permeability Surface Area Product (PS)

PS represents the permeability of blood vessels and the surface area available for exchange of substances. It is measured in ml/100g/min. Increased PS may indicate increased permeability.

Relative Cerebral Blood Flow (rCBF)

rCBF represents the CBF in a region of the brain relative to a reference region. It is a normalized value for comparison.
