Neuromyelitis Optica (NMO)

Symptoms:

Neuromyelitis Optica typically presents with optic neuritis (eye pain, vision loss) and transverse myelitis (spinal cord inflammation, weakness, sensory disturbances).

Epidemiology:

NMO is a rare autoimmune disorder that primarily affects the optic nerves and spinal cord.

Pathology:

NMO is characterized by the presence of antibodies against aquaporin-4, a protein found in the central nervous system.

Treatment:

Treatment for NMO includes corticosteroids to reduce inflammation and immunosuppressive drugs to prevent future attacks. Plasma exchange therapy may also be used in severe cases.

Imaging Features:

  • Longitudinally extensive transverse myelitis (LETM) on MRI, involving three or more vertebral segments
  • Optic nerve involvement on MRI, often with optic nerve swelling or enhancement

Differential Diagnosis:

  • Multiple sclerosis (MS)
  • Acute disseminated encephalomyelitis (ADEM)
  • Systemic lupus erythematosus (SLE)
