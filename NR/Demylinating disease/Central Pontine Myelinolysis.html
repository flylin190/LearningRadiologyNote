Central Pontine Myelinolysis

Symptoms:

Central pontine myelinolysis can present with various neurological symptoms, including altered mental status, dysarthria, dysphagia, quadriplegia, and impaired consciousness or coma.

Epidemiology:

Central pontine myelinolysis is a rare condition that can occur in individuals of any age but is more common in those with rapid corrections of hyponatremia or chronic alcoholism.

Pathology:

Central pontine myelinolysis is characterized by the demyelination of pontine neurons, typically due to rapid shifts in osmotic gradients across cell membranes, leading to oligodendrocyte injury and myelin sheath destruction.

Treatment:

Treatment for central pontine myelinolysis is primarily supportive, focusing on managing the underlying condition and providing supportive care for neurological deficits. Slow correction of electrolyte imbalances is crucial in prevention.

Imaging Features:

  • Symmetric, well-demarcated lesion in the central pons on MRI
  • Hyperintense on T2-weighted and FLAIR sequences
  • Potential involvement of other brain regions (extrapontine myelinolysis)

Differential Diagnosis:

  • Basilar artery infarction
  • Pontine glioma
  • Multiple sclerosis
  • Central pontine myelinolytic lesion
