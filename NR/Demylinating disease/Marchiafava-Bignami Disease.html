Marchiafava-Bignami Disease

Symptoms:

Marchiafava-Bignami Disease is a rare neurological disorder characterized by demyelination and necrosis of the corpus callosum. Symptoms may include confusion, disorientation, seizures, and coma.

Epidemiology:

Marchiafava-Bignami Disease is most commonly seen in chronic alcoholics, although cases have been reported in non-alcoholic patients as well. The exact cause is unknown.

Pathology:

The pathology of Marchiafava-Bignami Disease involves demyelination and necrosis of the corpus callosum, which is thought to be related to chronic alcohol abuse and nutritional deficiencies.

Treatment:

There is no specific treatment for Marchiafava-Bignami Disease, and management focuses on supportive care and addressing underlying causes, such as alcohol cessation and nutritional support.

Imaging Features:

  • Loss of normal hyperintense signal in the corpus callosum on T1-weighted MRI
  • Restricted diffusion in the affected areas on diffusion-weighted imaging (DWI)
  • Atrophy of the corpus callosum in later stages

Differential Diagnosis:

  • Multiple sclerosis (MS)
  • Central pontine myelinolysis (CPM)
  • Acute disseminated encephalomyelitis (ADEM)
