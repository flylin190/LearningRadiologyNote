Sturge-Weber Syndrome

Symptoms:

Sturge-Weber Syndrome is characterized by a port-wine stain birthmark on the face, typically on one side, along with neurological abnormalities such as seizures and intellectual disability.

Epidemiology:

Sturge-Weber Syndrome is rare, with an estimated incidence of 1 in 20,000 to 50,000 individuals.

Pathology:

The syndrome is caused by somatic mutations in the GNAQ gene, leading to vascular malformations in the skin and brain.

Treatment:

Treatment may involve management of seizures with antiepileptic medications, laser therapy for the port-wine stain, and early intervention for developmental delays.

Imaging Features:

  • Leptomeningeal angiomatosis on brain imaging
  • Calcifications in the cerebral cortex
  • Vascular malformations on angiography

Differential Diagnosis:

  • Other causes of seizures and developmental delay
  • Other vascular malformation syndromes
