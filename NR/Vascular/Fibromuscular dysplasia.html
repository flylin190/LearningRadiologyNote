Fibromuscular Dysplasia

Symptoms:

Fibromuscular dysplasia can be asymptomatic or may cause symptoms such as hypertension, headache, pulsatile tinnitus, and dizziness.

Epidemiology:

Fibromuscular dysplasia is more common in women and often affects the renal and carotid arteries.

Pathology:

Fibromuscular dysplasia is characterized by abnormal cell growth in the walls of arteries, leading to narrowing and weakening of the affected arteries.

Treatment:

Treatment for fibromuscular dysplasia depends on the location and severity of the condition but may include medication to control blood pressure, angioplasty to widen the affected artery, or surgery in severe cases.

Imaging Features:

  • String-of-beads appearance on angiography, indicating alternating areas of stenosis and dilation in the affected artery
  • May also show arterial dissection or aneurysm formation

Differential Diagnosis

  • Arterial atherosclerosis
  • Arteritis
  • Arterial dissection
