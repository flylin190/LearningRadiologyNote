Cerebral Autosomal Dominant Arteriopathy with Subcortical Infarcts and Leukoencephalopathy(CADASIL)

Symptoms:

Symptoms of CADASIL include recurrent ischemic strokes, migraine headaches, cognitive impairment, and psychiatric disturbances.

Epidemiology:

CADASIL is a rare inherited disorder caused by mutations in the NOTCH3 gene. It is estimated to affect approximately 1 in 50,000 individuals worldwide.

Pathology:

CADASIL is characterized by progressive degeneration and thickening of the smooth muscle cells in small and medium-sized arteries, leading to impaired blood flow and ischemic injury to the brain.

Treatment:

There is no specific treatment for CADASIL. Management focuses on controlling risk factors for stroke, such as hypertension and hyperlipidemia, and providing symptomatic treatment for migraines, cognitive impairment, and psychiatric disturbances.

Imaging Features:

  • White matter hyperintensities on MRI, particularly in the temporal lobes and external capsules
  • Lacunar infarcts and microhemorrhages on MRI
  • Brain atrophy in advanced cases

Differential Diagnosis:

  • Multiple sclerosis
  • Small vessel ischemic disease
  • Other inherited leukoencephalopathies
