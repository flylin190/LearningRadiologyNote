Vein of Galen Aneurysmal Malformation

Symptoms:

Symptoms of a vein of Galen aneurysmal malformation depend on the size and location of the malformation, but may include heart failure, hydrocephalus, seizures, developmental delays, and high-output cardiac failure in infants.

Epidemiology:

Vein of Galen aneurysmal malformations are rare congenital vascular malformations that occur in the fetal or neonatal period. They are more common in males than females.

Pathology:

The malformation involves a fistulous connection between the arterial system and the vein of Galen, leading to a high-flow arteriovenous shunt. This can result in dilation of the vein of Galen and congestive heart failure.

Treatment:

Treatment may include endovascular embolization to occlude the abnormal blood vessels, surgery to remove the malformation, or supportive care to manage symptoms.

Imaging Features:

  • Arteriovenous shunting seen on angiography
  • Dilated vein of Galen on imaging
  • Enlarged cardiac silhouette on chest X-ray due to heart failure

Differential Diagnosis:

  • Arteriovenous malformation (AVM)
  • Vein of Galen varix
  • Subarachnoid hemorrhage
