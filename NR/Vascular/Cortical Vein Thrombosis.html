Cortical Vein Thrombosis

Symptoms:

Common symptoms of cortical vein thrombosis include headache, seizures, focal neurological deficits, altered consciousness, and signs of increased intracranial pressure.

Epidemiology:

Cortical vein thrombosis is a rare condition that can affect individuals of any age, but it is more common in young adults and females.

Pathology:

Cortical vein thrombosis occurs when a blood clot forms in the veins that drain blood from the cerebral cortex. This can lead to impaired blood flow and oxygen delivery to the brain tissue.

Treatment:

The main treatment for cortical vein thrombosis is anticoagulation therapy to prevent further clot formation and promote clot resolution. In some cases, thrombectomy or thrombolysis may be considered.

Imaging Features:

  • Hyperintensity on T1-weighted MRI
  • Lack of enhancement on contrast-enhanced MRI
  • Parenchymal hemorrhagic infarction

Differential Diagnosis

  • Arterial stroke
  • Subarachnoid hemorrhage
  • Meningitis
  • Encephalitis
