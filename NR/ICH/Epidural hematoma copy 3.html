Epidural Hematom

Symptoms:

Common symptoms of epidural hematoma include headache, confusion, dizziness, nausea and vomiting, loss of consciousness, and weakness or numbness in one side of the body. These symptoms can develop rapidly following a head injury.

Epidemiology:

Epidural hematoma is more common in young adults and middle-aged individuals, and it is more common in males than females. It often occurs as a result of a head injury, such as a skull fracture.

Treatment:

The main treatment for epidural hematoma is surgical evacuation of the hematoma to relieve pressure on the brain. This is usually done urgently to prevent complications such as brain damage or death.

Imaging Features:

  • Biconvex or lens-shaped hematoma on CT scan
  • Hyperdense appearance on CT scan due to acute bleeding
  • May cause midline shift of brain structures

Differential Diagnosis

  • Subdural hematoma
  • Intracerebral hemorrhage
  • Subarachnoid hemorrhage
