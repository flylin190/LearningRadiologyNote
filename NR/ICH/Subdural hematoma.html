Subdural Hematoma

Symptoms:

Common symptoms of subdural hematoma include headache, confusion, dizziness, nausea, vomiting, slurred speech, and weakness or numbness on one side of the body.

Epidemiology:

Subdural hematoma is more common in older adults, particularly those who have experienced a head injury. It can also occur in infants as a result of birth trauma.

Treatment:

The main treatment for subdural hematoma is surgical removal of the hematoma. In some cases, a procedure called a burr hole may be performed to drain the blood.

Imaging Features:

  • Crescent-shaped hyperdense lesion on CT scan
  • Midline shift of brain structures
  • Effacement of sulci and gyri

Differential Diagnosis

  • Extradural hematoma
  • Subarachnoid hemorrhage
  • Stroke
