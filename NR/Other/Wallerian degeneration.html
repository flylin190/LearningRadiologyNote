Wallerian Degeneration

Symptoms:

Wallerian degeneration is not a disease but a process of nerve degeneration that occurs after a nerve injury. Symptoms depend on the location and extent of the injury but may include weakness, numbness, and muscle atrophy.

Epidemiology:

Wallerian degeneration can occur in individuals of any age who experience nerve injury.

Pathology:

Wallerian degeneration is characterized by the degeneration of the distal portion of a nerve fiber following injury to the nerve.

Treatment:

There is no specific treatment for Wallerian degeneration. Recovery depends on the extent of the nerve injury and may include physical therapy to improve function.

Imaging Features:

  • Diffusion restriction on MRI in the affected nerve fibers
  • Atrophy of the affected muscles

Differential Diagnosis:

  • Chronic inflammatory demyelinating polyneuropathy (CIDP)
  • Peripheral nerve sheath tumors
  • Neuropathies due to metabolic or toxic causes
