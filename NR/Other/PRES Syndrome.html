PRES Syndrome

Symptoms:

The main symptoms of PRES (Posterior Reversible Encephalopathy Syndrome) include headache, altered mental status, visual disturbances, and seizures.

Epidemiology:

PRES can occur in individuals of any age but is more common in certain high-risk groups, such as those with eclampsia, hypertensive encephalopathy, or undergoing immunosuppressive therapy.

Pathology:

PRES is believed to be caused by a disruption of the blood-brain barrier, leading to vasogenic edema, particularly in the posterior regions of the brain.

Treatment:

Treatment for PRES involves addressing the underlying cause, such as controlling hypertension or discontinuing offending medications. In some cases, anti-seizure medications or corticosteroids may be administered.

Imaging Features:

  • Vasogenic edema, predominantly in the parietal and occipital lobes
  • Possible involvement of other regions, such as the frontal lobes or brainstem
  • Typically no enhancement on contrast-enhanced MRI

Differential Diagnosis:

  • Ischemic stroke
  • Venous sinus thrombosis
  • Encephalitis
  • Hypertensive encephalopathy
