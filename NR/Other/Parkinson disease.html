Parkinson's Disease

Symptoms:

The primary symptoms of Parkinson's disease include tremor, bradykinesia (slow movement), rigidity, and postural instability. Non-motor symptoms, such as cognitive impairment, depression, and sleep disturbances, can also occur.

Epidemiology:

Parkinson's disease is a common neurodegenerative disorder, affecting approximately 1% of individuals over 60 years old. The prevalence increases with age, and men are slightly more affected than women.

Pathology:

Parkinson's disease is characterized by the progressive loss of dopaminergic neurons in the substantia nigra region of the midbrain, leading to a deficiency of the neurotransmitter dopamine.

Treatment:

Treatment for Parkinson's disease typically involves a combination of medications (e.g., levodopa, dopamine agonists, MAO-B inhibitors) to manage symptoms, physical therapy, and in some cases, deep brain stimulation surgery.

Imaging Features:

  • Reduced signal intensity in the substantia nigra on T2-weighted MRI
  • Potential atrophy and volume loss in the midbrain and brainstem
  • Functional imaging may show decreased dopamine transporter binding

Differential Diagnosis:

  • Other neurodegenerative disorders (e.g., multiple system atrophy, progressive supranuclear palsy)
  • Secondary parkinsonism (e.g., drug-induced, vascular)
  • Essential tremor
