Mesial Temporal Sclerosis

Symptoms:

Common symptoms of mesial temporal sclerosis include seizures (often starting in childhood or adolescence), memory loss, and cognitive impairment.

Epidemiology:

Mesial temporal sclerosis is a common cause of epilepsy, especially in adults. It is more common in individuals with a history of febrile seizures in childhood.

Pathology:

Mesial temporal sclerosis is characterized by hippocampal atrophy, neuronal loss, and gliosis in the mesial temporal structures, particularly the hippocampus.

Treatment:

Treatment for mesial temporal sclerosis typically involves antiepileptic medications to control seizures. In some cases, surgery (such as anterior temporal lobectomy) may be considered.

Imaging Features:

  • Volume loss and signal abnormalities in the hippocampus on MRI
  • Increased T2 signal and decreased T1 signal in the affected hippocampus
  • Loss of internal architecture and hippocampal sclerosis

Differential Diagnosis

  • Alzheimer's disease
  • Frontotemporal dementia
  • Other causes of temporal lobe epilepsy
