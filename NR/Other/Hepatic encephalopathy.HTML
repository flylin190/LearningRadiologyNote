Hepatic Encephalopathy

Symptoms:

Common symptoms of hepatic encephalopathy include confusion, changes in personality or behavior, forgetfulness, difficulty concentrating, sleep disturbances, and in severe cases, coma.

Epidemiology:

Hepatic encephalopathy occurs as a complication of liver disease, particularly in patients with cirrhosis. It is more common in older adults and tends to worsen as liver disease progresses.

Pathology:

Hepatic encephalopathy is thought to be caused by the accumulation of toxic substances, such as ammonia, in the bloodstream due to liver dysfunction. These substances can affect brain function and lead to neurological symptoms.

Treatment:

Treatment for hepatic encephalopathy aims to reduce the levels of toxic substances in the bloodstream and improve liver function. This may include medications to lower ammonia levels, dietary changes, and management of underlying liver disease.

Imaging Features:

  • Diffuse cerebral edema
  • Loss of gray-white matter differentiation
  • Changes in signal intensity on MRI

Differential Diagnosis

  • Metabolic encephalopathy
  • Wernicke encephalopathy
  • Carbon monoxide poisoning
  • Brain infection
