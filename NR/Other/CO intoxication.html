CO Intoxication

Symptoms:

Common symptoms of CO intoxication include headache, dizziness, weakness, nausea, vomiting, chest pain, and confusion. Severe cases can lead to loss of consciousness, seizures, and even death.

Epidemiology:

CO intoxication can occur in any age group, but it is more common in children, elderly individuals, and those with chronic medical conditions. It is more prevalent in urban areas and during the winter months when heating appliances are used.

Treatment:

The main treatment for CO intoxication is to remove the individual from the source of CO and administer high-flow oxygen therapy. In severe cases, hyperbaric oxygen therapy may be necessary to rapidly eliminate CO from the body.

Imaging Features:

  • Bilateral symmetric globus pallidus hyperintensity on T1-weighted MRI
  • Hypointensity of the white matter on T2-weighted MRI

Differential Diagnosis

  • Methanol intoxication
  • Ethylene glycol intoxication
  • Acute hypoxic-ischemic encephalopathy
  • Metabolic encephalopathy
