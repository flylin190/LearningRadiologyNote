Amyotrophic Lateral Sclerosis (ALS)

Symptoms:

ALS is a progressive neurodegenerative disease that affects nerve cells in the brain and spinal cord. Symptoms include muscle weakness, twitching, and eventually muscle wasting, leading to paralysis.

Epidemiology:

ALS can affect anyone, but it is more common in people aged 40-70. Men are slightly more likely to develop ALS than women.

Pathology:

ALS is characterized by the degeneration of motor neurons in the brain and spinal cord. This leads to muscle weakness and eventually paralysis.

Treatment:

There is no cure for ALS, but treatment can help manage symptoms and improve quality of life. This may include medications, physical therapy, and respiratory support.

Imaging Features:

  • No specific imaging features are diagnostic of ALS, but MRI may show atrophy of the motor cortex and corticospinal tracts.

Differential Diagnosis

  • Other motor neuron diseases
  • Muscle disorders
  • Peripheral neuropathy
  • Spinal cord tumors
