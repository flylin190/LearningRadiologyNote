Wilson Disease (CNS Manifestations)

Symptoms:

CNS manifestations of Wilson disease can include movement disorders (e.g., tremors, dystonia), psychiatric disturbances, cognitive impairment, and seizures.

Epidemiology:

Wilson disease is a rare autosomal recessive disorder, with an estimated prevalence of 1 in 30,000 individuals worldwide.

Pathology:

Wilson disease is caused by mutations in the ATP7B gene, leading to the accumulation of copper in various tissues, including the brain.

Treatment:

Treatment for Wilson disease includes chelation therapy with agents like penicillamine or trientine to remove excess copper, and zinc salts to reduce copper absorption.

Imaging Features:

  • Basal ganglia hyperintensity on T1-weighted MRI
  • Midbrain and brainstem involvement
  • Atrophy and cystic changes in advanced cases

Differential Diagnosis

  • Other movement disorders (e.g., Parkinson's disease)
  • Hepatic encephalopathy
  • Huntington's disease
  • Metabolic disorders (e.g., Leigh's disease)
