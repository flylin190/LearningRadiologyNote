Fahr Disease

Symptoms:

Fahr disease is characterized by bilateral and symmetric calcifications in the basal ganglia and cerebral cortex. Patients may present with movement disorders, cognitive impairment, psychiatric symptoms, or seizures.

Epidemiology:

Fahr disease is a rare disorder with an unknown prevalence. It typically presents in mid-adulthood, but can occur at any age.

Pathology:

The exact cause of Fahr disease is unknown, but it is thought to be related to abnormalities in calcium metabolism. Genetic factors may also play a role.

Treatment:

There is no specific treatment for Fahr disease. Management is supportive and focused on addressing symptoms, such as medications for movement disorders or seizures.

Imaging Features:

  • Bilateral and symmetric calcifications in the basal ganglia and cerebral cortex on CT or MRI
  • Calcifications may also be seen in other brain regions, such as the thalamus or cerebellum

Differential Diagnosis:

  • Hypoparathyroidism
  • Idiopathic basal ganglia calcification (Fahr syndrome)
  • Metabolic disorders affecting calcium metabolism
