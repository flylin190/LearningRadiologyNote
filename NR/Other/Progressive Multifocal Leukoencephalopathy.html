Progressive Multifocal Leukoencephalopathy (PML)

Symptoms:

Common symptoms of PML include progressive weakness on one side of the body, clumsiness, vision problems, and changes in personality, mood, or behavior.

Epidemiology:

PML is a rare disease that usually occurs in people with weakened immune systems, such as those with HIV/AIDS, cancer, or taking immunosuppressive medications.

Pathology:

PML is caused by the JC virus, which is a common virus that is harmless in most people but can cause PML in those with weakened immune systems.

Treatment:

There is no specific treatment for PML, but antiretroviral therapy (ART) can help improve the immune system's response to the JC virus.

Imaging Features:

  • White matter lesions on MRI
  • Lesions are typically multifocal and asymmetrical

Differential Diagnosis:

  • Multiple sclerosis (MS)
  • Metastatic brain tumors
  • Acute disseminated encephalomyelitis (ADEM)
  • Other viral encephalopathies
