Hypoglycemic Encephalopathy

Symptoms:

Hypoglycemic encephalopathy presents with altered mental status, confusion, seizures, and in severe cases, coma. Neurological deficits may be transient or permanent depending on the duration and severity of hypoglycemia.

Epidemiology:

Hypoglycemic encephalopathy can occur in individuals with diabetes who use insulin or certain medications. It can also occur in individuals with severe malnutrition or liver disease.

Pathology:

Hypoglycemic encephalopathy is caused by low blood glucose levels, which deprive the brain of energy. Prolonged hypoglycemia can lead to neuronal cell death and brain damage.

Treatment:

The main treatment for hypoglycemic encephalopathy is to raise blood glucose levels quickly. This may involve administering glucose orally or intravenously.

Imaging Features:

  • Diffuse cerebral edema on CT or MRI
  • May show reversible or irreversible changes depending on the duration of hypoglycemia

Differential Diagnosis:

  • Metabolic encephalopathy
  • Hypoxic-ischemic encephalopathy
  • Drug-induced encephalopathy
