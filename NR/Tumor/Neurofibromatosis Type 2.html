Neurofibromatosis Type 2

Symptoms:

Neurofibromatosis type 2 (NF2) is characterized by bilateral vestibular schwannomas (acoustic neuromas), meningiomas, and other central nervous system tumors.

Epidemiology:

NF2 is a rare autosomal dominant disorder affecting approximately 1 in 25,000 individuals worldwide.

Pathology:

NF2 is caused by mutations in the NF2 gene, which encodes the protein merlin, a tumor suppressor that regulates cell growth and division.

Treatment:

Treatment for NF2 depends on the symptoms and may include surgery, radiation therapy, and medication to manage symptoms and slow tumor growth.

Imaging Features:

  • Bilateral vestibular schwannomas on MRI
  • Meningiomas on MRI

Differential Diagnosis:

  • Schwannomatosis
  • Neurofibromatosis type 1 (NF1)
  • von Hippel-Lindau disease
