Olfactory Neuroblastoma

Symptoms:

Common symptoms of olfactory neuroblastoma include nasal obstruction, epistaxis (nosebleeds), anosmia (loss of smell), headache, and facial pain or swelling.

Epidemiology:

Olfactory neuroblastoma is a rare type of cancer that primarily affects adults. It accounts for approximately 3% of all sinonasal malignancies.

Pathology:

Olfactory neuroblastoma arises from the olfactory epithelium in the upper nasal cavity. It is characterized by small round blue cells with neurosecretory granules.

Treatment:

The main treatment for olfactory neuroblastoma is surgical resection followed by radiation therapy. Chemotherapy may be used in some cases, particularly for advanced or recurrent disease.

Imaging Features:

  • Enhancement of the mass on contrast-enhanced MRI
  • Bone erosion or destruction on CT scan
  • Extension into adjacent structures, such as the cribriform plate or orbit

Differential Diagnosis:

  • Esthesioneuroblastoma
  • Squamous cell carcinoma of the sinonasal tract
  • Inverted papilloma
  • Nasopharyngeal carcinoma
