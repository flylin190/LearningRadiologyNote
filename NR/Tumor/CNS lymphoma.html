CNS Lymphoma

Symptoms:

Common symptoms of CNS lymphoma include headache, confusion, memory loss, seizures, and weakness or numbness in the arms or legs. Other symptoms may include changes in personality or behavior, difficulty with balance or walking, and vision problems.

Epidemiology:

CNS lymphoma is relatively rare and accounts for only a small percentage of all brain tumors. It can occur at any age, but it is more common in older adults, especially those with a weakened immune system.

Pathology:

CNS lymphoma is a type of non-Hodgkin lymphoma that originates in the lymphocytes of the central nervous system. It can arise from either the brain parenchyma or the leptomeninges.

Treatment:

The main treatment for CNS lymphoma usually involves a combination of chemotherapy, radiation therapy, and sometimes stem cell transplantation. In some cases, surgery may be performed to remove as much of the tumor as possible.

Imaging Features:

  • Single or multiple enhancing masses on MRI
  • Periventricular or perivascular distribution
  • Restricted diffusion on DWI
  • Leptomeningeal enhancement

Differential Diagnosis

  • Glioblastoma multiforme
  • Meningioma
  • Metastatic brain tumors
  • Toxoplasmosis
