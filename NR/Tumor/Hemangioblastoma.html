Hemangioblastoma

Symptoms:

Hemangioblastomas often cause symptoms due to their size and location in the brain. Common symptoms include headaches, dizziness, nausea, vomiting, and neurological deficits.

Epidemiology:

Hemangioblastomas are rare tumors that most commonly occur in adults between the ages of 40 and 60. They are more common in men than women.

Pathology:

Hemangioblastomas are vascular tumors that arise from the cells of the central nervous system. They are often associated with von Hippel-Lindau (VHL) disease, a genetic condition that predisposes individuals to the development of tumors.

Treatment:

Treatment for hemangioblastomas depends on the size and location of the tumor. Options may include surgery to remove the tumor, stereotactic radiosurgery, or embolization to shrink the tumor and reduce symptoms.

Imaging Features:

  • Well-defined, enhancing mass on MRI
  • Typically hypervascular with flow voids
  • May have a cystic component

Differential Diagnosis:

  • Meningioma
  • Glioblastoma
  • Metastatic brain tumor
