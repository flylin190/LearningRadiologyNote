Oligodendroglioma

Symptoms:

The symptoms of oligodendroglioma can vary depending on the location and size of the tumor, but may include seizures, headaches, cognitive impairment, and neurological deficits.

Epidemiology:

Oligodendrogliomas account for approximately 4-5% of all primary brain tumors and are more common in adults, typically between the ages of 30 and 50 years.

Pathology:

Oligodendrogliomas are primary brain tumors that arise from oligodendrocytes, the cells that produce myelin in the central nervous system. They are classified as grade II or III tumors based on their histological features and molecular characteristics.

Treatment:

Treatment for oligodendroglioma typically involves surgical resection followed by radiation therapy and/or chemotherapy, depending on the tumor grade and molecular characteristics.

Imaging Features:

  • Well-circumscribed, calcified mass lesion on CT or MRI
  • Mixed solid and cystic components
  • Heterogeneous enhancement on contrast-enhanced MRI

Differential Diagnosis:

  • Astrocytoma
  • Glioblastoma
  • Metastatic brain tumor
  • Lymphoma
