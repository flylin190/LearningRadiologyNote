Craniopharyngioma

Symptoms:

Common symptoms of craniopharyngioma include headache, visual disturbances, growth failure in children, and hormonal imbalances.

Epidemiology:

Craniopharyngioma is a rare type of brain tumor that mainly affects children and older adults.

Pathology:

Craniopharyngiomas are believed to develop from remnants of the craniopharyngeal duct. They are typically cystic and contain solid components.

Treatment:

Treatment for craniopharyngioma may include surgery, radiation therapy, and hormone replacement therapy.

Imaging Features:

  • Calcifications within the tumor on CT scan
  • Cystic components with solid mural nodules
  • Suprasellar location with hypothalamic involvement

Differential Diagnosis:

  • Rathke's cleft cyst
  • Pituitary adenoma
  • Craniopharyngioma
