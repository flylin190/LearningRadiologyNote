Calcifying Pseudoneoplasm of the Neuraxis(CAPNON)

Symptoms:

CAPNON are often asymptomatic and discovered incidentally. However, some patients may experience symptoms related to mass effect, such as headaches, seizures, or neurological deficits.

Epidemiology:

CAPNON are rare lesions, primarily affecting children and young adults. They account for approximately 0.7% of intracranial masses.

Pathology:

CAPNONs are usually solitary extra-axial, or less frequently intra-axial, masses. They are benign, slow-growing lesions composed of fibrous tissue, calcifications, and a variable degree of chronic inflammation. They typically arise from the subependymal region or the choroid plexus.

Treatment:

Treatment for calcifying pseudoneoplasms depends on the size, location, and symptoms caused by the lesion. Small, asymptomatic lesions may be observed, while larger or symptomatic lesions may require surgical resection.

Imaging Features:

  • Well-circumscribed, calcified mass
  • Typically located in the supratentorial region
  • Minimal or no enhancement on contrast-enhanced MRI
  • Possible associated edema or mass effect

Differential Diagnosis:

  • Craniopharyngioma
  • Low-grade glioma
  • Meningioma
  • Oligodendroglioma
