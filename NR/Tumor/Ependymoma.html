Ependymoma

Symptoms:

Symptoms of ependymoma depend on the location and size of the tumor, but may include headaches, nausea, vomiting, seizures, and neurological deficits such as weakness, numbness, or vision problems.

Epidemiology:

Ependymomas account for approximately 3-9% of all primary brain tumors. They can occur at any age, but are more common in children and young adults.

Pathology:

Ependymomas are glial tumors that arise from the ependymal cells lining the ventricular system and central canal of the spinal cord. They can be classified into various subtypes based on their histological features and molecular characteristics.

Treatment:

Treatment for ependymoma typically involves surgical resection followed by radiation therapy. Chemotherapy may be used in certain cases, particularly for high-grade or recurrent tumors.

Imaging Features:

  • Well-defined, heterogeneous mass
  • Commonly located in the fourth ventricle or spinal canal
  • Calcifications or cystic components may be present
  • Variable enhancement pattern on contrast-enhanced imaging

Differential Diagnosis:

  • Medulloblastoma
  • Pilocytic astrocytoma
  • Choroid plexus papilloma
  • Metastatic lesions
