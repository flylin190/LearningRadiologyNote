Clival Chordoma

Symptoms:

Common symptoms of clival chordoma include headache, vision changes, double vision, facial numbness or weakness, difficulty swallowing, and hoarseness.

Epidemiology:

Clival chordoma is a rare type of bone cancer that primarily affects adults, with a peak incidence between 50 and 60 years of age. It is slightly more common in men than women.

Pathology:

Chordomas arise from remnants of the notochord, a structure present during embryonic development. They typically occur at the skull base, including the clivus, and are slow-growing but locally aggressive tumors.

Treatment:

The main treatment for clival chordoma is surgical resection, often followed by radiation therapy. Due to the tumor's location near critical structures, complete resection can be challenging.

Imaging Features:

  • Clival erosion and destruction on CT scan
  • Soft tissue mass with lobulated appearance on MRI
  • Enhancement with contrast administration

Differential Diagnosis

  • Chondrosarcoma
  • Chondroid chordoma
  • Chondromyxoid fibroma
  • Chondroblastoma
