Choroid Plexus Papilloma

Symptoms:

Choroid plexus papilloma may present with signs and symptoms of increased intracranial pressure, such as headache, nausea, vomiting, and vision changes.

Epidemiology:

Choroid plexus papilloma is a rare brain tumor that typically occurs in children and is more common in males than females.

Pathology:

Choroid plexus papilloma is a benign tumor that arises from the choroid plexus, which is responsible for producing cerebrospinal fluid.

Treatment:

Treatment for choroid plexus papilloma usually involves surgical resection of the tumor. In some cases, chemotherapy or radiation therapy may be used.

Imaging Features:

  • Well-defined intraventricular mass on MRI
  • Enhancement of the mass with contrast

Differential Diagnosis:

  • Choroid plexus carcinoma
  • Colloid cyst
  • Ependymoma
