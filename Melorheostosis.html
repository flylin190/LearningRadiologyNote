Melorheostosis

Symptoms:

Melorheostosis is a rare bone disorder characterized by the thickening and overgrowth of the cortical bone. It can lead to pain, stiffness, and limited movement in the affected area.

Epidemiology:

Melorheostosis is a rare disease that can affect people of all ages. It is usually diagnosed in childhood or early adulthood.

Pathology:

The exact cause of melorheostosis is unknown. It is believed to result from abnormal bone formation during development.

Treatment:

Treatment for melorheostosis aims to relieve symptoms and improve function. This may include physical therapy, pain management, and in severe cases, surgery to correct deformities or relieve pressure on nerves.

Imaging Features:

  • Cortical hyperostosis on X-ray
  • Candle wax dripping appearance on X-ray

Differential Diagnosis:

  • Osteopoikilosis
  • Florid reactive periostitis
  • Chronic osteomyelitis
