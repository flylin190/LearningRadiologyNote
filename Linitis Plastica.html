Linitis Plastica

Symptoms:

Linitis plastica, also known as "leather bottle stomach," presents with early satiety, weight loss, and abdominal pain. It is often associated with gastric adenocarcinoma.

Epidemiology:

Linitis plastica is rare and most commonly affects older adults. It is more common in men than women.

Pathology:

Linitis plastica is characterized by thickening and rigidity of the stomach wall due to diffuse infiltration of cancer cells, leading to a shrunken, leather-like appearance of the stomach.

Treatment:

Treatment for linitis plastica usually involves surgery to remove the affected part of the stomach, followed by chemotherapy or radiation therapy.

Imaging Features:

  • "Leather bottle" or "coke bottle" appearance of the stomach on barium swallow or CT scan
  • Diffuse thickening of the stomach wall

Differential Diagnosis:

  • Benign gastric ulcers
  • Gastric lymphoma
  • Gastric sarcoma
