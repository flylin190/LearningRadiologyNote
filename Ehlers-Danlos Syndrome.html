Ehlers-Danlos Syndrome

Symptoms:

Ehlers-Danlos Syndrome is a group of connective tissue disorders that can affect the skin, joints, and blood vessels. Symptoms may include hyperelastic skin, joint hypermobility, and easy bruising.

Epidemiology:

Ehlers-Danlos Syndrome is a rare disorder, with an estimated prevalence of 1 in 5,000 to 1 in 20,000 individuals.

Pathology:

Ehlers-Danlos Syndrome is caused by defects in the synthesis or structure of collagen, which is an important component of connective tissue.

Treatment:

Treatment for Ehlers-Danlos Syndrome focuses on managing symptoms and preventing complications. This may include physical therapy, bracing, and in some cases, surgery to repair damaged joints or blood vessels.

Imaging Features:

  • Joint hypermobility on clinical examination
  • Thin, translucent skin
  • Easy bruising

Differential Diagnosis

  • Marfan syndrome
  • Loeys-Dietz syndrome
  • Stickler syndrome
